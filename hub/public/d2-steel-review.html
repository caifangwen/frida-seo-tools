At a Glance

D2 is a high-carbon, high-chromium tool steel (AISI D2 / DIN 1.2379) with ~1.5% carbon and ~12% chromium — just below the 13% threshold typically required for true stainless classification. It is often described as "semi-stainless," offering meaningfully better corrosion resistance than plain carbon steels while delivering tool-steel-grade edge retention driven by large chromium carbides. Developed originally for industrial dies and punches, its abrasion resistance translates directly to blade durability. D2 is the dominant mid-tier tool steel in the global EDC and working-knife market, widely positioned as a cost-effective alternative to powder metallurgy steels like S30V at a significantly lower price point.
