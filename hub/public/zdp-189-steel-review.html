At a Glance

ZDP-189 is an ultra-high-carbon, high-chromium powder metallurgy steel from Hitachi Metals (Japan), engineered to achieve the absolute maximum hardness possible in a commercially produced knife steel. With ~3.0% carbon and ~20% chromium, it can be hardened to HRC 67–68 — the highest of any mainstream knife steel — pushing edge retention to its theoretical limit. The trade-off is extreme brittleness relative to any other steel on this page: ZDP-189 is designed for precision cutting of soft materials, not impact or lateral stress. It is comparable to M390 in chromium level but radically different in carbon content and resulting properties. Primarily specified for premium collector knives and ultra-premium Japanese culinary knives where maximum sharpness is the singular criterion.
