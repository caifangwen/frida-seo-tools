At a Glance

CPM Cru-Wear is a powder metallurgy tool steel produced by Crucible Industries (USA) via the CPM (Crucible Particle Metallurgy) process. It is based on a high-speed steel alloy chemistry (tungsten, vanadium, molybdenum, chromium) that delivers near-PM-steel edge retention levels while retaining better toughness than comparable alloy-rich conventional tool steels. Its CPM processing eliminates the large carbide segregation typical of conventionally produced high-alloy steels, resulting in a more uniform microstructure. Comparable in edge retention to S30V, but with higher toughness — positioned as the tool-steel alternative for users who want PM-level cutting performance without sacrificing shock resistance. Primarily specified for high-end custom and production working knives.
