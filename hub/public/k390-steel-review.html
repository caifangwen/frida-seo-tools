At a Glance

K390 is a high-vanadium powder metallurgy tool steel from Böhler (Austria), engineered for extreme abrasion resistance applications. Its very high vanadium content (~9%) creates an exceptionally dense distribution of hard vanadium carbides, delivering edge retention that rivals or exceeds most PM stainless steels. Unlike PM steels such as M390 which prioritize the stainless balance, K390 is an unapologetically wear-resistant tool steel — corrosion resistance is minimal. Comparable in edge retention to CPM-10V, it is positioned as the performance ceiling of the Böhler tool steel line. Primarily specified for high-end production knives targeting users who prioritize maximum cutting longevity over corrosion or ease of sharpening.
