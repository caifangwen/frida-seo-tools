At a Glance

W2 is a water-hardening tool steel in the AISI W-series, characterized by its high carbon content (~1.0%) and trace vanadium for grain control. Its defining feature is an extremely fast quench requirement (water or brine), which produces a pronounced hardness differential between the hardened edge and the softer spine — the basis of the visual hamon line prized in Japanese-style blades. It is functionally comparable to Shirogami (White Paper) steels in use case and aesthetic potential, though its composition and quench behavior differ. Primarily used in artisan and collector knife contexts where visual beauty and carbon steel performance are equally valued.
