### 3Cr13

**Tags:** `Budget` | `Martensitic Stainless` | `Kitchen Cutlery` | `Mass Production` | `Wholesale-Friendly`

**Overview:**
3Cr13 is a basic martensitic stainless steel in the Chinese GB standard system, equivalent to AISI 420 and closely comparable to 420J2 in the Western market. Its low carbon content (~0.3%) is intentional — not a flaw — delivering maximum toughness and corrosion resistance at the lowest possible production cost. As the most widely produced entry-level stainless steel in China's knife industry, it serves as the de facto standard for promotional cutlery, gift knife sets, and high-volume retail programs in Western mass-market channels.

### 5Cr15MoV

**Tags:** `Budget` | `Martensitic Stainless` | `Kitchen Cutlery` | `High-Volume OEM` | `Wholesale-Friendly`

As one of the top-volume OEM steels globally, it serves as the industry standard for entry-to-mid level kitchen cutlery across Western retail and wholesale channels.

**Tags:** `Mid-Range` | `Martensitic Stainless` | `Chef Knives` | `Private Label` | `Heat-Treatment Friendly`

It is comparable to 440A in corrosion resistance but outperforms it in edge stability and HT response. Widely adopted by Scandinavian and North American mid-range knife brands for kitchen and outdoor applications.

### W2

It is functionally comparable to Shirogami (White Paper) steels in use case and aesthetic potential, though its composition and quench behavior differ. Primarily used in artisan and collector knife contexts where visual beauty and carbon steel performance are equally valued.

### D2

**Tags:** `Mid-Range` | `Tool Steel` | `EDC Knives` | `Private Label` | `Heat-Treatment Friendly`

**Overview:**
D2 is a high-carbon, high-chromium tool steel (AISI D2 / DIN 1.2379) with ~1.5% carbon and ~12% chromium — just below the 13% threshold typically required for true stainless classification. Developed originally for industrial dies and punches, its abrasion resistance translates directly to blade durability. D2 is the dominant mid-tier tool steel in the global EDC and working-knife market, widely positioned as a cost-effective alternative to powder metallurgy steels like S30V at a significantly lower price point.

### S35VN

**Tags:** `High-End` | `Powder Metallurgy Steel` | `EDC Knives` | `Private Label`

### M390 / 20CV

**Tags:** `High-End` | `Powder Metallurgy Steel` | `EDC Knives` | `Private Label`

M390 (Böhler, Austria) and 20CV (Crucible, USA) are chemically near-identical powder metallurgy stainless steels — both high in carbon (~1.9%), chromium (~20%), vanadium, and molybdenum — produced by different manufacturers to effectively the same specification. ### MagnaCut

**Tags:** `High-End` | `Powder Metallurgy Steel` | `Outdoor Knives` | `Chef Knives` | `Private Label`

**Overview:**
MagnaCut is a purpose-designed knife-specific powder metallurgy stainless steel, developed by metallurgist Dr. Larrin Thomas and produced by Carpenter Technology (USA). Its unique alloy design deliberately avoids chromium carbide formation — all chromium remains in solution, maximizing corrosion resistance — while vanadium and niobium carbides provide edge retention. The result is an unprecedented combination of high toughness, high corrosion resistance, and strong edge retention in a single steel, breaking the traditional trade-offs between these properties. Since its 2021 launch, it has been rapidly adopted by both top custom bladesmiths and high-end production brands. There is no direct Western equivalent — it occupies a unique position above S45VN in all three primary performance dimensions.

### ZDP-189

**Tags:** `High-End` | `Powder Metallurgy Steel` | `Chef Knives` | `EDC Knives`

**Overview:**
ZDP-189 is an ultra-high-carbon, high-chromium powder metallurgy steel from Hitachi Metals (Japan), engineered to achieve the absolute maximum hardness possible in a commercially produced knife steel. The trade-off is extreme brittleness relative to any other steel on this page: ZDP-189 is designed for precision cutting of soft materials, not impact or lateral stress. It is comparable to M390 in chromium level but radically different in carbon content and resulting properties. Primarily specified for premium collector knives and ultra-premium Japanese culinary knives where maximum sharpness is the singular criterion.
