At a Glance

M390 (Böhler, Austria) and 20CV (Crucible, USA) are chemically near-identical powder metallurgy stainless steels — both high in carbon (~1.9%), chromium (~20%), vanadium, and molybdenum — produced by different manufacturers to effectively the same specification. Their extremely fine, uniform carbide distribution (a result of PM processing) enables both the highest edge retention and excellent corrosion resistance simultaneously, a combination impossible in conventional high-alloy steels. For several years, M390/20CV has been the benchmark flagship stainless PM steel in the premium EDC collector market. Comparable to CPM-S90V in wear resistance category, but with better toughness; positioned as the premium alternative to S35VN when maximum edge retention is the priority.
