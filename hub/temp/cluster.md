## 02 Understanding Knife Steel: The Basics

A "good" steel is relative to its intended use. For kitchen knives, we balance five main properties: Hardness, Toughness, Wear Resistance, Corrosion Resistance, and Ease of Sharpening.

### The Five Key Properties

| Property | What It Means | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Hardness (HRC) | Resistance to deformation | Edge retention |
| Toughness | Resistance to chipping | Durability under impact |
| Wear Resistance | Resistance to abrasion | Long-term performance |
| Corrosion Resistance | Resistance to rust/stains | Low maintenance |
| Sharpening Ease | How easy to restore edge | User convenience |

### Carbon Steel vs Stainless Steel

Carbon steel contains more carbon and lacks chromium, making it incredibly sharp but prone to rust. Stainless steel adds at least 12-14% chromium to resist corrosion.

## 06 Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the best steel for kitchen knives?**
A: It depends on your priorities. For the best all-around performance, VG-10 offers excellent edge retention and corrosion resistance.

**Q: Does higher Rockwell hardness mean a better knife?**
A: Not necessarily. While higher HRC means better edge retention, it often results in a more brittle blade.
