01
Getting Started

Step 1: Define Your Requirements

Before contacting suppliers, clarify these five critical parameters. This prevents costly mistakes and ensures accurate quotations.

1
Parameter 1 Load Capacity

What weight will the bag carry? 10kg, 25kg, 50kg? This determines GSM and construction. Add 20% safety margin.

2
Parameter 2 Storage Conditions

Indoor warehouse or outdoor exposure? Outdoor requires UV-stabilized fabric. Humid environments need lamination.

3
Parameter 3 Filling Method

Manual filling (open mouth) or automatic (valve bags)? Valve bags cost 15-20% more but enable automated packing lines.

4
Parameter 4 Printing Requirements

Simple logo (flexo printing) or full-color graphics (BOPP lamination)? More colors = higher cost and MOQ.

5
Parameter 5 Certification Needs

Food contact requires FDA/EU compliance. Export to specific markets may need additional certifications.

Pro Tip: Create a specification sheet before requesting quotes. Include: dimensions (L×W), GSM, bag type (open mouth/valve/gusseted), printing colors, quantity, and delivery deadline. This reduces back-and-forth and ensures comparable quotes.

02
GSM Selection

Step 2: Choose the Right GSM

GSM (grams per square meter) is the single most important specification. It determines strength, durability, and cost. Here's how to match GSM to your application:

Product Type Recommended GSM Bag Type Notes
Light Grains (5-10kg) 65-75 GSM Open mouth, gusseted Retail packaging, cost-sensitive
Rice/Flour (10-25kg) 80-90 GSM Valve or open mouth Standard food-grade, laminated
Animal Feed (20-40kg) 85-95 GSM Open mouth, PE liner Moisture barrier critical
Cement (25-50kg) 90-100 GSM Valve bags High tensile strength required
Sand/Gravel (20-40kg) 95-110 GSM Open mouth, UV-treated Abrasion resistance needed
Chemicals (25-50kg) 90-120 GSM Laminated, custom liner Chemical compatibility testing
Export Packaging 90-100 GSM Gusseted, laminated Stack stability for shipping

Cost Optimization: Don't over-specify GSM. Every 10 GSM increase adds 8-12% to bag cost. For 25kg rice, 85 GSM works as well as 95 GSM if bag construction is quality. Request load testing data from supplier.

03
Lamination Decision

Step 3: Laminated vs Non-Laminated

Lamination adds a film layer (BOPP or PE) to the woven fabric. This affects moisture resistance, print quality, and cost. The choice depends on your product's sensitivity to humidity.

Laminated Bags

  • Complete moisture barrier (0% absorption)
  • Premium print surface (BOPP)
  • Dust-proof containment
  • Easy-clean surface
  • 15-25% higher cost
  • Best for: food, chemicals, export

Non-Laminated Bags

  • Breathable (air circulation)
  • Lower cost
  • Suitable for dry products
  • Standard flexo printing
  • Not moisture-proof
  • Best for: vegetables, non-hygroscopic materials

04
Supplier Evaluation

Step 4: Evaluate Suppliers

Price is important, but reliability matters more. A supplier who delivers late or inconsistent quality costs more in the long run. Use this evaluation framework:

Supplier Scorecard (100 points total)
  • Quality Certification (20 pts): ISO 9001, BRC, FDA registration
  • Production Capacity (15 pts): Monthly output, equipment age, automation level
  • Sample Quality (20 pts): Tensile test results, print accuracy, seam strength
  • Lead Time Reliability (15 pts): On-time delivery rate, rush order capability
  • Communication (10 pts): Response time, English proficiency, problem-solving
  • Price Competitiveness (10 pts): Not lowest, but fair market value
  • After-Sales Support (10 pts): Warranty terms, complaint resolution, replacement policy

Red Flags to Avoid

  • No physical address or factory photos
  • Unwilling to provide samples
  • No quality certifications for food-grade claims
  • Quotes significantly below market average (20%+ lower)
  • Vague answers about lead time or capacity
  • No references from existing customers
  • Refuses third-party inspection

Green Flags to Look For

  • In-house testing lab with tensile strength equipment
  • Transparent about production process and limitations
  • Provides detailed spec sheets and test reports
  • Offers free samples with your specifications
  • Clear contract terms including quality standards
  • Responsive communication (24-hour response time)
  • Willing to sign NDA for custom designs
05
Sample Testing

Step 5: Test Samples Before Bulk Order

Never place bulk orders without physical sample approval. Here's what to test:

01

Dimensional Check

Measure length, width, gusset. Tolerance should be ±5mm. Verify against your spec sheet.

02

GSM Verification

Cut 10×10cm sample, weigh on precision scale. Weight (g) × 10 = GSM. Should be ±5% of spec.

03

Load Test

Fill with product (or equivalent weight), drop from 1m height. Check for seam failure or tearing.

04

Print Quality

Check color accuracy, registration, smudging. Rub with damp cloth — ink should not transfer.

05

Seam Strength

Pull bottom and side seams. Stitching should be tight with no loose threads or gaps.

06

Moisture Test

For laminated bags: fill with water, leave 24h. Check for leaks or seepage through fabric.

Sample Policy: Reputable suppliers provide free samples (5-10 pcs) for qualified buyers. You pay shipping only. Custom printed samples cost $50-150 (refundable with bulk order). Lead time: 5-7 days for plain samples, 10-12 days for printed.

    Quality Control

    Step 8: Quality Control & Inspection

    Define acceptance criteria in your purchase order. Standard quality benchmarks:

    Parameter Acceptance Standard Test Method Tolerance
    Dimensions Within spec Tape measure ±5mm
    GSM As specified Weight measurement ±5%
    Tensile Strength Min. requirement Tensile tester Warp: ≥35 MPa, Weft: ≥30 MPa
    Print Registration Aligned colors Visual inspection ≤2mm offset
    Seam Strength No failure Load test ≥80% of fabric strength
    Color Consistency Match approved sample Delta E meter ≤5 Delta E

    Third-Party Inspection: For orders over $10,000, consider hiring SGS, BV, or Intertek for pre-shipment inspection. Cost: $300-500. They verify quantity, quality, packaging, and provide detailed report. Worth the investment for critical orders.
