01
Introduction

What Makes Woven Bags "Food Grade"?

Food grade woven bags meet regulatory standards for direct contact with edible products. Every component — from PP resin to printing ink — must be certified safe for food contact.

Key Requirement: Food grade doesn't just mean "safe material." It means the entire manufacturing process — extrusion, weaving, printing, storage — occurs in controlled conditions preventing contamination.

Food Products Requiring Food-Grade Bags

  • Grains: Rice, wheat, flour, corn, barley, oats
  • Sugar & Sweeteners: White sugar, brown sugar, powdered sugar
  • Animal Feed: Poultry feed, cattle feed, fish feed, pet food
  • Spices: Pepper, cumin, coriander, chili powder
  • Dehydrated Foods: Dried fruits, vegetables, herbs
  • Food Additives: Salt, baking powder, yeast
02
Certifications

Required Certifications by Market

United States (FDA)

FDA regulates food-contact materials under Title 21 CFR 177.1520 (Polyolefins).

Requirement Details Documentation
Base Resin PP must meet 21 CFR 177.1520 Letter of Guarantee from resin supplier
Additives UV stabilizers, colorants must be FDA-listed FDA 21 CFR compliance certificate
Ink Low-migration, heavy metal-free INK-001 compliance, test reports
Lamination Film Must meet 21 CFR 177.1520 Supplier's FDA letter
Facility GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice) Facility audit report

European Union (EU 10/2011)

EU Regulation 10/2011 governs plastic materials intended for food contact.

  • Declaration of Compliance (DoC): Mandatory for all food-contact materials
  • Migration Testing: Overall migration ≤10 mg/dm², specific migration limits for listed substances
  • Heavy Metals: Lead, cadmium, mercury, chromium VI must be below detection limits
  • Primary Aromatic Amines (PAA): Must not be detectable (0.01 mg/kg limit)

United Kingdom (BRC Packaging)

BRC Global Standard for Packaging Materials is required by major UK retailers.

BRC Certification Levels
  • Grade AA: Highest level, unannounced audit (top retailers require this)
  • Grade A: Announced audit, acceptable for most customers
  • Grade B: Minimum acceptable, may limit customer base
  • Grade C: Below standard, corrective action required

03
Documentation

Documentation Checklist for Buyers

Before placing food-grade orders, request and verify these documents:

Essential Documents
  • FDA Letter of Guarantee: From both resin supplier and bag manufacturer
  • EU Declaration of Compliance: Signed DoC per Regulation 10/2011
  • Migration Test Report: From accredited lab (SGS, Intertek, TUV)
  • Heavy Metal Analysis: XRF or ICP-MS test results
  • BRC Certificate: If supplying UK market (check grade and expiry)
  • GMP Audit Report: Recent facility inspection
  • Certificate of Analysis (CoA): Per production batch
  • Traceability Records: Batch numbers linked to raw material lots

Warning: Generic "FDA Approved" claims without documentation are meaningless. Always request actual certificates with your company name, product specification, and issue date. Verify certificate authenticity with issuing body.

04
Manufacturing

Food-Grade Manufacturing Requirements

True food-grade production requires controlled environment and strict protocols:

Facility Requirements

  • Dedicated Production Line: Food-grade bags made on separate equipment from industrial bags
  • Clean Room Area: Printing and sewing in dust-controlled environment
  • Pest Control: Regular fumigation, bait stations, entry traps
  • Worker Hygiene: Hand washing stations, hair nets, clean uniforms
  • Raw Material Storage: Covered, off-floor, segregated from non-food materials
  • Finished Goods Warehouse: Pallet storage, climate control, pest monitoring

Quality Control Protocols

01

Incoming Material Check

Verify FDA certificates for each resin batch. Quarantine until CoA reviewed.

02

In-Process Inspection

Hourly checks on GSM, dimensions, print quality. Document all parameters.

03

Migration Testing

Quarterly third-party testing for overall and specific migration limits.

04

Final Inspection

AQL 2.5 sampling. Visual check for contamination, defects, print accuracy.

05

Batch Traceability

Each pallet tagged with batch number, production date, resin lot number.

05
Product-Specific

Special Considerations by Food Type

High-Fat Foods (Nuts, Seeds, Pet Food)

Fats can accelerate chemical migration. Extra precautions needed:

  • Use virgin PP resin only (no recycled content)
  • Specify low-migration ink systems
  • Consider barrier coating or liner
  • Shorter shelf-life recommendations (6-12 months)

Moisture-Sensitive Products (Flour, Sugar, Spices)

Moisture causes clumping and spoilage:

  • Mandatory lamination (BOPP or PE)
  • Add PE liner for extra barrier
  • Heat-seal closure for complete seal
  • Include desiccant packet for export shipments

Strong Odor Products (Spices, Coffee)

Odor transmission and flavor scalping concerns:

  • Use odorless PP resin
  • Avoid solvent-based inks (use water-based or UV-cure)
  • Test for flavor scalping (absorption of aromas by packaging)
  • Consider metallized barrier film for premium products

Audit

How to Audit Your Supplier

Before committing to large orders, conduct supplier audit (in-person or third-party):

Audit Area Key Questions Red Flags
Raw Material Storage Are food-grade resins segregated? Covered? Off-floor? Mixed with industrial materials, direct floor contact
Production Floor Clean? Organized? Dedicated food-grade lines? Dust accumulation, pest activity, dirty equipment
Quality Lab Tensile tester? GSM cutter? In-house testing capability? No testing equipment, all outsourced
Documentation Can they produce certificates on demand? "We'll send later", expired certificates, photocopies only
Warehouse Pallet storage? Climate control? Pest control logs? Direct floor storage, visible pests, no monitoring
