Overview

The Complete Production Flow

PP woven bag manufacturing involves six distinct stages, each critical to final quality. Understanding this process helps buyers evaluate suppliers and specify requirements accurately.

1
Stage 1 Raw Material Preparation

PP resin + additives (CaCO₃, UV stabilizers, color masterbatch) blended in precise ratios.

2
Stage 2 Extrusion & Tape Making

Melted PP extruded through spinnerets, stretched 4-5x to align polymer chains for strength.

3
Stage 3 Weaving

Tapes woven on circular looms into tubular fabric. Width: 40-110cm based on bag size.

4
Stage 4 Lamination & Printing

Optional BOPP/PE coating. Flexo or rotogravure printing for branding and labeling.

5
Stage 5 Cutting & Sewing

Fabric cut to length, bottom sewn (single/double fold), top hemmed or valve attached.

6
Stage 6 QC & Packaging

Dimensional check, load testing, visual inspection. Palletized for shipment.

02
Stage 1

Raw Material Preparation

Quality begins with raw materials. Premium manufacturers use virgin PP resin with controlled additive blends.

Raw Material Composition

Component Percentage Function Quality Check
Virgin PP Resin 70-85% Base polymer for strength Melt Flow Index (MFI): 2-3 g/10min
Recycled PP (optional) 0-20% Cost reduction ≤15% for standard grade, 0% for food-grade
Calcium Carbonate (CaCO₃) 5-15% Filler for stiffness, opacity Particle size: 8-12 microns
Color Masterbatch 2-5% Natural white or custom colors Pigment dispersion, color matching
UV Stabilizers 0.5-2% Outdoor protection HALS (Hindered Amine Light Stabilizers)
Additives 0.5-1% Anti-slip, anti-block agents As per application requirement

Food-Grade Note: For food-contact bags, only virgin PP resin is permitted. No recycled content. All additives must be FDA 21 CFR 177.1520 compliant. Separate production lines prevent cross-contamination.

03
Stage 2

Extrusion & Tape Making

The extrusion process transforms raw PP into flat tapes — the building blocks of woven fabric.

01

Blending & Feeding

Raw materials blended in high-speed mixer, then fed into extruder hopper. Precise ratio control critical for consistency.

02

Melting & Extrusion

PP melted at 260-280°C in extruder barrel. Screw conveyor pushes molten polymer through flat die with multiple spinnerets.

03

Film Formation

Molten PP emerges as thin film (0.05-0.08mm). Immediately cooled on chrome-plated rollers with water circulation.

04

Slitting

Cooled film slit into narrow tapes (2-3mm width) using razor blade cutters. Tape width determines final fabric GSM.

05

Stretching (Orientation)

Tapes stretched 4-5x original length between heated rollers. This aligns polymer chains, increasing tensile strength 6-8x.

06

Winding

Stretched tapes wound onto bobbins. Each bobbin feeds one shuttle in circular loom.

Quality Control: Tape Stage

  • Tape Width: 2.5mm ±0.2mm tolerance
  • Density: 0.90-0.92 g/cm³
  • Tensile Strength: ≥400 MPa (individual tape)
  • Elongation at Break: 25-35%

Weave Pattern

Standard plain weave (over-under pattern) provides balanced strength in both directions:

  • Warp direction: Tapes running lengthwise (machine direction)
  • Weft direction: Tapes running circularly (cross direction)
  • Balance: Warp:Weft strength ratio should be 1.2:1 to 1.5:1

Quality Indicator: Uniform tape spacing with no gaps or bunching. Fabric should feel smooth, not rough. Hold against light — consistent opacity indicates even weaving.

05
Stage 4

Lamination & Printing

For laminated bags, fabric passes through coating and printing lines.

BOPP Lamination Process

1
Film Printing

BOPP film printed with rotogravure presses (up to 10 colors). Reverse printing — ink applied to underside of film.

2
Adhesive Application

Printed film coated with solvent-based or water-based adhesive.

3
Lamination

Film pressed onto woven fabric using heated rollers (120-140°C, 3-5 bar pressure).

4
Curing

Laminated material aged 24-48 hours for adhesive bonding completion.

Flexographic Printing (for non-laminated or PE laminated)

  • Printing stations: 1-6 colors (typically 2-4 for standard bags)
  • Ink type: Water-based or solvent-based
  • Drying: Hot air drying between colors
  • Speed: 60-100 meters/minute
06
Stage 5

Cutting & Sewing

Final conversion from fabric rolls to finished bags.

Automatic Bag Making Machine

01

Unwinding

Fabric roll mounted on unwinder with tension control for consistent feed.

02

Length Measurement

Sensor measures exact fabric length per bag (based on size specification).

03

Cutting

Hot knife or ultrasonic cutter severs fabric. Heat seals cut edges to prevent fraying.

04

Bottom Folding

Bottom edge folded 2-3cm, then sewn with lockstitch (single needle) or chainstitch (double needle).

05

Top Hemming

Top edge hemmed 1-2cm for reinforced mouth. For valve bags, valve inserted at this stage.

06

Stacking & Counting

Finished bags stacked, counted, and bundled (typically 100 pcs/bundle).

Quality Control

3-Stage Quality Control System

Premium manufacturers implement QC at every stage, not just final inspection.

Stage 1: Incoming Material Inspection

  • PP resin MFI testing
  • Additive verification (certificate cross-check)
  • Ink viscosity and color matching
  • BOPP film thickness and print quality

Stage 2: In-Process Inspection

  • Every 2 hours: Tape width, GSM check
  • Every 4 hours: Fabric tensile strength test
  • Every batch: Print registration, color accuracy
  • Continuous: Visual inspection for weaving defects

Stage 3: Final Inspection (AQL 2.5)

Parameter Standard Tolerance Test Method
Dimensions Per specification ±5mm Tape measure
GSM Per specification ±5% Weight measurement
Tensile Strength Warp ≥35 MPa ≥90% of standard Tensile tester
Seam Strength No failure ≥80% of fabric Load test
Print Quality Clear, aligned ≤2mm offset Visual inspection
Color Consistency Match approved sample ≤5 Delta E Spectrophotometer

AQL 2.5 Standard: Acceptable Quality Level 2.5 means maximum 2.5% defective items in sample. For 10,000 pcs order: sample size 200 pcs, accept if ≤10 defective, reject if ≥11 defective.
