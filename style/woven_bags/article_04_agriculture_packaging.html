Overview

Why Woven Bags Dominate Agriculture

PP woven bags are the packaging standard for agricultural products worldwide. They combine strength, breathability, moisture resistance, and cost-effectiveness — critical factors for farm-to-market supply chains.

Key Advantages for Agriculture
  • Breathability: Micro-gaps allow air circulation, preventing mold in grains
  • Moisture Resistance: PP fiber doesn't absorb water, protecting contents
  • High Strength: 35-50 MPa tensile strength handles rough handling
  • UV Options: Stabilized fabric for outdoor farm storage (18-24 months)
  • Cost Effective: 60-80% cheaper than jute, 40-50% cheaper than paper
  • Customizable: Printing for branding, grading, traceability

Packaging Solutions by Product

Rice & Grain Packaging

Rice, wheat, corn, and other grains require breathable packaging that prevents moisture buildup while allowing ventilation.

Product Recommended GSM Bag Type Special Features
Rice (5-25kg) 80-90 GSM Gusseted, open mouth Laminated for export, PE liner optional
Wheat (25-50kg) 85-95 GSM Open mouth or valve Food-grade, breathable
Corn (25-50kg) 85-95 GSM Open mouth High breathability
Barley/Oats (20-40kg) 80-90 GSM Open mouth Standard food-grade

Export Tip: For ocean freight, specify laminated bags with PE liner. Humidity in shipping containers can reach 90%+, causing condensation ("container rain"). Lamination prevents moisture ingress.

Fertilizer Packaging

Fertilizers (NPK, urea, ammonium) are hygroscopic and corrosive. Bags must resist moisture and chemical attack.

  • GSM: 90-100 GSM for 25-50kg bags
  • Lamination: BOPP or PE coating mandatory
  • UV Treatment: Required for outdoor storage at farms
  • Valve Type: Preferred for automated filling lines
  • Anti-slip: Textured surface for stable pallet stacking

Vegetable & Fresh Produce

Potatoes, onions, carrots, and other root vegetables need ventilated packaging for air circulation.

  • Perforated bags: Micro-holes (2-5mm) for ventilation
  • GSM: 70-85 GSM for 5-20kg loads
  • Non-laminated: Breathability is critical
  • Mesh options: Extra ventilation for high-moisture produce

Food Safety

Food Grade Requirements & Certifications

Food-contact woven bags must comply with strict regulations. Here's what to verify:

Required Certifications for Food Packaging: FDA 21 CFR 177.1520 (USA), EU 10/2011 (Europe), GB 4806.7-2016 (China). All materials (PP resin, ink, lamination film) must be food-grade certified.

Certification Checklist

  • FDA Letter of Guarantee: Confirms compliance with US food contact regulations
  • EU Declaration of Compliance: Required for European market access
  • BRC Packaging Certificate: British Retail Consortium standard (optional but preferred)
  • ISO 22000: Food safety management system
  • Heavy Metal Testing: Ensures ink and materials are lead/cadmium-free
  • Migration Testing: Verifies no harmful substance transfer to food

For detailed food safety requirements, see: Food Grade Woven Bags: Safety & Compliance Guide

Storage & Handling

Best Practices for Farm Storage

Proper storage extends bag life and protects product quality. Follow these guidelines:

01

Pallet Storage

Always store bags on pallets (15cm minimum). Never place directly on concrete or soil — moisture wicking causes bottom bag degradation.

02

Stack Height

Maximum 10-12 bags high for 25kg, 8-10 for 50kg. Excessive weight crushes bottom bags, damaging seams.

03

Indoor Storage

Preferred: dry warehouse with 50-70% humidity. Non-UV bags last 3-5 years indoors. Keep away from direct sunlight.

04

Outdoor Storage

Use UV-stabilized bags only. Cover with tarpaulin. Expected life: 18-24 months. Inspect monthly for degradation.

05

FIFO Rotation

First In, First Out. Mark bags with production date. Don't store filled bags beyond 12 months for food products.

06

Pest Control

Woven bags are rodent-resistant but not proof. Maintain clean warehouse, use bait stations, inspect regularly.
