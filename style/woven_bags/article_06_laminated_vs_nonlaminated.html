01
Quick Answer

At a Glance: Key Differences

Lamination adds a film layer to woven fabric. This single difference affects moisture resistance, print quality, cost, and suitable applications.

Laminated Bags

  • Film coating (BOPP or PE) on surface
  • 100% moisture-proof barrier
  • Premium print surface (photo quality with BOPP)
  • Dust-proof containment
  • Easy-clean, wipeable surface
  • 15-25% higher cost
  • Best for: food, chemicals, export, retail

Non-Laminated Bags

  • Bare woven fabric (no coating)
  • Breathable (air passes through)
  • Standard flexo printing only
  • Some dust seepage possible
  • Cannot be wiped clean
  • Lower cost (baseline)
  • Best for: vegetables, dry non-hygroscopic materials

Rule of Thumb: If your product is hygroscopic (absorbs moisture), sensitive to humidity, or requires premium branding — choose laminated. If your product needs breathability (fresh vegetables) or cost is the primary driver — non-laminated works.

PE Liner (Insert)

Separate PE bag inserted inside woven bag, not laminated to fabric.

Cost-Saving Option: PE liner bags (0.03-0.05mm thickness) provide moisture barrier at lower cost than lamination. Liner is inserted after filling, then heat-sealed. Common for export rice and flour.

04
Print Quality

Printing Comparison

Lamination dramatically affects print quality and color reproduction:

BOPP Laminated

  • Full-color rotogravure printing
  • Photo-quality graphics
  • Smooth, glossy surface
  • Excellent color vibrancy
  • Fine detail reproduction (text, barcodes)
  • Scratch-resistant surface

PE Laminated

  • Flexographic printing (1-6 colors)
  • Good solid color reproduction
  • Semi-gloss surface
  • Decent color accuracy
  • Simple logos and text OK
  • Moderate scratch resistance

Non-Laminated

  • Flexographic printing only
  • Rough texture affects print
  • Matte, porous surface
  • Limited color (1-3 colors)
  • No fine details
  • Ink can smudge if wet
05
Application Guide

When to Choose Each Type

Choose BOPP Laminated When:

  • Retail packaging: Supermarket shelves, brand visibility
  • Premium products: Organic foods, specialty grains, gift packaging
  • Complex graphics: Photos, gradients, detailed illustrations
  • High moisture sensitivity: Sugar, flour, milk powder
  • Export packaging: Ocean freight, tropical climates

Choose PE Laminated When:

  • Industrial products: Chemicals, minerals, construction materials
  • Animal feed: Moisture barrier needed, premium print not critical
  • Medium-budget projects: Need moisture protection, cost-conscious
  • Simple branding: Logo and text only, no complex graphics

Choose Non-Laminated When:

  • Fresh produce: Potatoes, onions, vegetables (need breathability)
  • Dry non-hygroscopic materials: Plastic pellets, certain minerals
  • Cost-sensitive applications: Maximum budget constraint
  • Short-term storage: Quick turnover, minimal storage time
  • Internal transfer: Not for retail or export
