Material Science

Material Composition & Manufacturing

PP Woven Bags: Synthetic Polymer

PP (polypropylene) woven bags are made from extruded plastic tape yarn woven on circular looms. The manufacturing process:

1
Step 1 Polymer Extrusion

PP pellets melted at 260-280°C, extruded through spinnerets to form continuous filaments.

2
Step 2 Tape Drawing

Filaments stretched (4-5x original length) to align polymer chains, increasing tensile strength.

3
Step 3 Weaving

Tapes woven on circular looms into tubular fabric. Width: 40-110cm depending on bag size.

4
Step 4 Lamination & Printing

Optional BOPP/PE coating for moisture barrier. Flexo or rotogravure printing for branding.

Jute Bags: Natural Fiber

Jute is a vegetable fiber from Corchorus plants (mainly grown in India and Bangladesh). Processing:

1
Step 1 Retting

Jute stems soaked in water 10-30 days. Microbial action separates fiber from woody core.

2
Step 2 Stripping & Drying

Fibers manually stripped, washed, and sun-dried to 12-15% moisture content.

3
Step 3 Spinning & Weaving

Fibers spun into yarn, then woven on handloom or power loom into hessian cloth.

4
Step 4 Cutting & Sewing

Cloth cut to size, sewn into bags with reinforced seams. Often lined with cotton or PP.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is more eco-friendly: PP or jute?

It depends on usage pattern. Jute is biodegradable but has higher production carbon footprint. PP requires only 11 reuses to break even vs. single-use plastic; jute needs 130+ reuses due to higher production impact. For industrial use (50+ reuses), PP is more sustainable. For occasional retail use with composting, jute wins.

Can jute bags match PP bag strength?

No. Even at 400 GSM (heavy jute), tensile strength is 20-35 MPa vs. 35-50 MPa for 90 GSM PP. To match PP strength, jute needs 500-600 GSM, making it prohibitively expensive and heavy. For loads over 25kg, PP is the only practical choice.

Are there hybrid options combining both materials?

Yes. Jute-PP laminated bags use jute exterior for aesthetics with PP interior liner for moisture barrier. These cost 40-60% more than pure PP but offer compromise between eco-image and functionality. Also available: jute bags with removable PP liner inserts.

Which material prints better for branding?

PP woven bags accept flexographic (1-4 colors), rotogravure (up to 10 colors), and BOPP lamination (full-color photo quality). Jute's rough texture limits printing to 1-2 colors with lower resolution. For complex logos and gradients, PP is superior. For simple stamped logos, jute works.

Do jute bags have odor issues?

Yes, especially when new. Jute has characteristic earthy smell from retting process. This can transfer to sensitive products (flour, sugar, spices). PP is odorless and food-safe. For food packaging, PP woven bags are strongly recommended over jute.
