General Questions

Product Basics

What are PP woven bags?

PP (polypropylene) woven bags are industrial packaging made from woven polypropylene tape yarn. The tapes are extruded from PP resin, woven into tubular fabric on circular looms, then cut and sewn into bags. They offer high strength-to-weight ratio, moisture resistance, and cost-effectiveness — making them ideal for packaging 5-50kg of agricultural, industrial, and food products.

What is GSM in woven bags?

GSM = Grams per Square Meter. It measures fabric weight and correlates with thickness and strength. Common ranges: 65-75 GSM (light-duty, 5-10kg), 80-90 GSM (standard, 10-25kg), 90-100 GSM (heavy-duty, 25-50kg), 110-130 GSM (extra heavy, industrial). Higher GSM = stronger bag but higher cost. For most applications, 85-95 GSM provides optimal balance.

What sizes are available?

Standard sizes (width × length): 30×40cm (5kg), 35×55cm (10kg), 45×70cm (15-20kg), 50×80cm (25kg), 55×85cm (30kg), 60×100cm (50kg). Custom sizes available with MOQ 500-1000 pcs. Gusseted bags add 8-15cm side fold for 3D shape and stable stacking.

What's the difference between laminated and non-laminated bags?

Laminated bags have a film coating (BOPP or PE) providing 100% moisture barrier and premium print surface. Non-laminated bags are breathable (air passes through woven structure) but not moisture-proof. Choose laminated for food, chemicals, export, or humid environments. Choose non-laminated for fresh vegetables or cost-sensitive dry products. See our detailed comparison: Laminated vs Non-Laminated Guide.

Are PP woven bags food-safe?

Yes, when manufactured to food-grade standards. Food-grade bags use virgin PP resin (no recycled content), FDA-compliant additives and inks, and are produced in GMP-certified facilities. Required certifications: FDA 21 CFR 177.1520 (USA), EU 10/2011 (Europe), GB 4806.7-2016 (China). Always request documentation before using for food products. See: Food Grade Woven Bags Guide.

Performance & Durability

Product Performance

How much weight can PP woven bags hold?

Load capacity depends on GSM and construction: 65-75 GSM: 5-10kg. 80-90 GSM: 10-25kg. 90-100 GSM: 25-40kg. 110-130 GSM: 40-50kg. All bags include 20% safety margin. For dynamic loading (dropping, rough handling), specify higher GSM or double-stitched seams. FIBC bulk bags handle 500-2000kg.

Are PP woven bags waterproof?

Standard (non-laminated) woven PP is water-resistant but NOT waterproof — the woven structure has micro-gaps allowing some moisture passage. For complete waterproof protection, specify laminated bags (BOPP or PE coating) or add PE liner bags. Laminated bags provide 100% moisture barrier suitable for humid environments and ocean freight.

How long do PP woven bags last?

Lifespan depends on storage conditions: Indoor storage (non-UV): 3-5 years without significant degradation. Outdoor storage (UV-stabilized): 18-24 months in direct sunlight. Outdoor storage (non-UV): 3-6 months before noticeable weakening. Actual lifespan varies with GSM, usage frequency, and environmental conditions (temperature, humidity, UV exposure).

Can PP woven bags be reused?

Yes, PP woven bags are designed for multiple reuses. Typical reuse count: 30-50 times for retail shopping bags, 10-20 times for industrial packaging, 5-10 times for harsh conditions (construction, outdoor). Reusability makes them more economical and environmentally friendly than single-use packaging. Environmental break-even point: only 11 reuses needed to offset carbon footprint vs. single-use plastic.

Are PP woven bags UV-resistant?

Standard bags have minimal UV resistance. For outdoor use, specify UV-stabilized fabric with HALS (Hindered Amine Light Stabilizers) additives. UV-treated bags last 18-24 months in direct sunlight vs. 3-6 months for non-treated. Essential for: cement bags, construction materials, agricultural products stored outdoors, export packaging in tropical climates.

Sustainability

Environmental & Recycling

Are PP woven bags recyclable?

Yes, PP woven bags are 100% recyclable (#5 plastic). Recycling process: collection → sorting → shredding → washing → re-extrusion into pellets → new PP products. Recycled PP used for: lower-grade bags, plastic lumber, park benches, automotive parts. Check local recycling facilities for acceptance. Some municipalities accept #5 plastics curbside.

Are PP woven bags biodegradable?

No, standard PP woven bags are NOT biodegradable. They are petroleum-based plastic that does not decompose naturally. Environmental benefit comes from reusability (30-50 uses) and recyclability. For biodegradable options, consider: PLA-based bags (corn starch), jute bags, or oxo-degradable additives (controversial, not true biodegradation). Note: "biodegradable" claims require certification — verify before purchasing.

Do you offer eco-friendly options?

Yes, several sustainable options: Recycled PP bags (30-50% post-consumer recycled content). rPET bags (made from recycled plastic bottles). Bio-based PP (emerging technology, limited availability). Lighter GSM options (reduce material use). Reusable design (promote multiple uses). We provide environmental impact data and carbon footprint calculations for sustainability reporting.
