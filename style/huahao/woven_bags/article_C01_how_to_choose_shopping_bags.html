🛒 How to Choose Shopping Bags for Your Business

Complete Buyer's Guide 2026: Material, Size, Printing & Cost Decisions

Choosing the right shopping bags for your business is more complex than it seems. The wrong choice can lead to torn bags, unhappy customers, wasted budget, and missed branding opportunities. The right choice? It enhances customer experience, builds brand visibility, and delivers exceptional marketing ROI.

This comprehensive buyer's guide will walk you through every decision you need to make—from material selection to printing options, sizing to cost calculations—so you can choose the perfect shopping bags for your specific business needs in 2026.

📋 Quick Decision Framework: Before diving in, ask yourself these 5 questions:
  1. What will my customers typically carry in these bags?
  2. What's my budget per bag?
  3. Do I want my logo printed on the bags?
  4. How many bags do I need per month?
  5. Is eco-friendliness important for my brand image?

Step 1: Choose the Right Material

The material determines durability, cost, print quality, and environmental impact. Here are the main options:

Material Cost/Bag Load Capacity Print Quality Best For
Woven PP $0.15-$0.50 15-25 kg Excellent Most retail businesses
Non-Woven PP $0.20-$0.60 8-12 kg Good Events, promotions
Cotton $1.00-$3.50 10-15 kg Moderate Premium/luxury brands
Canvas $1.50-$4.00 12-18 kg Good Lifestyle brands
Paper $0.10-$0.40 5-8 kg Excellent Single-use retail
RPET $0.80-$2.00 10-15 kg Good Eco-focused brands
✅ Our Recommendation: For 80% of retail businesses, woven polypropylene (PP) shopping bags offer the best combination of durability, print quality, cost-effectiveness, and environmental performance. They're the industry standard for good reason.

Step 2: Determine the Right Size

Bag size should match what your customers typically purchase. Too small, and bags will overstuff and tear. Too large, and you're wasting money on unused capacity.

Bag Size (W×H) Common Name Typical Use GSM Recommendation
25×30 cm Small Jewelry, cosmetics, accessories 70-90 GSM
30×35 cm Medium Books, small clothing items, gifts 80-100 GSM
35×40 cm Large Groceries, multiple items, standard shopping 90-110 GSM
40×45 cm Extra Large Bulk shopping, heavy items, family groceries 100-130 GSM
45×50 cm+ Jumbo Warehouse shopping, bedding, large items 110-140 GSM
Don't guess bag sizes! Measure the typical items your customers buy and test with actual products. A bag that's 5cm too small can lead to overstuffed bags and broken handles.

Step 4: Choose GSM (Thickness)

GSM (Grams per Square Meter) measures fabric weight and directly correlates with bag strength:

GSM Feel Load Capacity Use Case Relative Cost
70-80 Thin, flexible 5-8 kg Light items, boutiques $
80-100 Medium 8-12 kg Standard retail, clothing $$
100-120 Sturdy 12-18 kg Groceries, supermarkets $$$
120-140 Heavy-duty 18-25 kg Heavy items, bulk shopping $$$$
🎯 Sweet Spot: For most retail businesses, 90-110 GSM provides the optimal balance of strength, flexibility, and cost. It handles typical shopping loads comfortably while keeping costs reasonable.

Step 5: Decide on Printing & Branding

Printing transforms a generic bag into a powerful marketing tool. Your decision depends on design complexity and budget:

Option A: No Printing (Plain Bags)

  • Cost: Lowest ($0.10-$0.30/bag)
  • MOQ: As low as 500 bags
  • Best For: Internal use, budget-conscious businesses, temporary needs

Option B: Simple Logo Printing (Flexo)

  • Cost: $0.15-$0.40/bag
  • Colors: 1-4 spot colors
  • MOQ: 1,000-3,000 bags
  • Best For: Most businesses with standard logos

Option C: Full-Color Printing (Rotogravure/BOPP)

  • Cost: $0.30-$0.80/bag
  • Colors: Unlimited (CMYK + spots)
  • MOQ: 5,000-10,000 bags
  • Best For: Premium brands, complex designs, photos

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Choosing by Price Alone
The cheapest bag that tears and spills customer purchases costs far more in lost goodwill and reputation damage than a slightly higher-quality option.
Mistake #2: Ignoring Lead Times
Custom printed bags take 15-25 days to produce. Don't wait until you're out of stock—plan reorders at least 6 weeks before you need them.
Mistake #3: Skipping the Sample
Digital proofs show layout, but only a physical sample reveals true material feel, handle comfort, and print quality. Always request samples for first orders.
Mistake #4: Underestimating Quantity Needs
Ordering 2,000 bags when you need 5,000 monthly means reordering frequently, paying setup costs multiple times. Order 2-3 months' supply at once.

🧭 Quick Decision Helper

If you need bags for groceries/heavy items → Choose: Woven PP, 110-120 GSM, webbing handles
If you're a fashion/cosmetics brand → Choose: BOPP laminated woven PP, 90-100 GSM, full-color print
If you're on a tight budget → Choose: Standard woven PP, 90 GSM, 1-2 color flexo print
If you need bags urgently → Choose: Plain bags + stock available, or simple 1-color print (faster production)
If eco-friendliness is your priority → Choose: Woven PP (recyclable, low break-even uses), consider RPET if budget allows

How to Evaluate Suppliers

Not all manufacturers are equal. Here's what to look for:

  • Experience: Look for 5+ years in shopping bag manufacturing
  • Certifications: ISO 9001, FDA (for food-contact), BRC (for UK/EU)
  • Sample Policy: Reputable suppliers offer free or low-cost samples
  • Design Support: Free artwork preparation is a value-add
  • MOQ Flexibility: Can they accommodate both small and large orders?
  • Lead Time: Standard is 15-25 days. Faster is better for urgent needs.
  • Communication: Responsive, clear, English-speaking support
  • References: Ask for client references or case studies
