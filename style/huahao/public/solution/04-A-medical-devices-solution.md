## 🎯 Industry Pain Points

- **Sterility assurance**: Any breach in sterile barrier can cause infection, device failure, or patient harm — zero tolerance for defects
- **Regulatory labyrinth**: FDA 510(k), EU MDR, ISO 11607 — documentation must be flawless and audit-ready
- **Material-device compatibility**: Packaging materials must not degrade, corrode, or interact with the device they protect
- **Accelerated aging validation**: Shelf-life claims (2-5 years) must be backed by real-time and accelerated testing data
- **Global distribution stress**: Devices shipped by air, sea, and ground across climate extremes — packaging must survive all of them

## 🏷️ Material Selection Guide

| Material | Sterilization Method | Barrier Level | Cost | Best For |
|----------|---------------------|--------------|------|----------|
| Tyvek (1073B, 1059B) | EO, Gamma, Steam | High (microbial) | $$ | General sterile devices |
| Aluminum Foil | EO, Gamma | Complete (gas/moisture) | $$$ | Moisture/oxygen sensitive |
| PETG Rigid Tray | EO, Gamma, Steam | N/A (structural) | $$ | Device presentation, kits |
| HDPE Film | EO, Gamma | Moderate | $ | Cost-sensitive single-use |
| Nylon/Polyester | Gamma, EO | Low-moderate | $$ | Cleanroom, lint-sensitive |

**Decision Tip**: For general surgical instruments, Tyvek pouches offer the optimal balance of sterilization compatibility, peelability, and cost. For moisture-sensitive devices (electronics, implants), foil barriers with desiccant are mandatory.

## ❓ FAQ

**Q1: What sterilization methods are compatible with your packaging?**
A: We validate packaging for EO (ethylene oxide), gamma irradiation, steam autoclave, and e-beam. Each device-material pair requires specific validation; we provide complete compatibility documentation.

**Q2: How do you validate shelf life claims?**
A: We conduct accelerated aging per ASTM F1980 (typically 55-60°C) correlated with real-time aging at controlled conditions. Both datasets support shelf-life claims in regulatory submissions.

**Q3: What is the difference between Tyvek 1073B and 1059B?**
A: 1073B is heavier (74 g/m²) with higher tear strength and microbial barrier — used for heavier devices and longer shelf life. 1059B is lighter (64 g/m²) — cost-effective for lighter devices and shorter shelf life.

**Q4: Do you provide DQ/IQ/OQ/PQ documentation?**
A: Yes. We provide complete design qualification, installation qualification, operational qualification, and performance qualification documentation packages for regulatory submissions.

**Q5: Can you produce in cleanroom conditions?**
A: Yes. We operate ISO Class 7 (10,000) cleanrooms as standard, with ISO Class 5 (100) available for critical applications such as implantable and cardiovascular devices.

**Q6: How do you ensure seal integrity?**
A: Every production batch undergoes 100% automated seal inspection plus statistical sampling for seal strength (ASTM F88), peel force, and dye penetration testing per ISO 11607.
