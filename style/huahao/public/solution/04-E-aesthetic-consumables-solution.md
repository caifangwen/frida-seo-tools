# Aesthetic Consumables — Clinical Safety & Premium Perception

> Sterile single-use confidence | Treatment-room elegance | Traceability from manufacturer to patient

---

## 🎯 Industry Pain Points

- **Cross-contamination risk**: Reusable instruments in aesthetic procedures carry infection risk; single-use must be clearly identifiable and sterile
- **Regulatory duality**: Products sit between cosmetics and medical devices; packaging must satisfy both frameworks
- **Patient trust dependency**: Aesthetics patients pay premium prices; any packaging that looks "cheap" undermines perceived value and safety
- **Waste visibility**: Single-use consumables generate visible waste; clinics need sustainable options that don't compromise sterility
- **Expiration management**: Botulinum toxin, fillers, and threads have strict use-by dates; packaging must enable clear FIFO inventory management

## 🏷️ Material Selection Guide

| Material | Sterility | Barrier | Premium Feel | Best For |
|----------|----------|---------|-------------|----------|
| Tyvek Peel Pouch | EO/Gamma compatible | High microbial | Clinical | Single-use instruments |
| Aluminum Foil | N/A (secondary) | Complete | Luxurious | Fillers, toxins, light-sensitive |
| PETG Tray | Gamma/EO | N/A (structural) | Clean | Thread organization, kits |
| Rigid Board + Foam | N/A | N/A | Premium | Cartridges, device accessories |
| Sterile Wrap (SMS) | Steam/EO | Moderate | Clinical | Drapes, large kits |

**Decision Tip**: For sterile single-use instruments, Tyvek peel pouches are the gold standard. For hyaluronic acid fillers, aluminum foil secondary pouches with desiccant protect against oxidation and moisture that degrade product performance.

## ❓ FAQ

**Q1: Do aesthetic consumables need sterile packaging?**
A: It depends on classification and intended use. Needles, cannulas, and instruments that penetrate skin require sterile packaging per ISO 11607. Topical products may not require sterility but benefit from clean, protective packaging.

**Q2: How do you protect hyaluronic acid fillers from degradation?**
A: We use nitrogen-flushed aluminum foil pouches with oxygen absorbers and desiccants. This prevents oxidation and hydrolysis that degrade cross-linking and viscosity.

**Q3: Can packaging help with clinic inventory management?**
A: Absolutely. We design color-coded systems, expiration-date prominent labeling, and QR-linked digital inventory that alerts when products approach expiry or need reorder.

**Q4: What is the minimum order for custom aesthetic packaging?**
A: Standard MOQ is 5,000 units for sterile peel pouches. For procedure kits and trays, 2,000-unit minimums apply. We offer validation batches for new product launches.

**Q5: How do you balance sterility with premium aesthetics?**
A: Our "sterile elegance" approach uses high-quality Tyvek, subtle foil stamping, and clean typography. The packaging feels medical-grade but looks brand-worthy — building both trust and desire.

**Q6: Can patients see that products are sterile and authentic?**
A: Yes. We integrate tamper-evident seals, peel indicators, holographic authentication, and lot-number visibility. Many clients design "opened in front of you" presentation rituals.
