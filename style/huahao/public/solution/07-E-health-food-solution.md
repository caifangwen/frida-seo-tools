## 🎯 Industry Pain Points

- **Nutrient degradation**: Vitamins, proteins, and probiotics degrade with oxygen, moisture, and light — packaging must actively preserve functional claims
- **Claim credibility pressure**: "High protein," "keto-friendly," and "probiotic" must be true at consumption, not just at manufacture
- **On-the-go lifestyle**: Health foods are consumed in gyms, offices, and cars; packaging must be portable, resealable, and mess-free
- **Ingredient separation**: Multi-component products (powder + mix-ins, wet + dry) need packaging that keeps them separate until use
- **Regulatory scrutiny**: Health claims are heavily regulated; packaging must support claims without crossing into drug territory

## ❓ FAQ

**Q1: How do you keep probiotics alive in packaging?**
A: Nitrogen flush eliminates oxygen, foil barriers prevent ingress, and desiccants control moisture. We validate CFU counts at manufacture and through accelerated shelf-life to support "guaranteed at expiry" claims.

**Q2: What is the best packaging for meal replacement powders?**
A: High-barrier foil pouches with resealable zip for daily use. Single-serve stick packs for travel and gym. Both with nitrogen flush for nutrient preservation.

**Q3: Can health food packaging support regulatory claims?**
A: Yes. We design labels that support approved health claims while avoiding drug claims. We provide regulatory review to ensure compliance with FDA, EFSA, and TGA requirements.

**Q4: How do you handle multi-component products (powder + mix-ins)?**
A: Dual-chamber pouches with tear-separator keep components isolated until use. Popular for protein + collagen, powder + granola, and wet + dry meal kits.

**Q5: Is sustainable packaging viable for high-barrier health foods?**
A: Yes. Our compostable barrier films support 6-12 month shelf life for most health foods. For ultra-sensitive products (probiotics), concentrate formats and reusable containers reduce impact significantly.

**Q6: How important is on-the-go portability for health food packaging?**
A: Critical. 60%+ of health foods are consumed outside the home. Stick packs, resealable pouches, and single-serve formats significantly increase usage occasions and repurchase rates.
