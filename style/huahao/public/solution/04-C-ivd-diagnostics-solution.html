Challenges

Industry Pain Points

IVD packaging must protect specimen integrity, reagent stability, and test accuracy across unpredictable shipping environments. A compromised package does not just mean product loss — it means incorrect diagnoses and delayed treatment decisions.

Biohazard Containment

Diagnostic specimens and reagents carry infectious agents; packaging must prevent leakage and exposure.

Result Accuracy Dependency

A compromised reagent or specimen invalidates the test — packaging errors become diagnostic errors.

Temperature Fragility

Reagents, control materials, and specimens often require strict 2-8°C or frozen conditions.

Multi-Modal Shipping

IVD products travel by air, ground, and courier with varying handling quality — packaging must survive all.

Regulatory Diversity

CE-IVD, FDA 510(k), WHO prequalification — each market has distinct packaging and labeling demands.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a 95kPa biohazard pouch and when is it required?

A 95kPa pouch withstands 95 kilopascals of pressure differential without leaking. It's required by IATA for transporting UN 3373 Category B biological substances (human and animal specimens) by air.

How long can your cold chain shippers maintain temperature?

Standard configurations maintain 2-8°C for 48-72 hours and -20°C for 24-48 hours in summer ambient conditions. We validate each configuration with actual shipping lane data and ISTA 7E testing.

Can you package at-home diagnostic tests for consumers rather than labs?

Yes. We specialize in "clinical-grade, consumer-friendly" packaging — rigid boxes, soft-touch finishes, illustrated instructions, and discreet outer shipping — while maintaining all regulatory requirements.

What documentation do you provide for regulatory submissions?

We provide design history files, risk management documentation, biocompatibility data, shipping validation reports, and stability data packages tailored to CE-IVD, FDA 510(k), or WHO prequalification requirements.

How do you prevent test strip degradation from humidity?

We use aluminum foil pouches with desiccant and oxygen absorber, heat-sealed under low-humidity conditions. WVTR is validated to <0.1 g/m²/day for extended shelf life.

Can specimen packaging be customized for specific collection devices?

Absolutely. We design custom tube holders, swab slots, and primary container fits for your specific collection system — ensuring secure transport and intuitive use.
