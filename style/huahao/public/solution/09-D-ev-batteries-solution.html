Industry Analysis

Industry Pain Points

EV battery packaging is arguably the most safety-critical segment in all of packaging engineering. Lithium-ion batteries store enormous energy density in a chemically unstable form. When damaged, shorted, or overheated, they can enter thermal runaway — a self-sustaining reaction that produces temperatures exceeding 1,000 degrees C, toxic gases, and violent ejection of flaming materials. The packaging must not merely protect; it must prevent, contain, and mitigate. Meanwhile, international transport of lithium batteries is governed by UN38.3 — a comprehensive testing protocol that many manufacturers fail. Non-compliance grounds shipments, delays production, and can result in regulatory penalties.

Thermal Runaway Risk

Lithium-ion batteries can ignite violently if damaged, shorted, or overheated. Packaging must prevent, contain, and mitigate.

UN38.3 Regulatory Maze

International transport requires rigorous testing and documentation. Non-compliance grounds shipments and stops production.

Weight and Size Extremes

EV battery packs weigh 300-500kg. Energy storage systems reach 10,000kg. Packaging must handle massive loads safely.

Insulation Requirements

Batteries must not short against conductive surfaces. Packaging must provide dielectric isolation throughout transport.

Supply Chain Scale

Gigafactories produce millions of cells weekly. Packaging must scale without compromising safety or compliance.

Q&A

Frequently Asked Questions

Technical questions about UN38.3 compliance, thermal runaway containment, and dangerous goods documentation — answered with battery safety expertise.

What is UN 38.3 and why is it mandatory?

UN 38.3 is the United Nations testing standard for lithium battery transport safety. It includes 8 tests (altitude, thermal, vibration, shock, external short, crush, overcharge, forced discharge). All lithium batteries must pass before international transport.

Can you design packaging that prevents thermal runaway?

Packaging cannot prevent thermal runaway, but it can contain and mitigate. We design with fire-retardant materials, thermal insulation, venting, and fire suppression integration to limit propagation and protect handlers.

What is the difference between UN 3480 and UN 3481?

UN 3480 covers standalone lithium batteries (higher restrictions). UN 3481 covers batteries packed with or contained in equipment (less restrictive). We determine classification based on your specific shipment configuration.

Do you provide dangerous goods documentation?

Yes. We provide complete dangerous goods declarations, packing instructions, labels, and emergency response information compliant with IATA, IMDG, and DOT regulations.

How do you handle prototype and low-volume battery shipments?

We offer UN-certified packaging for prototype quantities with flexible inner configurations. Dangerous goods documentation is prepared per shipment, and we coordinate with specialized battery logistics providers.

What is changing with solid-state battery packaging?

Solid-state batteries may have reduced fire risk but present different challenges (dendrite penetration, ceramic fracture). We are developing packaging standards in partnership with solid-state battery developers.
