Food processing packaging is barrier engineering. Coffee needs oxygen exclusion with carbon dioxide release. Snacks need moisture and oxygen barriers to maintain crispness. Meat needs vacuum sealing to prevent oxidation. Each product has a unique "enemy" — oxygen, moisture, light, or microorganisms — and the packaging is the defense system.

Shelf Life Extension

Freshness preservation is critical for product quality and reduced food waste throughout distribution.

Barrier Properties

Oxygen, moisture, and light protection requirements vary dramatically by product type.

Resealability

Consumer convenience and post-opening freshness drive zipper and valve adoption.

Product Differentiation

Standing out on crowded retail shelves requires innovative packaging formats and finishes.

Electronics

Electronics Industry Packaging

Electronics packaging protects against invisible threats. Electrostatic discharge (ESD) can destroy a $1,000 chip in microseconds. Moisture absorption can cause "popcorning" during reflow soldering. A single conductive particle can short a circuit board. The packaging is not passive — it is active protection.

ESD Protection

Electrostatic discharge can destroy sensitive components. Packaging must be a Faraday cage.

Moisture Sensitivity

Electronic components require strict humidity control to prevent corrosion and soldering defects.

Physical Protection

Shock and vibration damage during transit is a leading cause of field failures.

Clean Packaging

Contamination-free environment is essential for sensitive devices and cleanroom applications.
