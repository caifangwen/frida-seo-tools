## 🎯 Industry Pain Points

- **Germination rate decline**: Every percentage point of germination loss costs farmers yield and seed companies reputation
- **Moisture sensitivity**: Seeds absorb ambient humidity, triggering premature metabolic activity and viability loss
- **Light degradation**: Some seeds (lettuce, onion) lose viability within hours of light exposure
- **Regulatory seed labeling**: Most jurisdictions mandate specific labeling (germination %, purity, lot number, test date) — non-compliance blocks market access
- **Treatment chemical containment**: Treated seeds carry pesticides and fungicides; packaging must prevent human exposure while maintaining seed safety

## ❓ FAQ

**Q1: How long can seeds maintain viability in your packaging?**
A: Foil barrier pouches with nitrogen flush maintain viability 24-36 months for most species. Vacuum-sealed barrier film extends this to 5+ years for gene bank and backup storage.

**Q2: Do I need foil packaging for all seeds?**
A: Not necessarily. Short-turnover commodity seeds (6-12 months) may use multi-wall kraft with PE liner. High-value hybrids, organic, and long-inventory seeds benefit significantly from foil barriers.

**Q3: How do you handle treated seed safety regulations?**
A: We provide EPA and OSHA-compliant warning labels with universal icons, bilingual text, and prominent "TREATED — NOT FOR FOOD" messaging. All materials meet chemical resistance requirements.

**Q4: Can seed packaging include traceability and authenticity features?**
A: Yes. We offer QR-coded lot numbers, holographic authenticity seals, and blockchain traceability integration — critical for hybrid seed intellectual property protection.

**Q5: What labeling is required for international seed export?**
A: Most countries require: species, variety, lot number, germination percentage, purity percentage, test date, net weight, and origin. We customize labels to ISTA standards and specific country phytosanitary requirements.

**Q6: Are there sustainable options for organic seed packaging?**
A: Yes. Our compostable kraft + PLA packets meet organic certification requirements while providing adequate moisture protection for short-to-medium storage periods.
