Industry Pain Points

Medical device packaging operates under the most demanding quality standards in healthcare. A single defect can cascade into patient harm, regulatory sanctions, or product recalls. Understanding these critical pain points is the first step toward designing packaging that performs flawlessly under real-world clinical and distribution conditions.

Regulatory Labyrinth

Material Compatibility

Packaging materials must not degrade, corrode, or interact with the device they protect.

Aging Validation

Shelf-life claims (2-5 years) must be backed by real-time and accelerated testing data.

Global Distribution

Devices shipped by air, sea, and ground across climate extremes — packaging must survive all.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What sterilization methods are compatible with your packaging?

We validate packaging for EO (ethylene oxide), gamma irradiation, steam autoclave, and e-beam. Each device-material pair requires specific validation; we provide complete compatibility documentation.

How do you validate shelf life claims?

We conduct accelerated aging per ASTM F1980 (typically 55-60°C) correlated with real-time aging at controlled conditions. Both datasets support shelf-life claims in regulatory submissions.

What is the difference between Tyvek 1073B and 1059B?

1073B is heavier (74 g/m²) with higher tear strength and microbial barrier — used for heavier devices and longer shelf life. 1059B is lighter (64 g/m²) — cost-effective for lighter devices and shorter shelf life.

Do you provide DQ/IQ/OQ/PQ documentation?

Yes. We provide complete design qualification, installation qualification, operational qualification, and performance qualification documentation packages for regulatory submissions.

Can you produce in cleanroom conditions?

Yes. We operate ISO Class 7 (10,000) cleanrooms as standard, with ISO Class 5 (100) available for critical applications such as implantable and cardiovascular devices.

How do you ensure seal integrity?

Every production batch undergoes 100% automated seal inspection plus statistical sampling for seal strength (ASTM F88), peel force, and dye penetration testing per ISO 11607.
