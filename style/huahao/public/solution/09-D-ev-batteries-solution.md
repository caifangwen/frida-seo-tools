## 🎯 Industry Pain Points

- **Thermal runaway risk**: Lithium-ion batteries can ignite violently if damaged, shorted, or overheated — packaging must prevent, contain, and mitigate
- **UN38.3 regulatory maze**: International transport of lithium batteries requires rigorous testing and documentation; non-compliance grounds shipments
- **Weight and size extremes**: EV battery packs weigh 300-500kg; energy storage systems reach 10,000kg; packaging must handle massive loads
- **Insulation requirements**: Batteries must not short against conductive surfaces; packaging must provide dielectric isolation
- **Supply chain scale**: Gigafactories produce millions of cells weekly; packaging must scale without compromising safety

## ❓ FAQ

**Q1: What is UN 38.3 and why is it mandatory?**
A: UN 38.3 is the United Nations testing standard for lithium battery transport safety. It includes 8 tests (altitude, thermal, vibration, shock, external short, crush, overcharge, forced discharge). All lithium batteries must pass before international transport.

**Q2: Can you design packaging that prevents thermal runaway?**
A: Packaging cannot prevent thermal runaway, but it can contain and mitigate. We design with fire-retardant materials, thermal insulation, venting, and fire suppression integration to limit propagation and protect handlers.

**Q3: What is the difference between UN 3480 and UN 3481?**
A: UN 3480 covers standalone lithium batteries (higher restrictions). UN 3481 covers batteries packed with or contained in equipment (less restrictive). We determine classification based on your specific shipment configuration.

**Q4: Do you provide dangerous goods documentation?**
A: Yes. We provide complete dangerous goods declarations, packing instructions, labels, and emergency response information compliant with IATA, IMDG, and DOT regulations.

**Q5: How do you handle prototype and low-volume battery shipments?**
A: We offer UN-certified packaging for prototype quantities with flexible inner configurations. Dangerous goods documentation is prepared per shipment, and we coordinate with specialized battery logistics providers.

**Q6: What is changing with solid-state battery packaging?**
A: Solid-state batteries may have reduced fire risk but present different challenges (dendrite penetration, ceramic fracture). We are developing packaging standards in partnership with solid-state battery developers.
