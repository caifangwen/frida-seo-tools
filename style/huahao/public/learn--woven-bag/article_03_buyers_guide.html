Getting Started

Step 1: Define Your Requirements

Before contacting suppliers, clarify these five critical parameters. This prevents costly mistakes and ensures accurate quotations. Procurement managers who skip this step typically receive incomparable quotes and face unexpected costs later.

This guide is based on interviews with 150+ procurement managers, analysis of 500+ supplier quotations, and independent laboratory testing data. The step-by-step framework below will help you avoid common pitfalls and make informed decisions that save 15-30% on total packaging costs.

Why This Matters: A rice mill in Thailand learned this the hard way — they ordered 50,000 bags without specifying UV stabilization. Bags degraded within 3 months of outdoor storage, causing $45,000 in product losses. A simple specification requirement would have prevented this entirely.
1
Parameter 1 Load Capacity

What weight will the bag carry? 10kg, 25kg, 50kg? This determines GSM and construction. Add 20% safety margin to your maximum load. For example, if filling 25kg rice, specify 30kg capacity to account for handling stress.

2
Parameter 2 Storage Conditions

Indoor warehouse or outdoor exposure? Outdoor requires UV-stabilized fabric (0.3-0.5% HALS content). Humid environments or marine freight need lamination. Tropical climates (80%+ humidity) demand moisture barrier protection.

3
Parameter 3 Filling Method

Manual filling (open mouth) or automatic (valve bags)? Valve bags cost 15-20% more but enable automated packing lines (500+ bags/hour). Consider your existing equipment and labor costs.

4
Parameter 4 Printing Requirements

Simple logo (flexo printing) or full-color graphics (BOPP lamination)? More colors = higher cost and MOQ. Flexo: 1-4 colors, 500 pcs MOQ. Rotogravure: up to 10 colors, 2000+ pcs MOQ. BOPP: full color, 5000+ pcs MOQ.

5
Parameter 5 Certification Needs

Food contact requires FDA/EU compliance documentation. Export to specific markets may need additional certifications (BRC, HACCP, GMP). Verify certification validity through issuing body's database before ordering.

Pro Tip: Create a specification sheet before requesting quotes. Include: dimensions (L×W), GSM, bag type (open mouth/valve/gusseted), printing colors, quantity, and delivery deadline. This reduces back-and-forth and ensures comparable quotes. Download our free spec template below.

GSM Selection

Step 2: Choose the Right GSM

GSM (grams per square meter) is the single most important specification. It determines strength, durability, and cost. Higher GSM means thicker fabric, more material, and greater load capacity — but also 8-12% cost increase per 10 GSM increment. Here's how to match GSM to your application without over-specifying:

The key principle is right-sizing: select the lowest GSM that safely handles your product weight and storage conditions. Many buyers overspend by 15-25% by specifying heavier fabric than necessary. Conversely, under-specifying GSM leads to bag failures, product losses, and customer complaints — far more costly than the fabric savings.

03
Lamination Decision

Step 3: Laminated vs Non-Laminated

Lamination adds a film layer (BOPP or PE) to the woven fabric. This affects moisture resistance, print quality, and cost. The choice depends on your product's sensitivity to humidity and your branding requirements.

The lamination decision is critical because it impacts both performance and economics. Laminated bags cost 15-25% more but provide superior moisture protection and print quality. Non-laminated bags are more economical but offer no moisture barrier. Here's how to decide:

Quick Decision Rule: If your product absorbs moisture (flour, sugar, animal feed, chemicals) or ships through humid climates (Southeast Asia, Africa, coastal regions), specify lamination. For dry, non-hygroscopic products (sand, gravel, some grains) stored indoors, non-laminated is cost-effective.

Laminated Bags

  • Complete moisture barrier (0% water absorption)
  • Premium print surface (BOPP enables photo-quality printing)
  • Dust-proof containment for fine powders
  • Easy-clean surface, wipeable exterior
  • 15-25% higher cost vs. non-laminated
  • Best for: food, chemicals, export, humid climates

Non-Laminated Bags

  • Breathable fabric (air circulation prevents condensation)
  • Lower cost (saves 15-25% on material)
  • Suitable for dry, non-hygroscopic products
  • Standard flexo printing (1-4 colors, lower resolution)
  • Not moisture-proof (2-5% water absorption possible)
  • Best for: vegetables, onions, potatoes, non-hygroscopic materials

05
Sample Testing

Step 5: Test Samples Before Bulk Order

Never place bulk orders without physical sample approval. Samples reveal quality issues that specifications and photos cannot. Here's what to test systematically:

Sample testing is your insurance policy against costly mistakes. A $50 sample evaluation prevents $50,000+ in potential losses from defective bulk orders. Professional procurement managers maintain a standardized testing protocol and document all results for supplier comparison.

Real-World Impact: A feed manufacturer in Philippines skipped sample testing and ordered 30,000 bags directly. 40% had seam failures during filling, causing 3-day production halt and $28,000 in losses. Always test samples with your actual product and filling equipment before committing to bulk orders.
01

Dimensional Check

Measure length, width, gusset with steel tape measure. Tolerance should be ±5mm. Verify against your spec sheet. Measure 3 samples minimum — consistency matters.

02

GSM Verification

Cut 10x10cm sample, weigh on precision scale (0.01g accuracy). Weight (g) x 10 = GSM. Should be ±5% of spec. Example: 8.5g = 85 GSM. Test 3 different areas of bag.

03

Load Test

Fill with your product (or equivalent weight sand/rice), drop from 1m height onto concrete. Check for seam failure, fabric tearing, or stitch pull-out. Repeat 3 times minimum.

04

Print Quality

Check color accuracy against approved proof, registration alignment, smudging. Rub with damp cloth — ink should not transfer. Verify barcode scannability if applicable.

05

Seam Strength

Pull bottom and side seams manually. Stitching should be tight with no loose threads, gaps, or skipped stitches. seam should not separate under 15-20kg pull force.

06

Moisture Test

For laminated bags: fill with water, leave 24 hours. Check for leaks, seepage through fabric, or delamination. Bag should remain completely dry on exterior.

Sample Policy: Reputable suppliers provide free samples (5-10 pcs) for qualified buyers. You pay shipping only (typically $30-50 by DHL/FedEx). Custom printed samples cost $50-150 (refundable with bulk order). Lead time: 5-7 days for plain samples, 10-12 days for printed. Document all test results for comparison between suppliers.

Quality Control

Step 8: Quality Control & Inspection

Define acceptance criteria in your purchase order before production begins. Standard quality benchmarks for PP woven bags:

Quality control is not optional — it's your final defense against defective products reaching your production line or customers. Define clear acceptance criteria in your PO and enforce them consistently. Suppliers who know you inspect shipments will maintain higher standards.

Dimensions (LxW) Within spec sheet Steel tape measure ±5mm

Third-Party Inspection: For orders over $10,000, consider hiring SGS, BV, or Intertek for pre-shipment inspection. Cost: $300-500 per inspection day. They verify quantity, quality, packaging, labeling, and provide detailed photographic report. Worth the investment for critical orders or first-time supplier relationships. Include inspection clause in your PO: "Subject to passing third-party inspection before balance payment."
