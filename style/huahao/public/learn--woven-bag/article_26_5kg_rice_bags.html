Dimensions

Standard Dimensions

The 5kg rice bag occupies a sweet spot in retail packaging — large enough for family consumption, small enough for comfortable carrying, and compact enough for efficient shelf display. Getting dimensions right is critical for both consumer appeal and supply chain efficiency.

Width, Length, and Gusset for 5kg

Standard 5kg rice bag dimensions vary by region and rice variety (long grain vs. short grain have different bulk densities), but the most common specification is:

  • Width: 30–35 cm
  • Length: 50–60 cm
  • Gusset: 5–8 cm (optional, for stand-up stability)
  • Finished bag height (filled): 35–45 cm
  • Volume capacity: 6.5–7.5 liters (rice bulk density ~0.75 kg/L)

Long-grain rice (bulk density ~0.72 kg/L) requires slightly more volume than short-grain rice (~0.80 kg/L). For mixed-product lines, design around the lowest bulk density variety to ensure all types fit comfortably.

Asian markets (Southeast Asia, East Asia) tend toward narrower, taller bags (30×55 cm) that fit local retail shelving and carrying customs. African and Middle Eastern markets often prefer slightly wider, shorter formats (35×50 cm) that accommodate different retail fixtures.

Dimensional Tip: Always verify dimensions with filled product samples before finalizing tooling. "Flat" dimensions (cut fabric size) differ significantly from "filled" dimensions due to product expansion, gusset deployment, and bag bulge. A bag that looks correct when flat may be too tight or too loose when filled.

02
Specifications

Fabric Specifications

5kg rice bags don't require heavy-duty fabric, but they must maintain integrity through handling, transport, and shelf display. The key is balancing adequate strength with cost efficiency and consumer handling comfort.

65–75gsm, 10×10 Density

The industry standard for 5kg rice bags is 65–75 GSM fabric at 10×10 density. This specification provides:

  • Tensile strength: 800–1,200 N warp — sufficient for 5kg loads with 5:1 safety factor
  • Drop test: Passes 1.2 m drop from filled height without seam failure
  • Cost efficiency: Lower GSM than industrial bags keeps per-bag cost minimal
  • Consumer feel: Lightweight fabric is easier for retail customers to handle
  • Print quality: 10×10 density accepts 1–3 color flexo printing adequately

For premium retail brands using BOPP lamination, fabric weight can be reduced to 60–65 GSM because the film adds structural rigidity. For economy markets or rough-handling channels, 75–80 GSM provides extra durability margin.

💡
Over-Specification Warning: Some buyers specify 80–90 GSM for 5kg bags out of caution. This adds 15–20% material cost with no meaningful performance benefit — a 75 GSM bag handles 5kg easily. Reserve heavier fabric for export shipments or abusive handling environments.
03
Product Quality

Ventilation Requirements

Rice is a living grain that continues to respire after harvest, albeit at declining rates. Proper ventilation prevents moisture accumulation that leads to mold, yellowing, and off-flavors.

Breathable Weave for Moisture Escape

Standard unlaminated 10×10 woven fabric provides natural breathability with air permeability of 0.5–2.0 L/m²/s. This allows rice to "breathe" — releasing moisture from residual drying and respiration while preventing condensation buildup inside the bag.

However, this breathability is a double-edged sword. In humid environments, woven fabric also allows moisture ingress. For rice stored longer than 3 months in tropical climates, or for export to high-humidity destinations, consider:

  • BOPP lamination: Blocks moisture ingress while maintaining some breathability at seams
  • PE liner (loose): Inner liner provides moisture barrier without sealing
  • Perforation control: Avoid perforated bags for rice — excessive ventilation accelerates moisture exchange
  • Moisture content at fill: Rice should be ≤14% moisture before packaging; higher levels guarantee spoilage regardless of bag construction

Pro Tip: For retail rice in climate-controlled supermarkets, unlaminated woven bags with 10×10 density provide ideal ventilation. The air conditioning maintains low humidity, so moisture ingress is minimal. Lamination is only necessary for outdoor storage, export shipping, or regions with seasonal humidity spikes.

04
Consumer Experience

Consumer Handling

The 5kg format is specifically designed for consumer purchase and manual transport. Handling convenience directly influences purchase decisions, especially in markets where customers carry bags home rather than using carts.

Easy Carry, Resealable Options

Handles: Integrated loop handles or die-cut hand holes are standard for 5kg rice bags. As discussed in our handle design guide, 3–4 cm wide integrated handles with X-box reinforcement comfortably support 5kg loads. Handles should be positioned 8–12 cm below the top edge to allow hand clearance.

Bag rigidity: A slightly stiffer bag (achieved through higher tape orientation or light BOPP coating) maintains its shape during carrying, preventing the "sagging" that makes heavy bags feel heavier. However, excessive stiffness makes bags difficult to fold and store.

Resealable closures: While most rice bags use simple sewn or heat-sealed tops, some premium brands add zipper strips or tape closures to allow consumers to reseal partially used bags. This extends freshness and reduces spillage. Resealable features add $0.03–0.08 per bag but support premium positioning.

Tear notch: A small V-shaped notch at the top edge allows easy opening without scissors. Position the notch away from the handle attachment to prevent accidental tearing into the load-bearing structure.

05
Branding

Print Design

The 5kg format is where woven bag branding is most competitive. In supermarket rice aisles, dozens of brands compete for attention, and packaging design is the primary differentiator.

Brand, Grade, Origin, and Cooking Instructions

Mandatory information: Weight (5kg net), brand name, product type (jasmine, basmati, parboiled, etc.), and origin. Many markets require additional labeling: harvest date, milling date, nutritional information, and cooking instructions.

Brand differentiation: In crowded rice markets, color blocking, photographic imagery of paddy fields, and ethnic design motifs create shelf recognition. Premium brands use BOPP lamination with full-color gravure to achieve photographic quality. Economy brands rely on 1–2 color flexo printing with bold typography.

Cultural considerations: Rice packaging design varies enormously by region. Southeast Asian markets favor gold, red, and green colors with traditional motifs. Middle Eastern markets prefer understated elegance with Arabic calligraphy. African markets respond to bold, high-contrast designs with clear product photography.

  • Front panel: Brand logo, product name, appetite-appeal imagery
  • Back panel: Nutritional info, cooking instructions, QR code
  • Side panels: Origin story, certification marks (organic, fair trade)
  • Top/bottom: Batch code, date, barcode
  • Color strategy: Differentiate from competitors while respecting cultural associations
🌾
Market Insight: In many Asian markets, consumers associate bag "crispness" with rice freshness. A stiff, sharply creased bag signals recent production and quality milling. This perception favors slightly higher GSM fabric or light lamination that maintains bag shape, even for economy brands.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Why 5kg and not 4kg or 6kg?

5kg emerged as the global retail sweet spot through market evolution. It's heavy enough to provide value (family consumption for 1–2 weeks) but light enough for comfortable manual carrying by adults of varying strength. In some markets, 2kg and 10kg are also standard, but 5kg dominates globally. Regional exceptions exist: Japan favors 5kg and 10kg; India uses 5kg, 10kg, and 25kg; West Africa commonly sees 5kg and 25kg. Match your target market's established conventions rather than inventing new sizes.

Should 5kg rice bags be laminated?

For climate-controlled retail distribution, unlaminated bags are standard and preferred for breathability. Lamination is recommended when: (1) rice will be stored outdoors or in non-climate-controlled warehouses; (2) export shipping involves humidity exposure; (3) premium brand positioning requires glossy print quality; (4) the product is pre-washed or parboiled rice with higher surface moisture. For typical dry-milled rice sold through supermarkets, unlaminated 10×10 at 70 GSM is the economical standard.

What is the shelf life of rice in woven bags?

White rice in unlaminated woven bags maintains quality for 12–18 months in temperate, dry storage. In tropical climates with high humidity, quality declines after 6–9 months due to moisture absorption and potential pest activity. Brown rice (with bran layer) has shorter shelf life: 6 months temperate, 3 months tropical, due to bran oil rancidity. For extended shelf life, use BOPP lamination or sealed PE liners and store below 20°C with <60% RH.

Can I use perforated bags for rice?

Generally not recommended. Perforations increase air permeability, which accelerates moisture exchange with ambient air. In humid conditions, perforated bags admit moisture faster than standard weave. In dry conditions, they accelerate moisture loss that can cause rice to become brittle and increase breakage during cooking. Standard 10×10 weave provides adequate ventilation without perforation. Only consider perforation if rice is packed at high moisture and needs aggressive drying — but better practice is to dry rice to ≤14% before packaging.
