Overview

Why Woven Bags Dominate Agriculture

PP woven bags have become the packaging standard for agricultural products worldwide, accounting for over 85% of grain and fertilizer packaging in global markets. They combine strength, breathability, moisture resistance, and cost-effectiveness — critical factors for farm-to-market supply chains that span thousands of miles and multiple handling points.

The polypropylene woven fabric structure creates microscopic gaps that allow natural air circulation, preventing moisture buildup that leads to mold growth in stored grains. This inherent breathability, combined with the material's water-resistant properties, makes woven bags ideal for agricultural products that must remain dry and fresh during extended storage periods.

Unlike traditional packaging materials such as jute, paper, or cotton, PP woven bags offer consistent quality and predictable performance. Jute bags, while biodegradable, absorb moisture and degrade unpredictably. Paper bags lack the tensile strength needed for heavy loads and offer poor moisture resistance. Woven bags strike the optimal balance between durability, breathability, and affordability — making them the default choice for agricultural operations from small farms to international commodity traders.

Key Advantages for Agriculture
  • Breathability: Micro-gaps allow air circulation, preventing mold in grains
  • Moisture Resistance: PP fiber doesn't absorb water, protecting contents
  • High Strength: 35-50 MPa tensile strength handles rough handling
  • UV Options: Stabilized fabric for outdoor farm storage (18-24 months)
  • Cost Effective: 60-80% cheaper than jute, 40-50% cheaper than paper
  • Customizable: Printing for branding, grading, traceability

Product Applications

Packaging Solutions by Product

Rice & Grain Packaging

Rice, wheat, corn, and other grains require breathable packaging that prevents moisture buildup while allowing continuous ventilation. The key challenge in grain packaging is balancing moisture resistance with breathability — bags must block external humidity while allowing internal moisture to escape.

For domestic markets, open-mouth bags with standard weave provide adequate ventilation at minimal cost. Export shipments require additional protection: laminated bags with optional PE liner prevent moisture ingress during ocean freight, where container humidity can exceed 90%.

Grain packaging specifications vary by product characteristics:

  • Rice: Requires food-grade bags with smooth inner surface to prevent grain breakage during filling and handling. Long-grain rice often uses premium BOPP bags with window for quality visibility.
  • Wheat: Lower value commodity, so cost optimization is key. Standard laminated bags with 1-2 color printing suffice for bulk shipments.
  • Corn/Maize: Higher moisture content at harvest (18-25%) demands excellent breathability. Non-laminated or micro-perforated bags preferred for initial drying period.
  • Soybeans: High oil content requires bags that don't trap heat. Standard weave with adequate ventilation prevents oil rancidity.

Fertilizer Packaging

Fertilizers present unique packaging challenges: NPK, urea, ammonium sulfate, and other chemical fertilizers are highly hygroscopic (water-absorbing) and corrosive. Without proper protection, fertilizer absorbs moisture from air, causing caking, weight gain, and nutrient degradation. Bags must provide both moisture barrier and chemical resistance.

The moisture absorption rate varies by fertilizer type:

  • GSM: 90-100 GSM for 25-50kg bags — higher weight capacity requires thicker fabric
  • Lamination: BOPP or PE coating mandatory — creates moisture barrier that protects hygroscopic contents
  • UV Treatment: Required for outdoor storage at farms — prevents UV degradation during 12-18 month exposure
  • Valve Type: Preferred for automated filling lines — reduces dust, increases filling speed by 30-40%
  • Anti-slip: Textured surface for stable pallet stacking — prevents shifting during transport

Important: Never use non-laminated bags for fertilizer. Even brief exposure to humid air causes moisture absorption, leading to product caking and potential bag failure. Always specify BOPP lamination minimum.

Animal Feed Packaging

Animal feed — including poultry feed, cattle feed, fish feed, and pet food — requires careful moisture control to prevent spoilage, mold growth, and nutritional degradation. Feed products contain proteins, fats, and vitamins that are sensitive to oxygen and humidity, making barrier properties critical.

The primary concern is preventing fat oxidation and vitamin loss. Even moderate humidity exposure during storage reduces feed quality, causing rancidity in high-fat formulations and clumping in powdered feeds. PE liner or laminated construction provides the necessary moisture barrier.

Vegetable & Fresh Produce

Potatoes, onions, carrots, and other root vegetables need ventilated packaging that maximizes air circulation while providing containment for transport and retail display. Unlike grains, fresh produce continues to respire after harvest, releasing moisture and heat — making ventilation the top packaging priority.

The respiration rate varies significantly by produce type, affecting packaging requirements:

  • Perforated bags: Micro-holes (2-5mm) distributed across bag surface for uniform ventilation
  • GSM: 70-85 GSM for 5-20kg loads — lighter weight reduces cost while maintaining strength
  • Non-laminated: Breathability is critical — lamination blocks air flow and causes condensation
  • Mesh options: Extra ventilation for high-moisture produce like garlic and ginger

Food Safety

Food Grade Requirements & Certifications

Food-contact woven bags must comply with strict regulations that vary by market. Every component — from the PP resin base fabric to the printing ink and lamination film — must be certified safe for direct food contact. Failure to maintain proper certification can result in shipment rejection, fines, or product recalls.

📷 Food-Grade Certification Documents - FDA, EU, BRC Compliance

Required Certifications for Food Packaging: FDA 21 CFR 177.1520 (USA), EU 10/2011 (Europe), GB 4806.7-2016 (China). All materials (PP resin, ink, lamination film) must be food-grade certified. Supplier must provide Letters of Guarantee for each material batch.

Storage & Handling

Best Practices for Farm Storage

Proper storage extends bag life and protects product quality. Even the best woven bags will fail if stored incorrectly. Follow these guidelines to maximize shelf life and minimize product loss:

📷 Proper Pallet Storage in Warehouse - Bags Stacked on Pallets with Adequate Spacing
01

Pallet Storage

Always store bags on pallets (15cm minimum height). Never place directly on concrete or soil — moisture wicking causes bottom bag degradation within weeks. Use wooden or plastic pallets, not cardboard.

02

Stack Height

Maximum 10-12 bags high for 25kg, 8-10 for 50kg. Excessive weight crushes bottom bags, damaging seams and causing tears. Use cross-stacking pattern for stability.

03

Indoor Storage

Preferred: dry warehouse with 50-70% humidity, temperature below 30°C. Non-UV bags last 3-5 years indoors. Keep away from direct sunlight and heat sources.

04

Outdoor Storage

Use UV-stabilized bags only. Cover with breathable tarpaulin (not plastic sheet). Expected life: 18-24 months. Inspect monthly for UV degradation signs.

05

FIFO Rotation

First In, First Out. Mark bags with production date or batch code. Don't store filled bags beyond 12 months for food products — quality degrades over time.

06

Pest Control

Woven bags are rodent-resistant but not proof. Maintain clean warehouse, use bait stations at entry points, inspect bags regularly for signs of pest damage.

Common Mistake: Storing bags near fertilizer or chemicals. PP fabric absorbs odors and vapors, which can contaminate food products. Keep food-grade bags in separate, dedicated storage areas.

Printing & Branding

Product Identification & Traceability

Agricultural bags often require detailed labeling for grading, traceability, and regulatory compliance. Printing quality directly impacts brand perception — clear, professional printing signals product quality to buyers and consumers.

BOPP laminated bags offer the highest printing quality, supporting full-color CMYK graphics and fine text. Standard woven bags support 1-4 color flexographic printing, suitable for basic branding and product identification.

Selection Guide

How to Choose the Right Bag

Selecting the optimal woven bag for your agricultural product requires matching bag properties to product characteristics. Use this decision framework:

Step 1: What is your product type?

Grain/Food
→ Prioritize: Breathability + Food Safety

Choose non-laminated or micro-perforated bags for domestic use. For export, use laminated bags with optional PE liner. Ensure FDA/EU certification.

Fertilizer
→ Prioritize: Moisture Barrier + Chemical Resistance

BOPP lamination mandatory. Add PE liner for high-humidity climates or urea packaging. UV treatment required for outdoor storage.

Animal Feed
→ Prioritize: Moisture Control + Product Form

PE liner for mash feed (dust control). Laminated bags for pellet feed. High barrier for aquatic feed. Match GSM to weight (85-95 GSM typical).

Fresh Produce
→ Prioritize: Maximum Ventilation

Non-laminated perforated bags. Hole coverage 3-10% based on respiration rate. Lower GSM (70-85) to reduce cost since strength needs are lower.

Step 2: What is your distribution channel?

Domestic/Retail
→ Prioritize: Print Quality + Branding

BOPP laminated bags with 4-6 color printing. Eye-catching design drives shelf sales. Standard GSM is sufficient (80-90).

Export/Bulk
→ Prioritize: Durability + Compliance

Higher GSM (90-100) for transport resilience. Full certification documentation. Simple printing (1-2 colors) for product ID is sufficient.

Step 3: What are your storage conditions?

Indoor Warehouse
→ Standard bags, no UV needed

Save 10-15% by skipping UV treatment. Bags last 3-5 years indoors. Focus budget on lamination/barrier properties instead.

Outdoor/Farm
→ UV-stabilized bags mandatory

UV treatment adds 18-24 months outdoor life. Verify UV stabilization method (additive vs coating). Additive-based is more durable.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What GSM should I use for 25kg rice bags?

For 25kg rice bags, we recommend 80-90 GSM for domestic markets and 85-95 GSM with lamination for export. The higher GSM provides better drop resistance during handling. If your rice will be palletized and shipped via ocean freight, opt for 90 GSM laminated bags to ensure bags survive 30-45 day voyages without failure.

Can I use the same bags for fertilizer and grain?

No. Fertilizer requires laminated bags with moisture barrier (BOPP or PE coating), while grain often needs breathable non-laminated bags. Using non-laminated bags for fertilizer will cause moisture absorption and product caking. Using laminated bags for grain may trap internal moisture and cause mold. Always match bag type to product requirements.

How long can filled woven bags be stored outdoors?

UV-stabilized bags can last 18-24 months outdoors, but this refers to empty bag shelf life. For filled bags, we recommend limiting outdoor storage to 3-6 months maximum. Even with UV protection, prolonged sun exposure heats bag contents, accelerating product degradation. Always prefer indoor, climate-controlled storage for filled agricultural products.

What's the difference between BOPP lamination and PE coating?

BOPP (Biaxially Oriented Polypropylene) lamination provides excellent print surface + moisture barrier, ideal for premium retail packaging. PE (Polyethylene) coating is a simpler moisture barrier, often used as inner liner. BOPP offers better aesthetics and print quality; PE is more cost-effective for bulk/industrial applications. For fertilizer, either works. For retail grain bags, BOPP is preferred for branding.

Do I need food-grade certification for animal feed bags?

Animal feed bags don't legally require food-grade certification in most markets. However, using food-grade certified materials demonstrates quality commitment and may be required by certain feed manufacturers or retailers. If your feed targets pet food market (human-animal bond products), food-grade certification adds credibility. For livestock/poultry feed, standard industrial-grade bags are typically sufficient.

What's the minimum order quantity for custom printed bags?

Standard minimum order is 5,000-10,000 bags per design for custom printing. BOPP laminated bags with cylinder printing require plate setup (one plate per color), making smaller orders uneconomical. For trial orders, some suppliers offer stock bags with label overlay (sticker printing) at quantities as low as 1,000 bags. Contact us to discuss your volume needs.

How do I test if woven bags meet the claimed strength?

Request a tensile strength test report from the supplier (should show warp/weft strength in N/5cm or MPa). You can also perform a simple drop test: fill a bag with your product, seal it, and drop from 1.2 meters onto concrete. The bag should not burst or tear. For formal testing, third-party labs (SGS, Intertek, BV) can perform tensile, tear, and impact tests per ISO or ASTM standards.
