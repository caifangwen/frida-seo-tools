Types of Paper Bags: 10 Common Styles Explained

A comprehensive guide to paper bag structures, handle types, and applications — helping B2B buyers make informed purchasing decisions.

Understanding the different types of paper bags is essential for B2B buyers making informed purchasing decisions. Each bag style has unique structural features, weight capacities, and ideal use cases. Whether you need wholesale shopping bags, food-grade packaging, or luxury retail bags, this guide will help you match the right bag type to your business requirements.

Choosing the wrong bag type can lead to customer dissatisfaction, product damage, or unnecessary costs. A restaurant that picks heavy-duty retail bags wastes money on over-engineered packaging, while a boutique using food-grade sacks damages its brand perception. Getting it right matters.

Structural Classification

Paper Bags by Structure

1. SOS Self-Opening Sack (SOS Bag)

The SOS bag is engineered for speed and efficiency. Its pre-glued gusset design allows the bag to open automatically when the sides are pulled apart, eliminating the manual opening step entirely. This makes it the go-to choice for high-volume food service operations where every second counts during peak hours.

2. Pinch Bottom Bag

Pinch bottom bags feature a folded and glued bottom gusset that expands significantly when filled. Unlike SOS bags, the gusset is manually formed during production, which makes them slightly slower to open by hand — but their expanded capacity makes them ideal for grocery and bulk item packaging where volume matters more than speed.

3. Square Bottom Bag

Square bottom bags are designed with a flat, rigid bottom panel that allows the bag to stand upright on shelves or counters without support. This self-standing capability transforms the bag from mere packaging into a display tool — particularly valuable in retail environments where shelf presence influences purchasing decisions.

Handle Classification

Paper Bags by Handle Type

4. Twisted Handle Kraft Paper Bag

The twisted handle kraft paper bag is perhaps the most iconic and instantly recognizable retail bag style worldwide. Its twisted paper rope handle — typically 3–6mm in diameter — offers an exceptional balance of strength, comfort, and visual appeal. The handle is reinforced with an internal patch that distributes weight across a wider area of the bag body, preventing tear-out even under significant loads.

5. Flat Handle Paper Bag

Flat handle paper bags use a flat paper ribbon handle (15–30mm wide) instead of a twisted cord. This creates a sleeker, more minimalist aesthetic that appeals to brands seeking a contemporary, understated look. While they carry less weight than twisted handle bags, their lower profile makes them easier to stack and store flat when not in use.

6. Die-Cut Handle Bag

Die-cut handle bags eliminate the need for separate handle material entirely — the handle is cut directly into the bag body during the die-cutting process. This integrated approach reduces material costs and creates a modern, clean design that's particularly popular for events and promotional campaigns where budget efficiency matters.

7. Patch Handle Bag

Patch handle bags represent the strongest option among handled paper bags. A reinforced patch — either hidden inside the bag or visible as an external design element — is applied at the handle attachment point to distribute weight across a wider area. This construction enables the bag to carry heavy items like wine bottles, canned goods, or hardware products without handle tear-out.

Application-Based Classification

Paper Bags by Application

8. Flat Paper Bag (No Handle / Sack Bag)

Flat paper bags are the simplest and most economical option in the paper bag family. Without handles or complex structural elements, they serve as straightforward containment for products that don't require carrying convenience built into the bag itself. Their open-mouth or valve-type designs enable extremely fast packing speeds on automated production lines.

9. Laminated Paper Bag

Laminated paper bags feature a BOPP or PE lamination film applied to the surface, providing water resistance, grease resistance, and enhanced print vibrancy. The lamination creates a smooth gloss or matte finish that elevates the visual impact of printed graphics and brand colors — making it a popular choice for premium products that need both functional protection and shelf appeal.

  • Moisture-resistant surface — protects contents from humidity and spills
  • Enhanced print vibrancy — gloss or matte finish for vivid color reproduction
  • Trade-off: less recyclable — lamination film complicates the recycling process compared to non-laminated bags

Sustainability Note

If recyclability is a priority for your brand, consider non-laminated bags with water-based varnish instead. They offer moderate moisture protection while remaining fully recyclable in standard paper waste streams.

10. Multi-Wall Paper Bag

Multi-wall paper bags are the heavy-duty workhorses of the paper bag world. Constructed with 2–4 layers of kraft paper bonded together, they're designed for industrial and bulk applications where single-layer bags would fail. Each layer adds tensile strength and puncture resistance, enabling these bags to safely transport 25–50 kg of raw materials, agricultural products, or wholesale goods.

How to Choose the Right Paper Bag Type

Use this four-step decision framework to narrow down the ideal bag type for your specific application. Start with your product characteristics, then factor in weight requirements, environmental considerations, and budget constraints.

What are you packaging?

Retail products → Twisted handle, Square bottom

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between SOS bag and pinch bottom bag?

SOS bags open automatically and are ideal for high-speed packing machines. Pinch bottom bags have a manually folded gusset and better suit hand-packing or medium-speed operations. The key difference lies in the gusset formation: SOS bags come pre-opened, while pinch bottom bags require manual expansion.

Which paper bag type has the highest load capacity?

Patch handle bags with reinforced construction can hold 15–20 kg, making them the strongest option for retail packaging. For industrial applications, multi-wall bags exceed this with 25–50 kg capacity.

Are laminated paper bags recyclable?

Laminated bags with BOPP film are more difficult to recycle than non-laminated bags. If recyclability is a priority, choose non-laminated bags with water-based varnish instead, which maintain full recyclability in standard paper waste streams.

Can I mix different sizes in one order?

Most manufacturers allow 2–3 sizes per order with a combined MOQ of 10,000–30,000 pcs. Confirm with your supplier, as some factories impose a minimum per-size threshold.

What GSM should I choose for clothing store bags?

For clothing retail, we recommend 100–130 GSM kraft paper with twisted handles. This provides adequate strength for 3–8 kg loads while maintaining a premium feel and professional appearance.

How do I request a sample?

Contact your paper bag manufacturer with your dimensions, material preference, and design artwork. Expect to receive a PPS (Pre-Production Sample) within 5–10 days. Custom samples typically cost $50–$200 but are refundable on bulk orders.
