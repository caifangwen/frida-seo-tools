## 10 Key Metrics to Evaluate a Paper Bag Supplier

### 1. Production Capacity & Lead Time

A reliable **paper bag manufacturer** should clearly communicate:

| Metric | Typical Range | What to Look For |
|--------|--------------|------------------|
| Daily output | 50,000–500,000 bags | Capacity matching your order volume |
| Standard lead time | 25–40 days | Predictable, documented timelines |
| Rush order capability | 15–20 days (surcharge applies) | Flexibility for urgent needs |
| Sample lead time | 5–10 days | Fast prototyping for design approval |

**Red flag**: Suppliers who cannot provide a clear production schedule or refuse to share factory photos/videos.

### 2. Certifications (FSC, ISO, BRC)

Certifications are non-negotiable for B2B buyers. Key certifications include:

- **FSC (Forest Stewardship Council)**: Ensures responsibly sourced paper. [Learn more in our FSC Certification Guide](05-fsc-certification-guide.md)
- **ISO 9001**: Quality management system certification
- **ISO 22000 / BRC**: Required for **food grade paper bags wholesale**
- **SEDEX / SMETA**: Ethical audit for social compliance

Always verify certification numbers on the official registry. Fake certificates are more common than you think.

### 3. Customization Capabilities

Your ideal **custom paper bag manufacturer** should offer:

- Full size customization (no fixed molds)
- PMS color matching for brand consistency
- Multiple printing methods (flexo, offset, screen, digital)
- Surface finishing options (lamination, gloss/matte varnish, hot stamping, embossing, spot UV)
- Various handle types (twisted paper rope, flat ribbon, cotton rope, die-cut)

> 📖 Related: Compare different [paper bag printing methods: flexo vs offset vs screen vs digital](10-paper-bag-printing-methods.md)

### 4. Quality Control System

Ask your supplier about their QC process. A professional manufacturer follows a 4-stage inspection:

1. **IQC (Incoming Quality Control)**: Raw paper inspection — GSM, moisture, tensile strength
2. **IPQC (In-Process Quality Control)**: Printing color check, die-cut accuracy, glue bonding
3. **FQC (Final Quality Control)**: Finished bag dimensional check, handle pull test
4. **OQC (Outgoing Quality Control)**: Pre-shipment random sampling (AQL standard)

**Key test data to request:**

- Handle pull strength (N)
- Bottom seam peel strength (N/15mm)
- Print colorfastness (ΔE value ≤ 2.0)

### 5. MOQ & Price Transparency

| Order Type | Typical MOQ | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| Standard stock bags | 1,000–3,000 pcs | Limited customization |
| Custom printed bags | 10,000–30,000 pcs | Full customization, plate fees apply |
| Luxury/handmade bags | 3,000–5,000 pcs | Higher labor cost |

Be wary of suppliers quoting prices significantly below market. Hidden costs often appear in plate fees, tooling, packaging upgrades, and inspection charges.

### 6. Design & Pre-Press Support

Top-tier suppliers provide:

- Free dieline templates
- Artwork review & optimization
- 3D mockup rendering
- PPS (Pre-Production Sample) before mass production

### 7. Material Options & Sustainability

Your supplier should offer a range of materials:

- **Virgin kraft paper**: Premium strength, natural look
- **Recycled kraft paper**: Eco-friendly, cost-effective
- **White cardboard**: Clean printing surface for luxury brands
- **Specialty papers**: Textured, metallic, colored papers

### 8. Communication & Responsiveness

Evaluate:

- Response time (within 24 hours is standard)
- English proficiency
- Proactive problem-solving
- Dedicated account manager

### 9. Shipping & Logistics Support

A good **paper bag wholesale** supplier assists with:

- FOB / CIF / DDP Incoterms
- LCL vs FCL optimization
- Customs documentation
- Freight forwarding partnerships

### 10. After-Sales Service & Warranty

Look for:

- Replacement or refund for defective batches
- Clear claims process
- Dispute resolution mechanism

## FAQ: Choosing a Paper Bag Supplier

### Q1: What is the typical MOQ for custom printed paper bags?
Most manufacturers require **10,000–30,000 pcs** per size per design. Some offer lower MOQs (3,000–5,000 pcs) with surcharges.

### Q2: How do I verify FSC certification?
Visit [info.fsc.org](https://info.fsc.org), enter the certificate number, and confirm the certificate holder matches the supplier's company name.

### Q3: Can I order samples before bulk production?
Yes. Always request a **PPS (Pre-Production Sample)** before approving mass production. Expect to pay $50–$200 for custom samples (refundable on bulk orders).

### Q4: What payment terms are standard?
- **Deposit**: 30% T/T upfront
- **Balance**: 70% before shipment (after QC photos)
- L/C at sight for orders above $50,000

### Q5: How do I ensure color consistency across batches?
Request **PMS (Pantone Matching System)** color specification and approve a physical sample under **D65 standard lighting**.

### Q6: What documents do I need for customs clearance?
- Commercial invoice
- Packing list
- Bill of lading
- Certificate of origin (Form E / CO)
- FSC certificate (if applicable)
