White Card Paper Bags Guide: Properties, GSM & Applications

Everything B2B buyers need to know about white cardboard bags — from pulp composition and stiffness to print quality and luxury positioning.

Searching for a white card paper bag that delivers premium presentation and superior print results? White cardboard — also known as ivory board or white paperboard — is the material of choice for brands that demand brightness, rigidity, and flawless color reproduction. This guide breaks down the technical properties, GSM selection, and ideal applications of white cardboard bags for B2B buyers.

01
Material Science

What Is White Cardboard?

White cardboard is a premium paperboard manufactured from bleached chemical pulp, typically sourced from hardwood fibers such as eucalyptus or birch. Unlike unbleached kraft paper, white cardboard undergoes an extensive bleaching process that removes lignin and natural pigments, resulting in a bright white surface with exceptional uniformity.

Bleached Chemical Pulp

Multi-stage bleaching removes colorants, delivering a bright white base with minimal yellowing over time.

High Stiffness

Dense fiber packing and calendering create a rigid structure that holds its shape under load.

Smooth Surface

Ultra-smooth finish provides an ideal substrate for high-resolution offset and screen printing.

💡 Key Distinction

White cardboard is not the same as white kraft paper. White kraft (60–150 GSM) is flexible and used for shopping bags. White cardboard (210–350 GSM) is rigid board used for structured, premium bags and boxes.

03
Print Performance

Surface Characteristics & Print Base

The defining advantage of white card is its smooth, bright white surface that serves as an exceptional print base. Unlike brown kraft — which limits color gamut and mutes bright tones — white card allows for:

  • Full CMYK color reproduction with high color fidelity and contrast
  • PMS spot color matching with ΔE tolerances below 1.5
  • Fine detail rendering down to 0.1mm line weights in offset printing
  • Compatible with premium finishes including lamination, foil stamping, embossing, and spot UV

The surface is typically coated on one side (C1S) or both sides (C2S) with a thin clay layer that further enhances smoothness and ink holdout. C2S white card is preferred for bags where both the exterior and interior may be visible to the customer.

04
Applications

Where White Card Bags Excel

White card paper bags occupy the mid-to-high range of the packaging cost spectrum. Their bright, clean aesthetic and print versatility make them the material of choice for specific industries:

Luxury Retail

High-end boutiques and department stores use 300g+ white card for its substantial hand-feel and premium rigidity.

Cosmetics & Beauty

Brands demand flawless color accuracy for product photography and brand color matching on white card.

Premium Gifting

Corporate gift bags and seasonal packaging benefit from the clean, upscale presentation of white cardboard.

06
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between white card and white kraft paper?

White card (210–350 GSM) is a rigid paperboard made from bleached chemical pulp, designed for structured bags. White kraft (60–150 GSM) is a flexible paper made from bleached kraft pulp, used for softer shopping bags.

Can I print full-color photos on white card bags?

Yes. White card offers the best substrate for photographic printing. Use offset lithography or digital printing for full-color, high-resolution artwork reproduction.

Is white card recyclable?

Yes, uncoated or water-based coated white card is fully recyclable and biodegradable. However, if the bag has BOPP lamination or foil stamping, recyclability depends on local facility capabilities.

What GSM should I choose for a luxury cosmetics bag?

For luxury cosmetics, 250–300 GSM white card is the industry standard. It provides adequate rigidity for small-format bags while supporting premium finishes like matte lamination and hot stamping.

Does white card yellow over time?

High-quality white card with proper bleaching and UV-resistant coatings maintains brightness for 2–3 years under normal storage. Avoid direct sunlight and high humidity to prevent yellowing.
