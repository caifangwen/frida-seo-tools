Material Deep Dive

Virgin Kraft Paper

What It Is

Virgin kraft paper is made from fresh wood pulp (typically softwood pine) using the kraft pulping process. It has a natural brown color and distinctive fibrous texture that consumers associate with authenticity and environmental responsibility. The long fibers from softwood trees give kraft paper its exceptional strength, making it the go-to choice for applications requiring durability without excessive weight.

Tensile strength High — excellent tear and burst resistance

Virgin Kraft Paper: Pros and Cons

Evaluate whether this material fits your packaging needs

Advantages

  • Highest strength-to-weight ratio — thin but strong
  • Natural, eco-friendly aesthetic — consumers associate brown kraft with sustainability
  • Cost-effective — cheaper than white cardboard and specialty papers
  • FSC-certified options widely available

Limitations

  • Limited print vibrancy (brown background mutates colors)
  • Natural color variation between batches
  • Not ideal for high-end luxury branding

Typical Applications

Clothing, accessories, and general retail bags with custom handles

Premium Packaging

White Cardboard (Duplex Board / SBS)

What It Is

White cardboard (also known as duplex board with white top or SBS — Solid Bleached Sulfate) is a thick, rigid paperboard with a bright white printing surface on one or both sides. This material is the gold standard for premium retail packaging, offering a pristine canvas for high-resolution printing and delivering a tactile experience that communicates quality to consumers.

Sustainable Choice

Recycled Kraft Paper

What It Is

Recycled kraft paper is manufactured from post-consumer waste (PCW) or pre-consumer recycled content, giving it a slightly speckled, earthy appearance that visually communicates environmental responsibility. Each production run has unique character due to the mixed fiber sources, making every batch subtly distinct — a feature many eco-conscious brands embrace as part of their sustainability story.

Recycled Kraft Paper: Pros and Cons

Best for brands with strong environmental messaging

Advantages

  • Highest sustainability credentials — supports circular economy
  • Lower cost than virgin kraft paper
  • FSC Recycled label — strongest eco-friendly marketing claim
  • Unique aesthetic — visible recycled fibers appeal to eco-conscious brands

Limitations

  • Lower strength than virgin kraft
  • Inconsistent color/texture between batches
  • Not suitable for high-end luxury branding
  • Limited print detail on speckled surface

Industry Insight: Recycled kraft paper typically costs 8-15% less than virgin kraft, while offering stronger sustainability claims. For brands targeting eco-conscious consumers, the visible recycled fibers serve as a visual proof point that resonates more than any marketing message.

High-Resolution Printing

Art Paper (Coated Paper)

What It Is

Art paper (also called coated paper or C1S/C2S art paper) has a clay coating that creates an ultra-smooth surface for high-resolution printing. This coating fills the natural pores of the paper, resulting in exceptional ink holdout and color fidelity — making it the preferred choice for photographic reproductions, detailed illustrations, and brand-critical color matching.

Art Paper: Pros and Cons

When print quality is your top priority

Advantages

  • Best-in-class print quality — photographic detail
  • Smooth, uniform surface — consistent color reproduction
  • Supports lamination — for water resistance and durability

Limitations

Typical Applications

  • Bookstore bags featuring cover art and author imagery
  • Exhibition and event bags with full-color promotional graphics

Food Safety

Greaseproof Paper

What It Is

Greaseproof paper is treated (chemically or with coating) to resist oil and grease penetration, making it suitable for direct food contact. This material is essential for bakery items, fried foods, and any application where oils would compromise standard paper integrity. Modern greaseproof treatments are increasingly PFAS-free to meet evolving regulatory requirements in the EU and US markets.

Moisture Protection

Laminated Paper

What It Is

Laminated paper bags have a thin plastic film (BOPP or PE) bonded to the surface, providing moisture resistance and enhanced visual appeal. The lamination process creates a protective barrier that extends bag life in humid environments while delivering a premium finish that elevates brand perception. However, this comes at the cost of recyclability — a trade-off that brands must carefully evaluate against their sustainability commitments.

Laminated Paper: Pros and Cons

When moisture protection is essential

Advantages

  • Moisture and grease resistance
  • Enhanced print vibrancy
  • Increased durability

Limitations

  • Reduced recyclability — plastic film complicates paper recycling
  • Not biodegradable — conflicts with sustainability claims
  • Higher cost

Pro Tip: If recyclability is a priority for your brand, consider water-based varnish instead of lamination. It provides moderate moisture resistance while maintaining full recyclability — a growing preference among eco-conscious retailers.

Common Questions

FAQ: Paper Bag Materials

What is the most popular material for paper shopping bags?

80–120 GSM virgin kraft paper is the most widely used material for retail shopping bags. It offers the best balance of strength, cost, and aesthetics. This weight range handles everyday retail loads (2–5 kg) without tearing, while maintaining the natural kraft appearance that consumers associate with quality and sustainability.

Is recycled paper as strong as virgin paper?

No. Recycled kraft paper is typically 10–20% weaker than virgin kraft due to shorter fiber length after recycling. Each recycling cycle shortens the cellulose fibers, reducing tensile strength. For heavy loads (over 5 kg), choose virgin kraft or increase the GSM of recycled paper to compensate for the strength difference.

Can I print full-color photos on kraft paper?

Not effectively. The brown background distorts colors, making accurate reproduction nearly impossible. For full-color printing, use white cardboard or art paper. On kraft paper, limit designs to 1–2 spot colors or use a white ink underlay (which increases cost and may still not achieve photo-quality results).

Is laminated paper bag recyclable?

Laminated bags with BOPP film are technically recyclable but require specialized facilities that can separate the plastic film from the paper substrate. Many curbside recycling programs don't accept them. If recyclability is a priority for your brand, choose water-based varnish instead of lamination — it provides moderate moisture protection while maintaining full recyclability.

What material do luxury brands use for paper bags?

Most luxury brands use 150–250 GSM white cardboard with matte lamination, hot stamping, and embossing. Some opt for specialty papers (textured, metallic) for limited editions or seasonal collections. The combination of thick, rigid stock with premium finishing techniques creates the unboxing experience that luxury consumers expect.

Is white kraft paper the same as white cardboard?

No. White kraft paper is bleached kraft pulp (60–150 GSM, flexible), retaining the strength characteristics of standard kraft paper with a white surface. White cardboard is a thicker, rigid paperboard (150–250 GSM) designed for structural bags that hold their shape. They serve different purposes: white kraft for strong, printable bags; white cardboard for premium, structured packaging.
