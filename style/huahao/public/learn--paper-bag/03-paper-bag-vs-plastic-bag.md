### End-of-Life: Disposal & Degradation

| Metric | Paper Bag | Plastic Bag (HDPE) |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| Biodegradation time | 2–6 months | 100–1,000 years |
| Recycling rate | 68–80% (curbside programs) | 5–10% (film plastic, not accepted everywhere) |

## When to Choose Paper Bags

✅ **Choose paper bags when:**
- You want to project a premium, eco-friendly brand image
- Your market has plastic bag bans or taxes
- You need high-quality printing for brand recognition
- Your products weigh 3–15 kg
- You prioritize end-of-life recyclability

**Ideal industries**: Fashion retail, cosmetics, jewelry, food & beverage, e-commerce, luxury goods.

---

## When Plastic Bags May Still Be Appropriate

⚠️ **Consider plastic bags when:**
- Moisture resistance is critical (wet/frozen products)
- Budget is extremely constrained
- Lightweight items (<1 kg) with no branding need
- Your market has no plastic regulations (shrinking list)

**Note**: Even in these cases, **laminated paper bags** or **water-based coated paper bags** can often serve as viable alternatives.

## FAQ: Paper Bag vs Plastic Bag

### Q1: Do paper bags really have a higher carbon footprint than plastic?
Yes, in production alone. But when you factor in **recyclability, biodegradability, and reuse**, the lifecycle impact of paper bags is **comparable or lower** than plastic bags over multiple use cycles.

### Q2: Can paper bags replace plastic bags for food packaging?
Yes, for dry foods (bread, pastries, snacks). For wet or oily foods, **laminated paper bags** or **greaseproof paper bags** are recommended.

### Q3: How much more do paper bags cost than plastic bags?
Typically **3–5× more per unit**, but the gap narrows with plastic taxes ($0.10–$0.50/bag) and the brand value premium.

### Q4: Are paper bags stronger than plastic bags?
For load-bearing, **yes**. A properly constructed kraft paper bag with twisted handles can hold 10–20 kg, compared to 3–8 kg for most plastic bags.

### Q5: Which bag type do consumers prefer?
A 2025 consumer survey found that **73% of shoppers** prefer retailers using eco-friendly paper bags over plastic, and 41% have switched brands due to packaging concerns.

### Q6: Can I transition from plastic to paper gradually?
Yes. Start with your **premium product lines** or **regulated markets** first, then expand. Many suppliers offer both options during the transition.
