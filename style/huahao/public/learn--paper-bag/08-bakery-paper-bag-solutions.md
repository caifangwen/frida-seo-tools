## Material Selection for Bakery Bags

### Greaseproof Paper

**What it is**: Paper treated with a food-safe coating that resists oil and grease penetration.

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| GSM range | 40–80 GSM |
| Grease resistance | Kit level 8–12 (highest) |
| Food safety | FDA 21 CFR §176.170 / EU 10/2011 compliant |
| PFAS status | Fluorine-free options available (recommended) |

**Best for**: Donuts, croissants, cookies, fried pastries, oily baked goods.

> ⚠️ **Important**: The EU and several US states are phasing out **PFAS-based greaseproof treatments**. Always specify **fluorine-free (PFAS-free)** greaseproof paper for future-proof compliance.

### Natural Kraft Paper (Untreated)

**What it is**: Untreated kraft paper with no grease resistance.

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| GSM range | 60–90 GSM |
| Grease resistance | None — oil will seep through |
| Best for | Dry bread, baguettes, crackers, non-oily items |

**Use case**: French baguettes, sourdough loaves, bread rolls — items that don't release oil.

### PE-Lined Kraft Paper

**What it is**: Kraft paper with a thin PE (polyethylene) film laminated on the inner surface.

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| GSM range | 70–100 GSM (paper) + 10–15 micron PE |
| Grease resistance | Excellent (complete barrier) |
| Moisture resistance | High |
| Recyclability | Reduced (PE layer must be separated) |

**Best for**: Moist or oily products where maximum protection is needed.

## Food Safety & Certification Requirements

### Essential Certifications for Bakery Paper Bags

| Certification | Requirement | Region |
|--------------|------------|--------|
| **FDA 21 CFR §176.170** | Paper components for food contact | United States |
| **EU Regulation 10/2011** | Food contact materials | European Union |
| **LFGB (Germany)** | Food and feed code | Germany, EU |
| **ISO 22000** | Food safety management | Global |
| **BRC Packaging** | Packaging certification for food | UK, EU, Global |
| **FSC** | Sustainably sourced paper | Global (recommended) |

### Key Compliance Checklist

- ✅ Paper mill provides **food-grade declaration of compliance (DoC)**
- ✅ Inks are **food-safe** (low migration, heavy-metal free)
- ✅ Greaseproof treatment is **PFAS-free** (if required by your market)
- ✅ Manufacturing facility follows **hygienic production practices**
- ✅ **Migration testing** completed (ensures no chemical transfer to food)

## Printing & Branding for Bakery Bags

### Recommended Print Methods

| Print Method | Best For | Colors | Cost |
|-------------|---------|--------|------|
| **Flexographic** | Simple logos, high volume | 1–2 colors | $ |
| **Offset** | Detailed artwork, brand colors | 4+ colors (CMYK) | $$ |
| **Water-based ink** | Eco-friendly positioning | 1–3 colors | $$ |

### Design Tips for Bakery Bags

- ✅ **Large, legible logo** — visible from across the street
- ✅ **Warm color palette** — browns, golds, and creams evoke baked goods
- ✅ **Include contact info** — address, phone, website, social media handle
- ✅ **Add a tagline** — "Freshly Baked Daily" or "Since 1985"
- ✅ **QR code** — links to online menu, loyalty program, or Instagram
- ❌ **Avoid full-bleed dark ink** — makes bags look heavy and hides the natural kraft aesthetic

## Common Bakery Bag Mistakes & Solutions

| Mistake | Problem | Solution |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Using untreated kraft for oily pastries | Grease stains customer's hands | Switch to greaseproof or PE-lined paper |
| GSM too low (under 50) | Bags tear when holding multiple items | Use minimum 60 GSM for multi-item bags |
| No food-grade certification | Regulatory risk, customer safety concern | Require FDA/EU food-contact DoC from supplier |
| PFAS-based greaseproof paper | Being banned in EU/US states | Switch to fluorine-free (PFAS-free) treatment |
| Over-designed printing | Hides natural kraft aesthetic, high cost | Keep it simple: 1–2 colors on natural kraft |
| Wrong bag size | Products crushed or too much empty space | Match bag size to product (see size guide above) |

## FAQ: Bakery Paper Bags

### Q1: What is the best material for bakery bags?
For oily pastries (donuts, croissants): **greaseproof paper (PFAS-free)**, 60–80 GSM. For dry bread (baguettes, rolls): **natural kraft paper**, 60–90 GSM.

### Q2: Are greaseproof paper bags safe for direct food contact?
Yes, if they comply with **FDA 21 CFR §176.170** or **EU Regulation 10/2011**. Always request a **Declaration of Compliance (DoC)** from your supplier.

### Q3: What is SOS bag, and why do bakeries use it?
An **SOS (Self-Opening Sack)** opens automatically when pulled apart, enabling fast packing. It's the **most efficient bag for high-volume bakeries** packing dozens of orders per hour.

### Q4: Can I print my bakery logo on greaseproof paper bags?
Yes. Flexographic printing with **food-safe, water-based inks** works well on greaseproof paper. Limit to 1–2 colors for best results.

### Q5: How much do custom printed bakery bags cost?
- **Plain greaseproof SOS bags**: $0.04–$0.07/bag (30,000 pcs)
- **1-color printed greaseproof bags**: $0.05–$0.09/bag (30,000 pcs)
- **Custom printed twisted handle bags**: $0.10–$0.18/bag (10,000 pcs)

### Q6: How long do bakery paper bags last in storage?
Stored in a cool, dry place, paper bags have a **shelf life of 1–2 years**. Greaseproof coatings may degrade slightly over time, so use within 12 months for best performance.
