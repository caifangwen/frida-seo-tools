## Optimal Sizes for Clothing Store Bags

### Standard Size Reference

| Size | Dimensions (W×G×H) | Fits | Typical Garments |
|------|-------------------|------|-----------------|
| Small | 210×110×260mm | 1–2 items | T-shirt, blouse, accessories |
| Medium | 300×120×400mm | 2–4 items | Shirt + pants, or dress |
| Large | 350×130×430mm | 4–6 items | Multiple garments, shoes |
| Extra Large | 400×150×480mm | 6–10 items | Bulk purchases, seasonal shopping |
| XL (Coat/Overcoat) | 450×150×550mm | 1–2 heavy items | Coats, suits, overalls |

### Pro Tips for Sizing

- ✅ **Measure your largest garment** (folded) and add 30–50mm clearance
- ✅ **Order 2–3 sizes** to match different purchase volumes
- ✅ **Include a shoe box?** Add 50mm to height and depth
- ✅ **When in doubt, go larger** — customers prefer roomy bags

## Load Capacity & Strength Testing

A quality clothing store paper bag should pass these tests:

| Test | Standard | Pass Criteria |
|------|---------|--------------|
| **Handle pull test** | 80–150 N minimum | No handle detachment or tearing |
| **Static load test** | Hold 8 kg for 30 minutes | No bottom seam failure |
| **Drop test** | 1m drop with 5 kg load | No rupture or handle break |
| **Wet strength test** | Light rain simulation | Surface doesn't disintegrate |

### How GSM Affects Load Capacity

| GSM | Approximate Load Capacity (Twisted Handle) |
|-----|-------------------------------------------|
| 80 | 3–5 kg |
| 100 | 5–8 kg |
| 120 | 8–12 kg |
| 150 | 12–18 kg |

**Recommendation**: For a medium clothing store bag (300×120×400mm) carrying shirts, pants, and light jackets, **120 GSM kraft paper** with twisted handle is the sweet spot.

## Design Best Practices for Clothing Store Bags

### Logo Placement

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| Center front (single side) | Clean, minimalist | Less brand exposure |
| Both sides | Maximum visibility | Higher print cost |
| Inside (visible when open) | Surprise element | Limited exposure |
| Side gusset | Unique, subtle | Easy to miss |

**Best practice**: Print logo on **both sides** for maximum brand exposure. The marginal cost increase (+$0.01–$0.02/bag) is worth the doubled visibility.

### Color Strategy

- **Dark logo on kraft**: Natural, eco-friendly vibe
- **White/cream logo on kraft**: Subtle, premium feel
- **Full-color on white cardboard**: Vibrant, brand-accurate
- **Hot stamp gold/silver**: Luxury positioning

### Typography Tips

- Use **bold, simple fonts** — fine details get lost on kraft texture
- Keep logo size **at least 80mm wide** for visibility at 2 meters
- Avoid gradients on kraft paper — they print unevenly

### Interior Branding

Don't neglect the inside of the bag:

- Printed brand message or slogan inside
- Custom colored interior (white or brand color)
- QR code linking to loyalty program or social media
- Care instructions or brand story

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Consequence | Solution |
|---------|------------|----------|
| GSM too low (under 80) | Bags tear, handles rip | Use minimum 100 GSM for clothing |
| Handle not reinforced | Handles detach with heavy loads | Add inner patch reinforcement |
| Too many print colors on kraft | Muddy, unclear printing | Limit to 1–2 colors on kraft; use white cardboard for full color |
| Bag too small | Customers can't fit purchases | Offer 3 sizes: S, M, L |
| No branding inside | Missed engagement opportunity | Add interior print or QR code |
| Ignuring seasonality | Summer bags too heavy, winter bags too weak | Adjust GSM by season (lighter in summer) |

## FAQ: Paper Bags for Clothing Stores

### Q1: What is the most popular size for clothing store paper bags?
**300×120×400mm (Medium)** is the most commonly ordered size. It fits 2–4 garments comfortably and handles most typical purchases.

### Q2: Should I use kraft paper or white cardboard for my fashion brand?
- **Kraft paper**: Eco-friendly, casual to mid-range brands, cost-effective
- **White cardboard**: Premium, designer brands, best print quality
Choose based on your **brand positioning and price point**.

### Q3: How much should I budget for clothing store paper bags?
- **Budget**: $0.05–$0.10/bag (recycled kraft, 1-color, 30,000+ pcs)
- **Standard**: $0.10–$0.20/bag (virgin kraft, 2-color, 10,000 pcs)
- **Premium**: $0.25–$0.50/bag (white cardboard, full-color, luxury finishes, 5,000 pcs)

### Q4: Can I order bags with my brand's custom color?
Yes. Provide your **PMS (Pantone) color number**, and your manufacturer can match it. Expect a +5–10% cost premium for PMS ink.

### Q5: Do I need FSC-certified paper for clothing bags?
Not mandatory, but **highly recommended** for EU/UK markets and eco-conscious brands. It adds only 5–10% to the cost and strengthens your sustainability messaging.

### Q6: How many bags should I order?
Order enough for **3–4 months of sales** to avoid frequent reorders. A store with 500 transactions/month needs ~1,500–2,000 bags per quarter (accounting for multiple bags per transaction).
