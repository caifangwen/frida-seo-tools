Kraft Paper Bags Guide: Brown, White & Natural Kraft for B2B Buyers

From sulfate pulp origins to GSM selection and environmental appeal — everything you need to source kraft paper bags with confidence.

Kraft paper bags are the backbone of the sustainable packaging industry. Made from unbleached or bleached wood pulp using the kraft process, these bags offer an unmatched balance of strength, affordability, and eco-friendly credentials. Whether you're sourcing brown paper bags for a grocery chain or white kraft totes for a boutique brand, understanding kraft's properties is essential for making the right procurement decision.

01
Manufacturing

Kraft Paper Origins: The Sulfate Process

The term "kraft" comes from the German word for strength. Kraft paper is produced using the sulfate (kraft) pulping process, which treats wood chips with a mixture of sodium hydroxide and sodium sulfide. This chemical treatment breaks down lignin — the natural glue that holds wood fibers together — while preserving the long, strong cellulose fibers that give kraft its characteristic durability.

Strong Fibers

The sulfate process preserves long cellulose fibers, delivering superior tear and burst resistance compared to other pulping methods.

Unbleached Pulp

Natural brown kraft skips the bleaching stage, reducing chemical use and preserving the earthy aesthetic consumers associate with sustainability.

High Yield

The kraft process achieves near 95% fiber recovery, making it one of the most efficient pulping technologies available.

⚠ Print Limitation on Brown Kraft

The brown background absorbs and distorts light colors. Bright reds, yellows, and pastels will appear muddy. For brown kraft, limit designs to 1–2 dark spot colors (black, dark green, navy) or use a white ink underlay before printing colors.

Sustainability

Environmental Appeal: Biodegradable, Recyclable, Compostable

Kraft paper's environmental credentials are among the strongest in the packaging world. For B2B buyers navigating plastic bans and ESG reporting requirements, kraft offers clear compliance advantages:

  • Biodegradable: Unbleached kraft decomposes in 2–6 months in natural environments
  • Recyclable: Widely accepted in curbside paper recycling programs globally
  • Compostable: Non-laminated kraft meets home and industrial composting standards (EN 13432, ASTM D6400)
  • Renewable source: FSC-certified kraft ensures responsible forest management

Print Guide

Print Suitability: Bold Inks on Brown

While brown kraft's natural texture limits full-color printing, it excels with bold, dark inks that create high contrast against the earthy background. Flexographic printing with water-based inks is the most common and cost-effective method for kraft bags.

Recommended on Brown Kraft

  • Black logos and typography
  • Dark green, navy blue, burgundy
  • Metallic foil stamping (gold, silver, copper)
  • Single-color flexo prints

Avoid on Brown Kraft

  • Light pastels and pale yellows
  • Photographic CMYK images
  • Fine halftone gradients
  • Small text below 6pt

06
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is brown kraft paper stronger than white kraft?

Yes, slightly. The bleaching process in white kraft weakens fibers marginally. Natural brown kraft retains maximum fiber length and delivers 5–10% higher tear resistance at the same GSM.

What is the most popular GSM for shopping bags?

120–150 GSM is the industry standard for twisted-handle shopping bags. It offers the optimal balance of strength, cost, and foldability for retail applications.

Can kraft paper bags hold heavy items?

Yes. With 200+ GSM kraft and reinforced handles (patch or cotton rope), kraft bags can reliably carry 15–20 kg. For very heavy loads, consider double-wall construction or adding a cardboard base insert.

Are kraft bags water-resistant?

Standard kraft absorbs moisture. For water resistance, specify BOPP lamination (gloss or matte) or a water-based repellent coating. Note that lamination reduces recyclability in some regions.

What is the MOQ for custom printed kraft bags?

Most manufacturers require 10,000–30,000 pcs for custom flexo printing. Stock unprinted kraft bags may be available from 1,000 pcs with limited size options.
