Bag Structures

Single Art Paper Bags: Efficient, Versatile & Cost-Effective

A complete buyer's guide to single-wall paper bag construction, from folding methods and strength limits to common sizes and handle pairings.

The single art paper bag is the workhorse of the retail packaging world. When buyers need a single wall paper bag that balances material efficiency with acceptable performance, this construction method delivers. Also described as simple paper bag construction or the standard paper tote, the single-wall design uses one continuous sheet of paper folded and glued into a three-dimensional container. Understanding its limits and optimal pairings helps procurement teams avoid over-specifying—or under-engineering—their packaging.

01
Engineering

Construction Method

A single-wall bag begins as a rectangular sheet of art paper or kraft paper. The sheet is printed, then passed through a series of scoring and folding stations that create the bag's faces, sides, and bottom. Glue is applied at the side seam and bottom folds, and the top edge is typically turned over to form a reinforced hem for handle attachment.

Sheet Formation

One piece of paper is cut to size. For a standard tote, the width equals (2 × face width) + (2 × gusset) + glue flap.

Side Seam

An overlap or butt seam is glued with PVA or hot melt. Overlap seams add strength but create a slight ridge.

Bottom Fold

The base is folded into a rectangular or hexagonal bottom and glued. No separate base card is inserted unless specified.

Because there is only one layer of paper, the bag's structural integrity depends entirely on the grammage (gsm) and fiber quality of that single sheet. Common choices range from 128 gsm to 250 gsm coated art paper, or 150 gsm to 300 gsm kraft.

02
Performance

Strength Limitations

Single-wall bags are best for light items under 2 kg. Above this threshold, the paper walls begin to bow, the bottom folds stress, and handle attachment points become vulnerable to tear-out. The exact limit depends on paper grammage and handle type.

Paper Grammage Max Recommended Load Typical Contents Handle Compatibility
128–157 gsm0.5–1.0 kgJewelry, cosmetics samplesDie-cut, ribbon
180–210 gsm1.0–1.5 kgApparel accessories, small giftsTwisted paper, ribbon
230–250 gsm1.5–2.0 kgLight clothing, books, packaged foodTwisted paper, cotton rope
300 gsm kraft2.0–3.0 kgShoes, heavier retailCotton rope with reinforcement

For loads above 2 kg, upgrading to a double-wall structure is usually more cost-effective than simply increasing paper weight, because double-wall improves both stiffness and handle attachment strength simultaneously.

Load Distribution Tip

Even within the 2 kg limit, concentrate weight toward the bottom center of the bag. Off-center loads create torsional stress that single-wall construction resists poorly.

03
Economics

Cost Efficiency

Single-wall construction minimizes material waste. There is no inner liner, no secondary sheet, and no complex lamination. For high-volume orders, this simplicity translates directly to lower unit costs and faster production speeds.

30–40%lower material cost vs double-wall
15–25%faster production throughput
< 2 kgoptimal load range for value

For promotional events, trade shows, and retail chains distributing bags at scale, the single-wall format offers the best cost-per-impression ratio. When the bag's primary job is brand visibility rather than heavy transport, over-engineering is wasted budget.

05
Pairings

Handle Options

Single-wall bags accommodate the full range of handle types, but the match should respect the bag's load limits:

  • Die-cut handles: No additional material cost. Integrated into the bag body. Best for light items <1 kg and short carries. Can be uncomfortable under load.
  • Twisted paper rope: The eco-standard. Fully recyclable. Best for 1–2 kg loads in retail and grocery. See twisted paper rope handles.
  • Cotton rope: Premium feel for fashion and cosmetics. Requires reinforcement patch on single-wall to prevent tear-out. See cotton rope handles.
  • Ribbon: Luxury positioning. Ideal for gift and beauty. Load limit ~1.5 kg. See satin ribbon handles.
Q: Can single-wall bags be printed with full-color graphics?

A: Absolutely. Single-wall art paper accepts offset, screen, and digital printing beautifully. For maximum color impact, specify coated art paper with a matte or gloss lamination. See printing methods.

Q: Are single-wall bags recyclable?

A: Yes, if made from uncoated kraft or art paper without plastic lamination. Bags with PP lamination require separation at specialized facilities. Request FSC-certified paper for verified sustainability.

Q: When should I switch from single-wall to double-wall?

A: Upgrade when your typical load exceeds 2 kg, when bag reuse is a brand goal, or when you need a heavier, more luxurious hand feel. See double-wall paper bags.
