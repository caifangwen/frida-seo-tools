## Key Features for Restaurant Takeout Bags

### 1. Grease Resistance

**Why it matters**: Oily foods (burgers, fried chicken, stir-fry) will soak through untreated paper, staining the bag and the customer's hands.

| Solution | Grease Resistance | Recyclability | Cost |
|----------|------------------|--------------|------|
| Greaseproof paper (PFAS-free) | ★★★★ | High | +5–10% |
| PE inner lining | ★★★★★ | Reduced | +10–15% |
| Water-based grease-resistant coating | ★★★☆ | High | +5–10% |
| Untreated kraft paper | ★☆☆☆ | Highest | Baseline |

**Recommendation**: Use **PFAS-free greaseproof paper** for most applications. Reserve PE lining for very oily or saucy foods.

> ⚠️ **PFAS alert**: The EU, California, New York, and other jurisdictions are banning PFAS-based food packaging. Always specify **fluorine-free** greaseproof treatment.

### 2. Heat Retention

Paper bags naturally insulate better than thin plastic bags. Boost heat retention with:

- **Double-layer construction**: Two layers of kraft paper trap air
- **Aluminum foil inner lining**: Reflects heat (premium option)
- **Thicker GSM**: 120+ GSM retains heat longer than 60–80 GSM
- **Folded top closure**: Traps warm air inside

**Heat retention test data** (ambient 20°C, initial food temp 65°C):

| Bag Type | Temp After 15 min | Temp After 30 min |
|----------|------------------|-------------------|
| Thin plastic bag | 52°C | 42°C |
| 80 GSM kraft (untreated) | 55°C | 45°C |
| 120 GSM kraft + folded top | 58°C | 50°C |
| Double-layer kraft | 60°C | 53°C |

### 3. Secure Closure & Tamper Evidence

| Closure Type | Security Level | Tamper-Evident | Cost |
|-------------|--------------|----------------|------|
| Folded top (no seal) | Low | No | $0 |
| Sticker seal | Medium | Yes (sticker breaks) | +$0.01/bag |
| Adhesive strip (self-seal) | High | Yes | +$0.02/bag |
| Staple | Medium | Partial | +$0.005/bag |
| String tie | Medium | Yes (untied = obvious) | +$0.01/bag |

**Recommendation**: Use **branded sticker seals** for tamper evidence and additional brand exposure. Custom printed stickers cost ~$0.01–$0.02 each.

### 4. Handle Strength for Delivery

Delivery drivers carry multiple bags at once. Handles must withstand:

- **Combined weight** of 2–4 bags (up to 15–20 kg total)
- **Repeated lifting** from car seats, door handles, doorbells
- **Weather exposure** (rain, humidity)

| Handle Type | Load Capacity | Weather Resistance | Best For |
|------------|--------------|-------------------|----------|
| Twisted paper rope | 5–12 kg | Moderate (weakens when wet) | General takeout |
| Patch handle (reinforced) | 10–20 kg | Good | Heavy orders, multi-item |
| Flat paper handle | 3–6 kg | Moderate | Light orders, single items |
| Die-cut handle | 2–4 kg | Good (integrated) | Small orders |

## Food Safety & Compliance for Takeout Bags

### Required Standards

| Standard | Description | Region |
|----------|------------|--------|
| **FDA 21 CFR §176.170** | Paper components for food contact | United States |
| **EU 10/2011** | Food contact materials regulation | European Union |
| **ISO 22000** | Food safety management system | Global |
| **HACCP** | Hazard analysis and critical control points | Global |

### Checklist for Safe Takeout Packaging

- ✅ Food-grade certified paper (request DoC from supplier)
- ✅ PFAS-free greaseproof treatment
- ✅ Food-safe inks (low migration, heavy-metal free)
- ✅ Tamper-evident closure
- ✅ Clear allergen/ingredient labeling on attached card or sticker
- ✅ "Best consumed within" time recommendation printed on bag

## FAQ: Restaurant Takeout Paper Bags

### Q1: What is the best bag type for restaurant takeout?
For most restaurants, a **100 GSM twisted handle kraft bag with greaseproof inner lining** offers the best balance of cost, protection, and brandability.

### Q2: Are paper takeout bags safe for hot food?
Yes. Paper bags can handle food temperatures up to **100°C**. For extremely hot items (soups, hot pot), use a **double-layer bag** or include a heat-resistant inner container.

### Q3: How do I prevent grease from soaking through the bag?
Use **greaseproof paper (PFAS-free)** or **PE-lined kraft paper**. Both provide effective barriers against oil penetration.

### Q4: Should I seal takeout bags?
Yes. A **tamper-evident seal** (sticker or adhesive strip) assures customers their food hasn't been opened during transit. It's increasingly expected by delivery platform customers.

### Q5: Can I use paper bags for beverage delivery (coffee, smoothies)?
Yes. **Die-cut cup carrier bags** or **square bottom bags** with cup inserts work well. Ensure the bag is tall enough to prevent tipping.

### Q6: How much should restaurants budget for takeout bags?
- **Budget**: $0.04–$0.08/bag (SOS, plain or 1-color, 30,000+ pcs)
- **Standard**: $0.10–$0.16/bag (twisted handle, greaseproof inner, 10,000 pcs)
- **Premium**: $0.25–$0.40/bag (patch handle, white cardboard, full branding, 5,000 pcs)
