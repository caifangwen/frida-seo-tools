Sizing Guide

Optimal Sizes for Clothing Store Bags

Getting the size right is one of the most common challenges for clothing retailers. A bag that's too small frustrates customers and risks garment damage. A bag that's too large wastes material and looks sloppy. The key is matching your bag dimensions to your actual product mix, not arbitrary standards.

Size Dimensions (W×G×H) Fits Typical Garments
Small 210×110×260mm 1–2 items T-shirt, blouse, accessories
Medium 300×120×400mm 2–4 items Shirt + pants, or dress
Large 350×130×430mm 4–6 items Multiple garments, shoes
Extra Large 400×150×480mm 6–10 items Bulk purchases, seasonal shopping
XL (Coat/Overcoat) 450×150×550mm 1–2 heavy items Coats, suits, overalls
Table 2: Standard paper bag sizes for clothing stores
Sizing Rule #1
Add 30–50mm Clearance
Measure your largest garment (folded) and add clearance for easy insertion and removal.
Sizing Rule #2
Offer 2–3 Sizes
Match different purchase volumes — single items, multi-item purchases, and seasonal hauls.
Sizing Rule #3
Account for Shoe Boxes
Add 50mm to height and depth if customers frequently buy footwear alongside apparel.
Sizing Rule #4
Go Larger When Unsure
Customers prefer roomy bags that don't feel overpacked — it signals generosity and care.

The 300×120×400mm (Medium) size is the most commonly ordered dimension for clothing store paper bags. It accommodates the vast majority of typical purchases — a shirt and pants combination, a single dress, or a light jacket with accessories. However, relying on a single size creates problems: small purchases rattle around in medium bags, while bulk buyers need multiple bags or struggle with overfilled ones.

Performance Testing

Load Capacity & Strength Testing

Understanding load capacity is non-negotiable for clothing retailers. Unlike lightweight retail items, garments — especially denim, coats, and shoes — can be surprisingly heavy. A quality clothing store paper bag must pass standardized strength tests to ensure customer safety and protect your brand reputation.

Test Standard Pass Criteria
Handle Pull Test 80–150 N minimum No handle detachment or tearing
Static Load Test Hold 8 kg for 30 minutes No bottom seam failure
Drop Test 1m drop with 5 kg load No rupture or handle break
Wet Strength Test Light rain simulation Surface doesn't disintegrate
Table 6: Paper bag strength testing standards for clothing retail

The relationship between GSM and load capacity is direct but not always intuitive. Thicker paper doesn't always mean proportionally stronger bags — the handle attachment method, bottom seal quality, and paper fiber orientation all matter. Here's a practical reference for twisted handle bags:

Load Capacity by GSM (Twisted Handle Bags)
80 GSM
3–5 kg
100 GSM
5–8 kg
120 GSM
8–12 kg
150 GSM
12–18 kg

Recommendation: For a medium clothing store bag (300×120×400mm) carrying shirts, pants, and light jackets, 120 GSM kraft paper with twisted handle represents the optimal balance of strength, cost, and customer experience.

When testing potential suppliers, request sample bags and perform the static load test yourself: fill the bag with 8 kg of comparable weight items and let it sit for 30 minutes. Check for seam separation, handle stretch, or bottom bulge. Any of these indicate insufficient construction for your use case.

Design Best Practices for Clothing Store Bags

Your bag's visual design is where brand strategy meets physical reality. Good design doesn't just look appealing — it drives recognition, recall, and ultimately, repeat purchases. Below are evidence-based recommendations for maximizing the branding impact of your clothing store paper bags.

Table 7: Logo placement options for clothing store bags

Best Practice: Print your logo on both sides for maximum brand exposure. The marginal cost increase of $0.01–$0.02 per bag delivers doubled visibility as customers naturally rotate bags while walking — a small investment for a permanent walking billboard.

Color Strategy

  • Dark logo on kraft: Creates a natural, eco-friendly vibe that resonates with sustainability-focused consumers
  • White/cream logo on kraft: Delivers a subtle, premium feel without the cost of full-color printing
  • Full-color on white cardboard: Achieves vibrant, brand-accurate reproduction for labels with complex color palettes
  • Hot stamp gold/silver: Signals luxury positioning and justifies premium price points

Typography & Visibility Tips

Typography choices matter more than most retailers realize. The textured surface of kraft paper, in particular, can render fine details illegible. Use bold, simple fonts with clear letterforms — avoid thin serifs or script fonts that blur on kraft texture. Keep your logo at least 80mm wide for visibility at a 2-meter distance, which is the typical viewing range in retail environments.

Avoid gradients on kraft paper — they print unevenly due to the paper's natural texture and absorption rate. If your brand uses gradient effects, reserve them for white cardboard or laminated options where the smooth surface ensures consistent ink laydown.

Risk Prevention

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Learning from others' mistakes saves time, money, and customer frustration. These are the most frequent errors we see clothing retailers make with their paper bag specifications — and how to prevent them.

Mistakes & Solutions

Common pitfalls in clothing retail paper bag selection

What to Avoid

  • GSM too low (under 80) — bags tear under normal loads
  • Handles not reinforced — detach with heavy garments
  • Too many print colors on kraft — muddy, unclear results
  • Only one bag size — frustrates diverse purchase volumes
  • No interior branding — missed engagement opportunity
  • Ignoring seasonality — one GSM doesn't fit all seasons

How to Fix It

  • Use minimum 100 GSM for clothing; 120 GSM for mixed loads
  • Add inner patch reinforcement to prevent handle detachment
  • Limit to 1–2 colors on kraft; use white cardboard for full-color
  • Offer 3 sizes: Small, Medium, Large to match purchase patterns
  • Add interior print or QR code to drive loyalty engagement
  • Adjust GSM by season — lighter in summer, heavier in winter for coats

The most costly mistake is underspecifying GSM to save $0.02–$0.03 per bag. When a handle tears and a customer drops their purchase on the sidewalk, the damage to your brand reputation far exceeds any per-unit savings. Invest in adequate strength from the start.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most popular size for clothing store paper bags?

300×120×400mm (Medium) is the most commonly ordered size for clothing store paper bags. It fits 2–4 garments comfortably — typically a shirt and pants combination, or a single dress — and handles the vast majority of typical retail purchases. For stores with significant footwear sales, we recommend also stocking the Large size (350×130×430mm) to accommodate shoe boxes.

Should I use kraft paper or white cardboard for my fashion brand?

The choice depends on your brand positioning and price point. Kraft paper is eco-friendly, cost-effective, and well-suited for casual to mid-range brands that want to communicate sustainability and approachability. White cardboard offers premium aesthetics, superior print quality, and is the standard for designer brands and luxury labels. If your average garment price exceeds $80, white cardboard is usually the better investment.

How much should I budget for clothing store paper bags?

Budget tiers vary by material, print complexity, and order volume: Budget: $0.05–$0.10 per bag (recycled kraft, 1-color print, 30,000+ pieces). Standard: $0.10–$0.20 per bag (virgin kraft, 2-color print, 10,000 pieces). Premium: $0.25–$0.50 per bag (white cardboard, full-color printing, luxury finishes like hot stamping or spot UV, 5,000 pieces). Most mid-range clothing retailers find the Standard tier delivers the best balance of quality and cost.

Can I order bags with my brand's custom color?

Yes. Provide your PMS (Pantone) color number to your manufacturer, and they can match it precisely using PMS ink. Expect a 5–10% cost premium for PMS ink compared to standard CMYK process colors. For brands with strict color guidelines — particularly luxury labels — PMS matching is essential for maintaining brand consistency across all touchpoints.

Do I need FSC-certified paper for clothing bags?

FSC certification is not legally mandatory but is highly recommended for EU/UK markets and eco-conscious brands. It typically adds only 5–10% to the cost and significantly strengthens your sustainability messaging. Many commercial landlords and retail distributors now require FSC documentation as part of their leasing or vendor agreements. If your target market includes environmentally aware consumers, FSC certification is a small investment with outsized credibility returns.

How many bags should I order?

Order enough for 3–4 months of sales to avoid frequent reorders and potential stockouts. A clothing store with 500 transactions per month typically needs 1,500–2,000 bags per quarter, accounting for the fact that many purchases require multiple bags (for example, a customer buying clothes and shoes may need 2–3 bags). Always round up rather than down — storage costs for paper bags are minimal compared to the disruption of running out.
