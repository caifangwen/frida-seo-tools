Paper Material Types: The Five Core Substrates

Paper bags are built from five primary substrate families. Each offers a distinct balance of print quality, strength, cost, and environmental positioning.

White Card Paper

Bleached chemical pulp board (210–350g) with bright white surface. Best for full-color printing, luxury retail, and cosmetics. Superior print base but mid-to-high cost.

Kraft Paper

Natural brown or bleached white sulfate pulp (100–350g). The workhorse of sustainable packaging — strong, recyclable, and cost-effective. Limited print colors on brown.

Art Paper (Coated)

Clay-coated C1S/C2S paper (200–250g) delivering the highest color reproduction. Requires lamination for bag durability. Ideal for photo-heavy brand graphics.

Specialty Texture Paper

Linen, leather grain, felt, and wood grain textures created at the mill level. Delivers tactile brand differentiation at premium cost with higher MOQ.

Black Card

Dyed-through or surface-coated black board for stealth luxury. Demands foil stamping, screen white, or spot UV for visible branding. Soft-touch lamination is essential.

GSM & Weight Selection: 200g to 350g+

GSM (grams per square meter) is the primary specification that determines bag thickness, stiffness, and load capacity. But GSM is not the whole story — fiber composition, calendering, and construction method all influence real-world performance.

Our detailed GSM selection guide breaks down each tier:

1
200g–230g: Lightweight

Economy bags for short-term use. Best for promotions, events, and giveaways under 2 kg.

2
250g: Standard Retail

The most popular tier for fashion, cosmetics, and general retail. Balanced cost and rigidity for 3–7 kg loads.

3
300g: Premium Feel

Box-like rigidity for luxury brands. Supports heavy lamination, foil, and embossing without substrate stress.

4
350g+: Ultra-Luxury

Maximum stiffness for high-value gifting and heavy items. Requires pre-scoring to prevent fold cracking.

💡 Key Insight

Cost does not increase linearly with GSM. A 350g bag costs 60–100% more than a 200g bag — not 75% — because thicker stocks slow production, require specialized scoring, and have lower yield per ton.

Recycled & Sustainable Paper Options

Sustainability is no longer optional in B2B packaging. Buyers must navigate recycled content levels, fiber strength trade-offs, and certification requirements to meet both regulatory demands and consumer expectations.

Our recycled content guide explains:

PCR Content Levels

30%, 50%, and 100% post-consumer recycled options — each with distinct visual and physical properties.

Strength Trade-Offs

Shorter recycled fibers reduce tear resistance by 10–20%. Compensate with higher GSM or virgin kraft reinforcement.

Certifications

FSC Recycled, Green Seal, and other third-party validations for marketing claims and compliance.
