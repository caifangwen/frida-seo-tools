01
Process

Manufacturing Method

Thermal bonding (also called heat sealing or hot calendering) consolidates a fiber web by melting thermoplastic fibers at contact points. The process involves:

1
Web Formation

A carded web of 100% thermoplastic fibers is laid onto a conveyor.

2
Calendering

The web passes between two heated rollers at 120–160°C.

3
Melting & Fusion

Fibers at contact points melt and fuse together.

4
Cooling

The bonded web is cooled, locking fused points in place.

03
Limitations

Durability Limitations

⚠️ Weaknesses

  • Trapezoidal tear: 8–15 N (vs 20–35 N spunbond)
  • Abrasion: 50–100 cycles (vs 200–500)
  • Heat sensitivity: softens at 80–100°C
  • Limited reuse: 15–25 cycles (light loads)

✅ Still Works For

  • Single-day events & conferences
  • Ultra-budget promotions
  • Lightweight retail (< 3 kg)
  • Election & awareness campaigns
