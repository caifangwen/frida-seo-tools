02
Print

Screen Printing: The Economical Workhorse

Screen printing dominates nonwoven bag decoration because it delivers opaque, vibrant colors at the lowest per-unit cost for medium-to-large runs. Practical color limit is 4–6 colors.

→ Deep dive: Screen Printing Guide

03
Print

Heat Transfer: Flexibility Meets Color

When designs include photographs, complex gradients, or more than four colors, heat transfer becomes the logical choice. No color limit—a 100-color image costs the same as a two-color logo.

→ Deep dive: Heat Transfer Printing Guide

04
Print

Laminated CMYK: Magazine Quality

For brand imagery demanding photographic reproduction, laminated CMYK printing is the only viable option. Gravure-printed BOPP film at 150–175 LPI enables magazine-quality output.

→ Deep dive: Laminated CMYK Printing Guide

05
Construction

Seam Construction: Welding vs Sewing

Ultrasonic Welding

  • 0.5–1.5 sec per weld
  • No consumables
  • Water-resistant seams
  • 1,200–2,000 bags/hr
  • Best for: high volume, light duty

Sewn Construction

  • 3–10 sec per seam
  • Progressive failure mode
  • Repairable if damaged
  • 200–400 bags/hr
  • Best for: heavy loads, premium

Sewn Loop

Most versatile. 15–22 kg with reinforcement. Standard for retail.

07
Branding

Logo Placement & Print Strategy

Regardless of bag type, logo placement determines brand impact. Primary logo on front center (60–70% of panel width). Back panel carries secondary information. Maintain 2.5–3.0 cm clearance from seams.
