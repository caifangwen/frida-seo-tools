Nonwoven bags are not a single product category—they are a platform. The same base material can become a grocery tote, a luxury gift bag, a wine bottle protector, or a garment cover depending on how it is cut, constructed, printed, and finished.

01
Retail

Shopping Bags: The Retail Standard

The workhorse application—used by supermarkets, boutiques, pharmacies, and department stores. Standard spec: 80 GSM, loop handles, front-and-back print. Bulk pricing drops from $0.45 at 1,000 to $0.22 at 10,000+.

03
Premium

Gift Bags: Elevating Perceived Value

Premium gift bags differentiate through material upgrades and accessories: 100–120 GSM or laminated surfaces, box-bottom gusset, satin ribbon bows, and foil stamping.

→ Deep dive: Gift Bags Guide

04
Specialty

Wine Bags: Protection Meets Presentation

Wine bottles present unique challenges: 1.2–1.4 kg weight, fragile glass, cylindrical shape. Specialized wine bags address these with dividers, base reinforcement, and handles engineered for concentrated weight.

→ Deep dive: Wine Bags Guide

05
Protection

Garment & Dust Bags

Clothing storage demands breathability. Nonwoven garment bags solve this through porous PP construction that allows air circulation while blocking dust, light, and insects.

→ Deep dive: Garment Bags Guide

06
Structure

Flat vs Box-Bottom

Flat Bag

  • Lower material usage
  • Faster production
  • Folds completely flat
  • Tips easily; limited volume

Box-Bottom

  • 2–3× volume increase
  • Stands upright
  • Better weight distribution
  • +25–35% cost premium
