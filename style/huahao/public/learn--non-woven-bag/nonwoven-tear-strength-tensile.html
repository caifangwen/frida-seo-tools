A bag that tears in a customer's hands is a brand disaster. Understanding tear strength and tensile properties helps you specify nonwoven bags that withstand real-world abuse—while avoiding over-engineering that inflates cost.

01
Directionality

MD vs CD Strength

Nonwoven fabric is not isotropic—its strength varies depending on the direction force is applied relative to the production line.

Nonwoven TypeMD StrengthCD StrengthMD/CD Ratio
Spunbond (standard)25–40 N/5cm15–25 N/5cm1.5:1 to 2:1
Spunbond (high-isotropy)22–30 N/5cm18–24 N/5cm1.2:1 to 1.5:1
Needle-punched18–30 N/5cm15–25 N/5cm1.2:1 to 1.5:1
Spunlace20–35 N/5cm18–28 N/5cm1.1:1 to 1.3:1
Table 1: MD/CD strength by material type

Best practice: Specify balanced MD/CD ratio (< 1.5:1) for bag-grade fabric, or request rotated panel cutting if high MD/CD ratio is unavoidable.
