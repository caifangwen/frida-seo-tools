Two bags can have identical GSM yet feel completely different in your hand. One might feel crisp and paper-like; another, soft and fabric-like. This difference—hand feel—is a critical but often overlooked factor in bag sourcing.

01
Science

Thickness vs GSM Relationship

GSM measures weight per area. Thickness measures distance between surfaces. The relationship is governed by bulk density:

Bulk Density = GSM / Thickness
Nonwoven TypeTypical GSMTypical ThicknessBulk Density

Spunbond800.45–0.60 mm133–178 kg/m³
Needle-punched1001.50–2.00 mm50–67 kg/m³

Table 1: Bulk density by nonwoven type

Implication: A 100 GSM needle-punched bag feels dramatically thicker than a 100 GSM spunbond bag, despite weighing the same per square meter.

05
Sampling

Sampling Advice

  • Request A4-sized swatches (minimum 20×30 cm) rather than small chips
  • Ask for 2–3 GSM variants, 2 bonding types, and 2 finish options
  • Fold and unfold 10 times to simulate storage
  • Load with 3–5 kg and carry for 5 minutes
  • Check recovery time after compression
  • Get 3–5 stakeholders to rate softness on a 1–5 scale
