## Introduction

Exporting nonwoven bags to the European Union requires compliance with **REACH**—the Registration, Evaluation, Authorization, and Restriction of Chemicals regulation. REACH is among the world’s most comprehensive chemical safety frameworks, and non-compliance can result in market denial, product recalls, and significant financial penalties. For bag manufacturers and exporters, understanding REACH obligations is not optional—it is a prerequisite for market access.

In this guide, you’ll learn:

- How the REACH SVHC candidate list and 0.1% threshold work
- Which substances in nonwoven bags are most commonly restricted
- What supplier documentation you need to maintain
- When third-party testing is necessary vs. when documentation suffices
- Penalties for non-compliance and how to avoid them

## REACH SVHC Overview

### Candidate List and Threshold (0.1%)

**Substances of Very High Concern (SVHC)** are chemicals identified by the European Chemicals Agency (ECHA) as having serious consequences for human health or the environment. These include:

- Carcinogens, mutagens, and reproductive toxicants (CMR)
- Persistent, bioaccumulative, and toxic substances (PBT)
- Very persistent and very bioaccumulative substances (vPvB)
- Endocrine disruptors

**The 0.1% threshold**: Any article (including nonwoven bags) placed on the EU market must not contain SVHCs above **0.1% w/w** (weight by weight) in any homogeneous component. If SVHCs exceed this threshold, the supplier must:

1. Notify ECHA (if total quantity placed on market > 1 tonne/year)
2. Provide **SCIP database** information (Waste Framework Directive)
3. Communicate safe use information down the supply chain

**As of 2026**, the SVHC candidate list contains **240+ substances**, with new additions occurring every 6 months.

## Relevant Substances in Nonwoven

### Heavy Metals, Phthalates, Flame Retardants

Nonwoven bags and their components may contain restricted substances through raw materials, additives, or printing processes:

### Heavy Metals

| Substance | Common Source | REACH Status | Limit (if applicable) |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|----------------------|
| Lead (Pb) | Pigments, PVC additives, recycled content | Restricted (Annex XVII) | 0.05% in articles |
| Cadmium (Cd) | Pigments (especially reds/yellows), stabilizers | Restricted (Annex XVII) | 0.01% in plastics |
| Mercury (Hg) | Antimicrobial treatments | Restricted (Annex XVII) | 0.0005% |
| Chromium VI | Leather dyes, metal finishes | SVHC + Restricted | 0.0001% in leather |

**Risk in nonwoven bags**: Lead and cadmium may appear in colored masterbatches, especially from lower-cost suppliers. Always request heavy metal test reports for colored bags.

### Phthalates

| Substance | Common Source | REACH Status |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| DEHP | Plasticizers in PVC components | Restricted (Annex XVII) |
| DBP | Plasticizers, adhesives | Restricted (Annex XVII) |
| BBP | Plasticizers, printing inks | Restricted (Annex XVII) |
| DIBP | Plasticizers, adhesives | Restricted (Annex XVII) |

**Risk in nonwoven bags**: If bags include PVC windows, zipper tapes, or printed components using certain inks, phthalate content must be verified.

### Flame Retardants

| Substance | Common Source | REACH Status |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| SCCPs (Short-chain chlorinated paraffins) | Coatings, plasticizers | Restricted (Annex XVII) |
| DecaBDE | Flame retardant in plastics | Restricted (Annex XVII) |
| HBCDD | Flame retardant in polystyrene | SVHC + Restricted |

**Risk in nonwoven bags**: Generally low for standard PP bags, but relevant if bags include foam inserts, rigid plastic components, or coated fabrics.

### Other Relevant Substances

| Substance | Source | Concern |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Nonylphenol ethoxylates (NPEs) | Detergents, wetting agents | Restricted in textiles |
| Perfluorinated compounds (PFAS) | Water/stain repellents | Increasingly restricted |
| Azocolourants | Certain azo dyes | Restricted (can release aromatic amines) |
| Formaldehyde | Finishes, adhesives | Restricted in textiles |

## Supplier Documentation

### Declaration of Conformity, Test Reports

REACH compliance is primarily demonstrated through documentation rather than testing every shipment:

### Declaration of Conformity (DoC)
A formal statement from the manufacturer or supplier confirming:
- Product identification (model, batch, date)
- Applicable regulations (REACH, including Annex XVII restrictions)
- Compliance statement
- Authorized signatory and date

**Best practice**: Obtain a DoC for each product family, updated annually or when formulations change.

### Test Reports
Third-party laboratory testing provides objective evidence:

| Test | Method | What It Covers |
|------|--------|----------------|
| REACH SVHC screening | GC-MS, LC-MS, ICP-MS | 240+ SVHCs |
| Heavy metals (total) | EPA 3052 + ICP-OES | Pb, Cd, Hg, Cr VI |
| Phthalates | GC-MS | DEHP, DBP, BBP, DIBP |
| Azo dyes | EN 14362 | 22 restricted aromatic amines |
| Formaldehyde | EN ISO 14184-1 | Extractable formaldehyde |

**Report validity**: Most buyers accept test reports less than 2 years old if materials and suppliers have not changed.

### Safety Data Sheets (SDS)
Required for substances and mixtures (e.g., inks, adhesives) but not for finished articles like bags. However, SDS for printing inks and coatings should be available from suppliers.

## Testing Requirements

### When Third-Party Testing Is Necessary

Not every shipment requires laboratory testing. Risk-based testing is standard practice:

**Testing required when**:
- New supplier or material source
- New color or formulation not previously tested
- Customer or retailer mandate (common for major EU retailers)
- Regulatory inspection or market surveillance inquiry
- Material change notification from supplier

**Documentation sufficient when**:
- Same supplier, same material, same formulation as previously tested
- Valid test reports (< 2 years) on file
- Supplier provides DoC with batch traceability
- No known regulatory changes affecting the product

### Testing Costs

| Test Package | Scope | Typical Cost | Turnaround |
|-------------|-------|-------------|------------|
| Basic REACH | 30 common SVHCs + heavy metals | $200–400 | 5–7 days |
| Standard REACH | 100+ SVHCs + restricted substances | $400–800 | 7–10 days |
| Comprehensive | Full SVHC list (240+) | $800–1,500 | 10–14 days |

**Cost optimization**: Start with basic screening for new suppliers; escalate to comprehensive only if basic results indicate risk or if serving the most demanding customers.

### SCIP Database Obligation
Since January 2021, suppliers placing SVHC-containing articles on the EU market must submit information to the **SCIP database** (Substances of Concern In articles as such or in complex objects (Products)).

For nonwoven bags:
- If SVHCs are < 0.1% in all components: No SCIP obligation
- If SVHCs are ≥ 0.1% in any component: SCIP notification required
- Most standard PP nonwoven bags with compliant inks and dyes fall below thresholds

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Market Access and Legal Risks

REACH enforcement varies by EU member state but generally includes:

| Violation | Typical Penalty | Additional Consequence |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------|
| SVHC > 0.1% without notification | €10,000–50,000 | Product withdrawal from market |
| Annex XVII restriction violation | €5,000–100,000+ | Criminal prosecution possible |
| False documentation | €5,000–20,000 | Supply chain termination |
| Missing SCIP notification | €1,000–10,000 | Corrective submission required |
| Market surveillance failure | Product seizure | Reputational damage |

**National enforcement examples**:
- **Germany**: BfR and customs actively inspect imports; fines up to €100,000
- **France**: DGCCRF conducts random testing; publishes non-compliant product lists
- **Netherlands**: NVWA focuses on online sales and imports
- **Sweden**: KEMI emphasizes SVHC communication obligations

### Risk Mitigation Strategy
1. **Map your supply chain**: Know every chemical input (resin, masterbatch, ink, adhesive)
2. **Require DoC from all suppliers**: Document compliance at each tier
3. **Test strategically**: New suppliers/formulations get tested; established lines use documentation
4. **Monitor ECHA updates**: Subscribe to SVHC candidate list change notifications
5. **Maintain 5-year records**: REACH requires traceability documentation retention
