01
Process

The Spunlace Process

The spunlace process is elegant in its simplicity: instead of needles, heat, or chemicals, it uses high-pressure water jets to entangle fibers into a cohesive fabric.

01

Web Formation

Staple fibers (viscose, polyester, cotton) are carded and cross-lapped into a loose web.

02

Pre-Wetting

Light water spray prepares the web for high-pressure treatment.

03

Hydroentanglement

Water jets at 20–250 bar strike the web, pushing fibers through the thickness.

04

Drying

The wet fabric passes through drum dryers to reach <10% moisture content.
