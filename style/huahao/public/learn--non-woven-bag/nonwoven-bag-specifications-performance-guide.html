A specification sheet is the contract between buyer and manufacturer. Ambiguous specs produce inconsistent quality, disputed deliveries, and failed inspections. This guide provides the specification framework professionals use.

GSM: The Foundation of Every Spec

GSM (grams per square meter) defines material quantity, which drives strength, opacity, stiffness, and cost.

Load Capacity: Real-World Testing

Real bags fail at handles, seams, and stress points—not uniformly across fabric panels. Handle reinforcement matters more than GSM for practical load capacity.

Thickness & Hand Feel

Consumer research shows stiffer, thicker bags are perceived as higher quality. Regional preferences vary: US favors medium-stiff, EU prefers soft-medium, Asia selects stiff and structured.

→ Deep dive: Thickness & Hand Feel Guide

04
Color

Custom Colors & Pantone Matching

Achieving accurate color on PP nonwoven requires lab dip submission, spectrophotometer evaluation (ΔE < 2.0), and Grade 4+ color fastness for retail acceptance.

→ Deep dive: Color Matching Guide

05
Strength

Tear Strength & Tensile Properties

Tensile strength varies directionally: MD typically exceeds CD by 50–100%. For 80 GSM PP spunbond, expect MD tensile of 25–35 N/5cm and CD of 15–25 N/5cm.

→ Deep dive: Tear Strength & Tensile Guide
