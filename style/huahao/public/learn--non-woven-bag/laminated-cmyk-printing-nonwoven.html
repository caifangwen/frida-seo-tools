When brand imagery demands magazine-quality reproduction—photographic product shots, complex gradients, or intricate patterns—neither screen printing nor heat transfer can deliver. Laminated CMYK printing using gravure-printed BOPP film is the gold standard.

01
Process

Pre-Lamination Printing

01

Gravure Cylinders

Copper cylinders engraved with microscopic cells for each CMYK color.

02

Film Printing

BOPP film passes through 4-color gravure press. Ink applied in reverse.

03

Drying

Printed film passes through drying tunnels between color stations.

04

Lamination

Printed film bonded to nonwoven with PE adhesive.

05
Design

Design File Requirements

  • Resolution: Minimum 300 DPI at final print size
  • Color mode: CMYK only—no RGB
  • Black text: 100% K (not rich black)
  • Formats: AI, PDF/X-1a, TIFF preferred
  • Bleed: 3 mm beyond cut line
  • Safety margin: 5 mm inside cut line
  • Trap: 0.2–0.3 mm between adjacent colors
