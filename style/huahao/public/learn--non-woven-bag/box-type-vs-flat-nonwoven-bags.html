Box-Type vs Flat Nonwoven Bags

The structural choice that transforms bag utility—from flat giveaway to standing grocery carrier.

The simplest nonwoven bag is a flat rectangle with handles. The most useful is often a three-dimensional box. The structural choice between flat and box-type (gusseted) construction dramatically affects capacity, stability, cost, and user experience.

01
2D

Flat Bag Structure

Flat nonwoven bags are constructed from two rectangular panels joined at the sides and bottom. The simplest, lowest-cost option.

💰

Lowest Cost

No gusset panels. Fastest cutting and sewing.

📦

Compact Storage

Folds completely flat. Minimal shipping volume.

⚠️

Limitations

Bottom sags under weight. Prone to tipping. No lateral expansion.

02
3D

Box-Bottom Construction

Box-bottom bags add gusset panels that convert the flat rectangle into a three-dimensional prism.

01

Separate Bottom Panel

Rectangular panel sewn between front and back. True box shape with flat base.

02

Folded Bottom (Self-Gusset)

Bag body cut longer; bottom corners folded inward. Simpler, less volume.

03

Full Box

Front, back, two sides, and bottom panel. Maximum volume and integrity.
