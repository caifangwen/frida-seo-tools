02
Material

PP Spunbond: The Workhorse

PP spunbond nonwoven is the default material for reusable bags, accounting for roughly two-thirds of global production. Standard specifications range from 60 to 120 GSM, with 80 GSM as the industry sweet spot.

✅

Advantages

Lowest cost per m², lightweight, versatile printing, quick production, recyclable (#5)

❌

Limitations

Not biodegradable, softens above 110°C, UV degradation, not as strong as woven PP

→ Deep dive: PP Spunbond Nonwoven Guide

03
Material

Needle-Punched: Texture & Thickness

Needle-punched nonwoven is thick, soft, and felt-like. A 100 GSM needle-punched material measures 1.5–3.0 mm thick—three to four times the thickness of equivalent-GSM spunbond.

→ Deep dive: Needle-Punched Nonwoven Guide

04
Material

Spunlace: Soft-Touch Premium

At the top of the softness hierarchy sits spunlace. High-pressure water jets entangle fibers without needles, heat, or chemicals. The result is a fabric with a cotton-like drape and hand feel.

→ Deep dive: Spunlace Nonwoven Guide

05
Material

Thermal-Bonded: Budget Option

When cost is the primary constraint, thermal-bonded nonwoven offers the lowest entry point. Production speeds reach 200–500 meters per minute. Typical bag specs are 50–70 GSM.

→ Deep dive: Thermal-Bonded Nonwoven Guide

06
Material

Laminated: Waterproof & Print-Perfect

Lamination solves both print resolution and water resistance limitations of standard nonwoven. BOPP film bonded to the fabric surface enables 150–175 LPI photographic CMYK reproduction.

→ Deep dive: Laminated vs Standard Guide
