02
Process

Pantone Matching Process

1
Reference Submission

Provide Pantone C/U number and substrate specification.

2
Lab Dip Preparation

Supplier prepares 3 variant swatches with formulation codes.

3
Evaluation

Compare under D65 daylight. Measure ΔE with spectrophotometer.

4
Approval

Approve closest match (typically 1–2 rounds). Lock formulation.

Pro tip: For critical brand colors, request the supplier to match on the actual nonwoven substrate rather than paper or plastic chips.

05
Issues

Common Color Issues

🌈

Metamerism

Colors match under one light but differ under another. Always evaluate under multiple sources.

📦

Batch Variation

Raw material differences and temperature fluctuations cause shade shifts. Request shade bands.

🟡

Yellowing

White/light fabrics yellow from UV or heat. Specify UV stabilizers for outdoor use.
