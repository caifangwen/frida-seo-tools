GSM is the single most important specification when sourcing nonwoven bags. It determines everything from durability and load capacity to print quality and unit cost. Yet many buyers confuse GSM with thickness, or assume that higher GSM is always better.

01
Fundamentals

What Is GSM?

GSM stands for Grams per Square Meter (g/m²). It measures the mass density of fabric—the weight of a one-meter-by-one-meter sheet.

Key distinction: GSM is not thickness. Two fabrics with identical GSM can have different thicknesses depending on fiber density, bonding method, and fiber type.

Standard

80gsm: Standard Shopping Bags

The industry sweet spot. 80 GSM accounts for approximately 60% of all nonwoven bag production. It delivers the best balance of adequate strength (6–10 kg), reasonable durability (40–80 cycles), competitive pricing, and good printability.

100gsm: Premium Feel

05
Heavy-Duty

120gsm: Maximum Durability

120 GSM is the upper end of standard nonwoven bag production. Built for heavy loads (12–18 kg), long-term reuse (100–150+ cycles), and rugged environments.

Construction note: At 120 GSM, fabric stiffness increases significantly. Handle reinforcement (X-stitch or box-stitch) and box-bottom gusset are essential.
