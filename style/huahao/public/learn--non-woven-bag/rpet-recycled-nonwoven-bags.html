The most credible response to plastic pollution is not abandoning plastic—it is keeping existing plastic in circulation. rPET (recycled polyethylene terephthalate) nonwoven bags do exactly that, transforming post-consumer bottles into durable, reusable carriers.

01
Supply Chain

rPET Material Source

The journey from discarded bottle to nonwoven bag involves multiple stages:

1
Collection

Curbside recycling, deposit-return schemes, commercial recovery.

2
Sorting & Cleaning

Resin type separation, cap removal, washing.

3
Flake Production

Shredding into 5–10 mm flakes. Float-sink separation.

4
Pelletization

Melting and extruding into rPET resin pellets.

5
Nonwoven Conversion

Spunbond or staple fiber production into bag fabric.

Yield: Approximately 10–12 standard PET bottles (500 ml) produce enough rPET nonwoven for one standard tote bag (35×40 cm, 80 GSM).

Performance

Physical Properties vs Virgin PP

rPET Nonwoven

  • Tensile (MD): 30–50 N/5cm
  • Melting point: 250–260°C
  • Density: 1.38–1.40 g/cm³
  • Color: Slight gray/yellow tint

Virgin PP Spunbond

  • Tensile (MD): 25–40 N/5cm
  • Melting point: 160–170°C
  • Density: 0.90–0.91 g/cm³
  • Color: Excellent consistency
