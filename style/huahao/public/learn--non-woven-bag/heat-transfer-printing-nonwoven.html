When your design includes photographs, gradients, or complex multicolor artwork, screen printing hits its limits. Heat transfer printing steps in as the versatile alternative—delivering full-color reproduction without the setup costs of screens or gravure cylinders.

How Heat Transfer Works

01

Digital Print

Artwork printed in reverse onto transfer paper at 300–720 DPI.

02

Positioning

Transfer paper positioned on bag fabric.

03

Heat Press

140–180°C, 3–6 kg/cm² pressure for 10–25 seconds.

04

Peel

Backing paper removed—hot or cold peel depending on formulation.

Break-even point: Heat transfer becomes more expensive than screen print at approximately 2,000–3,000 pieces for simple designs. For complex multicolor artwork, the break-even extends to 5,000+.

04
Limitations

Limitations vs Screen Print

⚠️ Limitations

  • Stiffer hand feel at transfer area
  • Visible transfer edges
  • 30–50 wash cycles (vs 50–100+)
  • May crack under stretch

✅ Advantages

  • No screen setup costs
  • Unlimited colors
  • Photo-quality reproduction
  • Low MOQ (100+ pcs)
