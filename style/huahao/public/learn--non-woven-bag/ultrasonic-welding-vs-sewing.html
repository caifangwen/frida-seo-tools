How a nonwoven bag is held together is as important as what it's made from. The two dominant construction methods—ultrasonic welding and sewing—offer distinct trade-offs in strength, appearance, speed, and cost.

01
Process

Ultrasonic Welding Process

Ultrasonic welding uses high-frequency vibration (20–40 kHz) to generate localized heat that melts and fuses thermoplastic materials.

⚡

Speed

0.5–2 seconds per weld cycle

🚫

No Consumables

No thread, needles, or bobbins needed

💧

Water Resistance

Fused seams block moisture penetration

✨

Clean Finish

No stitch holes or thread ends visible

04
Decision

When to Choose Each

Choose Ultrasonic Welding

  • Volume > 5,000 units
  • Budget is tight
  • Water resistance matters
  • Clean aesthetic desired
  • Simple construction

Choose Sewing

  • Maximum strength required
  • Premium positioning
  • Complex construction
  • Repairability valued
  • Progressive failure preferred
