Printing Methods

Logo Printing Methods Compared

Choosing the right printing method is the most critical decision in your custom logo bag journey. The method you select affects not only the visual quality of your logo but also its durability, environmental impact, and ultimately your cost per impression. Each technique has distinct advantages and limitations that make it more or less suitable for specific applications.

The six primary printing methods—screen printing, heat transfer, laminated printing, digital printing, foil stamping, and embossing—span a spectrum from basic to premium. Your choice should align with your logo complexity, order quantity, budget constraints, and brand positioning. A budget-conscious startup might prioritize cost-effective screen printing, while a luxury retailer might opt for the premium appearance of laminated printing or foil stamping.

Screen Printing (Most Popular)

Process: Ink is pushed through a fine mesh screen onto the bag surface. Each color requires a separate screen.

Screen printing remains the most widely used logo printing method for non woven bags, and for good reason. The process creates a thick, opaque ink layer that adheres strongly to the non woven fabric surface, resulting in vibrant colors that withstand repeated use and washing. Unlike other methods that deposit thin layers of ink, screen printing builds up a substantial ink film that can be felt slightly raised above the fabric surface.

The method works by stretching a fine mesh screen tightly over a frame, then coating it with a light-sensitive emulsion. Your logo design is printed onto a transparent film and placed on the screen, which is then exposed to UV light. The emulsion hardens everywhere except where the design blocks the light, creating open areas that will allow ink to pass through. During printing, the screen is placed over the bag, ink is poured onto the screen, and a squeegee pulls the ink across, forcing it through the open mesh areas onto the fabric below.

Screen printing's primary advantage lies in its cost efficiency at scale. While the initial setup cost for each color ($50-150 per screen) makes small orders relatively expensive per unit, this cost amortizes dramatically across larger quantities. A 1,000-piece order reduces the setup cost contribution to just 5-15 cents per bag, making screen printing exceptionally economical for medium to large runs.

The method excels with simple, bold logos using 1-4 solid colors. Logos with gradients, photographic elements, or subtle color transitions cannot be effectively reproduced through screen printing. However, for the majority of corporate logos—which typically feature 2-3 solid colors—screen printing delivers excellent results with outstanding durability.

Heat Transfer (Full Color)

Process: Design is printed on special transfer film, then heat-pressed onto the bag surface.

Heat transfer printing bridges the gap between basic screen printing and premium laminated printing, offering full-color reproduction capabilities at a moderate price point. This method uses a specialized transfer film that receives your design through high-resolution printing, then applies that design to the non woven bag through a combination of heat and pressure.

The transfer process involves placing the printed film face-down on the bag surface, then applying a heat press at approximately 160-180°C for 10-15 seconds. The heat activates a thermoplastic adhesive layer on the film, bonding the design to the fabric. After cooling, the film backing is peeled away, leaving your logo adhered to the bag surface.

What makes heat transfer particularly valuable is its ability to reproduce complex artwork with gradients, shadows, and photographic elements—something screen printing simply cannot achieve. If your logo incorporates color transitions, subtle shading, or detailed illustrations, heat transfer becomes the minimum viable option for faithful reproduction.

The trade-off lies in durability. While screen-printed logos can withstand 50+ wash cycles, heat transfer designs typically begin showing wear after 30-40 washes. The transferred layer may crack, peel, or fade with extensive use, particularly if the bag is frequently washed or exposed to harsh conditions. For bags intended for regular, long-term use, this limitation should be factored into your decision.

Laminated Printing (Premium)

Process: PP film is printed with your design, then laminated onto the non woven fabric before bag construction.

Laminated printing represents the premium tier of non woven bag printing, delivering the highest quality visual output available. The process begins with gravure printing your design onto a thin polypropylene (PP) film using up to 9 individual color cylinders. This printed film is then thermally laminated onto the non woven fabric substrate before the bag is cut and sewn, creating a unified material with your design permanently bonded to the surface.

The gravure printing process uses engraved metal cylinders—one for each color in your design—to transfer ink onto the film. These cylinders are expensive to produce ($300-800 total), which is why laminated printing carries a higher MOQ of 1,000 pieces minimum. However, once created, these cylinders can be stored and reused for future orders, making the setup cost a one-time investment for repeat customers.

What distinguishes laminated printing from other methods is the resulting surface quality. The PP film creates a smooth, glossy finish that elevates the perceived value of the bag dramatically. Colors appear more vibrant and saturated than they would printed directly onto fabric, and fine details reproduce with exceptional clarity. The laminated surface is also completely waterproof, making these bags suitable for applications where moisture resistance is important.

The durability of laminated printing matches screen printing—both withstand 50+ wash cycles without significant degradation. However, laminated printing achieves this with full-color photographic capability, making it the ideal choice for brands that demand both premium quality and complex design reproduction. Luxury retailers, high-end events, and premium corporate gifts frequently specify laminated printing for these reasons.

Premium Choice — Up to 9 colors with gravure printing, excellent detail reproduction, waterproof surface, and most durable print option. Ideal for luxury retail applications.

Digital Printing (Small Runs)

Process: Inkjet printing directly onto the non woven fabric surface.

Digital printing has revolutionized short-run custom bag production by eliminating the need for screens, plates, or transfer films entirely. Using industrial inkjet printheads that traverse the fabric surface, digital printers deposit ink droplets directly onto the non woven material in a pattern controlled by your digital artwork file. This direct-to-substrate approach mirrors how a desktop inkjet printer works, but at industrial scale with significantly higher precision and color accuracy.

The most significant advantage of digital printing is its minimal setup requirements. With no physical tooling to create—no screens to burn, no cylinders to engrave, no dies to manufacture—the setup cost drops to $50-100, and the MOQ plummets to just 100 pieces. This makes digital printing the go-to solution for prototypes, test marketing campaigns, small event runs, or any situation where quantity requirements fall below the thresholds of other printing methods.

Digital printing handles full-color artwork with gradients and photographic detail, similar to heat transfer but without the intermediate film layer. The ink is absorbed directly into the fabric fibers, creating a softer, more integrated appearance compared to the surface-level adhesion of heat transfer. Some designers prefer this aesthetic for its natural, less "applied" look.

However, digital printing carries trade-offs in durability and per-unit cost at volume. The ink penetration into the fabric means the print can fade or wear more quickly with repeated washing—typically lasting 20-30 wash cycles compared to 50+ for screen or laminated printing. Additionally, while digital printing is cost-effective for small runs, the per-bag printing cost ($0.20-0.40) remains higher than screen printing at equivalent volumes, making it economically disadvantageous for orders above 1,000 pieces.

Low MOQ Advantage — Perfect for prototypes, samples, small promotional runs (100-500 pcs), personalized/variable data printing, and urgent orders. No plate/screen setup required.

Foil Stamping (Metallic Effects)

Process: Metallic foil is transferred to the bag surface using heat and pressure with a metal die.

Foil stamping introduces a dimension of visual impact that no ink-based printing method can replicate: genuine metallic reflectivity. The process uses a custom-engraved metal die (typically brass or magnesium) that carries your logo design in relief. The die is heated to 100-150°C and pressed against the bag surface with a thin metallic foil film sandwiched between them. The heat and pressure activate an adhesive layer on the foil, transferring the metallic coating precisely where the die contacts the fabric.

The result is a striking metallic logo that catches light and draws the eye—gold, silver, holographic, or custom-colored foil that creates an unmistakable impression of luxury and quality. This makes foil stamping particularly popular for high-end retail bags, premium event giveaways, wedding gift bags, and corporate executive gifts where the perceived value of the packaging reflects the value of the brand.

Foil stamping works best with solid areas of foil rather than large continuous surfaces. Fine details down to approximately 1mm can be reproduced clearly, though extremely small text or thin lines may fill in during the stamping process. The technique can be combined with other printing methods—for example, a screen-printed logo with foil-stamped accent elements—to achieve both full-color reproduction and metallic highlights on the same bag.

The durability of foil-stamped logos is good, typically withstanding 40+ wash cycles. However, the metallic layer can gradually dull or develop micro-cracks with extensive flexing and washing, particularly if the bag is machine-washed in hot water. For bags intended for occasional or light use, this is rarely a concern.

Embossing (Textured Logo)

Process: Logo is pressed into the fabric using matching male/female dies, creating a raised or recessed effect.

Embossing takes a fundamentally different approach from all ink-based printing methods by creating a tactile, three-dimensional logo through physical deformation of the fabric itself. The process uses a matched pair of metal dies—one male (raised) and one female (recessed)—that carry your logo design in mirror image. The non woven fabric is positioned between these dies, and substantial pressure (typically several tons) is applied, compressing the fabric fibers and creating a permanent raised or recessed impression of your logo.

What makes embossing unique is its complete absence of ink or applied materials. The logo exists purely through the physical topography of the fabric surface, making it the most environmentally friendly printing option available—there are no inks, solvents, films, or foils involved, and no chemical processes that could affect recyclability. For brands with strong sustainability commitments, embossing communicates environmental responsibility through the very nature of the technique itself.

The tactile experience of an embossed logo is powerful. When a recipient runs their fingers across the bag surface and feels the raised texture of your logo, it creates a sensory brand connection that flat printing cannot achieve. This physical interaction makes the brand more memorable and gives the bag a premium, handcrafted quality that resonates particularly well in luxury, artisan, or natural product categories.

Embossing works best with simple, bold logo designs that have clear outlines and minimal fine detail. Complex logos with thin lines, small text, or intricate details may not reproduce clearly, as the compression process naturally softens edges and blends fine features. The minimum recommended logo size for embossing is approximately 2cm in width, and line widths should be at least 1mm for reliable reproduction.

The permanence of embossing is unmatched—since no ink is involved, the logo cannot fade, peel, crack, or wear away over time. The physical deformation of the fabric persists for the life of the bag, making embossed logos truly permanent. However, the subtlety of embossing means the logo is less immediately visible from a distance compared to printed alternatives, making it better suited for applications where close-up brand recognition matters more than broad visual impact.

Design Guidelines

Logo Design Best Practices

Having selected your printing method, the next critical factor is your logo design itself. Even the most advanced printing technology cannot compensate for a poorly prepared or inappropriately sized logo. The decisions you make about logo dimensions, placement, color selection, and file preparation directly influence the final product's professional appearance and your brand's perceived credibility.

A well-designed custom bag becomes a walking advertisement that people are proud to carry. An poorly executed one ends up relegated to the back of a closet or, worse, creates a negative brand association. The difference often comes down to attention to design detail—ensuring your logo is appropriately sized for the bag, positioned for maximum visibility, and printed in colors that contrast effectively with the bag material.

Logo Placement Options

Where you place your logo on the bag is almost as important as the logo itself. Strategic placement ensures maximum visibility while maintaining aesthetic balance. Different positions serve different purposes, and the optimal choice depends on your brand visibility goals, bag usage patterns, and design preferences.

Center front placement remains the industry standard for good reason—it positions your logo directly in the line of sight when someone observes a bag being carried. However, alternative placements can differentiate your brand from competitors and create a more distinctive look. Bottom placement conveys minimalist sophistication, while side gusset positioning offers unexpected visibility from oblique angles.

Many brands opt for dual-sided printing—featuring their primary logo on the front and supplementary information (website URL, tagline, or contact details) on the back. This approach maximizes brand exposure while providing practical information that drives traffic to your digital properties. The incremental printing cost (typically 50-100% additional) is often justified by the dual communication benefit.

Color Selection Guidelines

Color is arguably the most powerful element in your logo design arsenal—but only if it works harmoniously with the bag color. Poor color combinations can render your logo invisible or visually jarring, while well-considered pairings make your brand pop and create lasting visual impact.

The fundamental principle governing color selection is contrast. Your logo must stand out clearly against the bag background, and the degree of contrast determines how quickly and easily the human eye registers your brand. The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) recommend a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text, and this principle applies equally to logo legibility. High-contrast combinations like black-on-white, white-on-navy, or gold-on-black create instant visual clarity.

Beyond basic contrast, consider the psychological associations of your color choices. Blue conveys trust and reliability, green suggests environmental consciousness, gold implies premium quality, and red communicates energy and urgency. Aligning your logo colors with your brand personality reinforces the message your bag is meant to communicate.

Proper artwork preparation is the bridge between your brand vision and the finished printed product. Even with the best printing method and optimal color selection, poorly prepared artwork will result in subpar results. This section covers the technical specifications your logo file must meet and the common pitfalls that derail production timelines.

Understanding artwork requirements upfront prevents costly revisions, production delays, and disappointing results. Different printing methods have different file requirements, and suppliers typically specify their preferences during the quoting process. However, arriving at that conversation with a properly prepared file demonstrates professionalism and accelerates the entire ordering process.

Common Artwork Mistakes to Avoid
  • Low Resolution: Images below 300 DPI appear pixelated when printed
  • RGB Color Mode: Prints in CMYK, colors will shift
  • Missing Bleed: White edges on full-bleed prints
  • Thin Lines: Lines below 0.5pt may not print clearly
  • Small Text: Text below 6pt becomes unreadable
  • Unconverted Fonts: Font substitution issues
  • No Color Specification: Unclear color expectations
  • Wrong File Format: Word, PowerPoint files not suitable

Pricing Guide

MOQ & Pricing by Quantity

Understanding the economics of custom logo non woven bags is essential for budgeting effectively and maximizing your return on investment. Pricing structure involves two distinct components: the base bag cost (determined by size, material weight, handle type, and bag color) and the logo printing cost (determined by printing method, number of colors, and order quantity).

The most important principle in custom bag pricing is economies of scale. Setup costs—whether for screen creation, transfer film production, cylinder engraving, or die manufacturing—are fixed regardless of order quantity. When spread across 500 bags, a $200 setup fee adds $0.40 per unit. When spread across 5,000 bags, that same fee adds just $0.04 per unit. This dynamic makes larger orders dramatically more cost-efficient on a per-unit basis.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

After working with hundreds of clients ordering custom logo non woven bags, we've compiled the most commonly asked questions to help you navigate the process with confidence. These questions address the concerns, uncertainties, and decision points that arise repeatedly in our consultation process.

What's the minimum order for custom logo non woven bags?

MOQ depends on printing method: Digital printing (100 pcs), Screen printing (500 pcs), Heat transfer (500 pcs), Laminated printing (1,000 pcs), Foil stamping/embossing (1,000-2,000 pcs).

How much does it cost to add my logo?

Cost varies by method and quantity. For 1,000 pcs: Screen printing (1-color) $0.07/bag, Screen printing (2-color) $0.12/bag, Heat Transfer $0.15/bag, Laminated $0.28/bag, Digital $0.30/bag. Plus one-time setup fees ($50-800 depending on method).

Can you match my brand colors exactly?

Screen printing: Pantone color matching with ΔE <3 tolerance (industry standard). Full color printing: CMYK matching with physical proof approval. For exact match, provide Pantone code, approve physical color swatch, and review printed sample under standard lighting.

What file format do you need for my logo?

Best: AI, EPS, or PDF (vector formats). Acceptable: High-res PNG, JPG (300+ DPI, actual size). Not suitable: Word, PowerPoint, low-res web images.

How large should my logo be on the bag?

General guideline: Logo width should be 1/3 to 1/2 of bag width. For small bags (25cm): 8-12cm, Medium bags (30cm): 10-15cm, Large bags (40cm): 15-20cm, XL bags (50cm): 20-25cm.

Can I print on both sides of the bag?

Yes, double-sided printing is available. It adds 50-100% to printing cost and requires separate setup for each side. Common for: front logo + back website/contact info.

How durable is the logo printing?

Durability by method: Screen printing (50+ washes), Laminated printing (50+ washes), Foil stamping (40+ washes), Heat transfer (30-40 washes), Embossing (Permanent, no ink to fade), Digital printing (20-30 washes).

Can I change my logo after placing the order?

Before production: Usually yes, may incur revision fee. During production: May incur costs for materials already used. After production: Not possible; new order required. Recommendation: Finalize logo before production starts.

Do I own the printing plates/screens after production?

Typically no, screens/plates remain supplier property. Exception: Can negotiate ownership for large orders or custom dies (foil/embossing).

What if the printed logo doesn't match my approval?

Resolution process: 1) Document the issue with photos, 2) Notify supplier within 7 days of receipt, 3) Provide approved sample for comparison, 4) Negotiate: reprint, discount, or credit, 5) For critical mismatches: full replacement. Prevention: Approve physical color proof and printed sample before production.
