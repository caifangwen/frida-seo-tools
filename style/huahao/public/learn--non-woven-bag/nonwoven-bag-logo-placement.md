## Introduction

A beautifully designed logo loses impact if it’s poorly positioned, too small, or competing with cluttered visuals. **Logo placement** and **print area optimization** are where brand strategy meets manufacturing reality. The nonwoven bag surface—textured, flexible, and finite—requires thoughtful design decisions to maximize brand visibility and minimize production issues.

In this guide, you’ll learn:

- Standard print positions and their visibility impact
- Maximum print dimensions by bag size and machine capability
- How to establish visual hierarchy across logo, tagline, and contact info
- Cost implications of multi-sided printing
- Common design mistakes that ruin otherwise great bags

## Standard Print Positions

### Front Center, Sides, Bottom Gusset

Nonwoven bags offer several printable surfaces, each with distinct visibility characteristics:

### Front Center (Primary Panel)
- **Visibility**: Highest—seen when bag is carried facing forward
- **Recommended size**: 60–80% of panel width
- **Best for**: Primary logo, key message, campaign artwork
- **Consideration**: Handle attachment may intrude on upper 5–8 cm

### Back Panel
- **Visibility**: High—seen when bag swings or rests
- **Recommended size**: Same as front or slightly smaller
- **Best for**: Secondary message, website, social media handles
- **Consideration**: Often printed as mirror of front for walking visibility

### Side Gussets (Box Bags)
- **Visibility**: Moderate—seen from angles
- **Recommended size**: 50–70% of gusset width
- **Best for**: Taglines, hashtags, QR codes
- **Consideration**: Gusset folds create registration challenges

### Bottom Gusset
- **Visibility**: Low—seen when bag is set down or carried low
- **Recommended size**: 40–60% of bottom width
- **Best for**: Subtle branding, copyright lines, care instructions
- **Consideration**: Maximum durability requirement (contact with surfaces)

### Handle Area
- **Visibility**: Moderate—close to eye level when carried
- **Recommended size**: Small, 3–8 cm wide
- **Best for**: Brand name, short tagline
- **Consideration**: Must avoid handle attachment stress points

| Position | Visibility Index | Print Cost Impact | Recommended Content |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Front center | ★★★★★ | Baseline | Logo, hero image |
| Back panel | ★★★★☆ | +30–50% | Supporting info |
| Side gusset | ★★★☆☆ | +25–40% | Tagline, QR |
| Bottom gusset | ★★☆☆☆ | +20–35% | Fine print |
| Handle | ★★★☆☆ | +15–25% | Brand name |

## Maximum Print Dimensions

### By Bag Size and Machine Capability

Print size is constrained by both bag dimensions and printing equipment:

### Screen Printing Limitations
| Bag Size (W×H) | Max Single Print | Typical Frame Size | Registration Area |
|---------------|------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Small (30×35 cm) | 20×25 cm | 25×30 cm frame | 22×27 cm |
| Medium (35×40 cm) | 25×30 cm | 30×35 cm frame | 27×32 cm |
| Large (40×45 cm) | 30×35 cm | 35×40 cm frame | 32×37 cm |
| Extra large (45×50 cm) | 35×40 cm | 40×45 cm frame | 37×42 cm |

**Key rule**: Maintain 2.5–3.0 cm clearance from seams and handles. Ink applied too close to seams creates weak points and printing defects.

### Heat Transfer Limitations
- **Maximum platen size**: 40×50 cm (standard heat press)
- **Oversized printing**: 50×70 cm available on specialized equipment
- **All-over print**: Requires seam-to-seam coverage with pattern repeats

### Laminated Printing Limitations
- **Gravure cylinder width**: Up to 120–160 cm (multiple bags across)
- **Repeat length**: Unlimited (continuous film print)
- **Edge-to-edge**: Possible but requires precise cutting alignment

## Visual Hierarchy

### Logo, Tagline, Contact Info Priority

Effective bag design guides the viewer’s eye through intentional hierarchy:

### Primary Element: Logo
- **Size**: Largest element; 40–60% of total print area
- **Position**: Upper-center or true center
- **Contrast**: Highest contrast against bag color
- **Purpose**: Instant brand recognition

### Secondary Element: Tagline or Message
- **Size**: 25–40% of logo size
- **Position**: Below or beside logo
- **Contrast**: Moderate; supports without competing
- **Purpose**: Communicate value proposition or campaign theme

### Tertiary Element: Contact Information
- **Size**: 15–25% of logo size; minimum 6 pt equivalent
- **Position**: Bottom edge or corner
- **Contrast**: Sufficient for close inspection
- **Purpose**: Enable follow-up action (website, phone, social)

### The 3-Second Rule
A person glancing at a bag should understand:
1. **Whose bag is this?** (logo, 1 second)
2. **What do they do?** (tagline, 1 second)
3. **How do I find them?** (contact, 1 second)

Designs that fail this test are either overcrowded or improperly scaled.

## Multi-Sided Printing

### Cost Implications and Impact

Printing on multiple bag surfaces increases both cost and brand exposure:

| Print Sides | Cost Multiplier | Brand Exposure | Best For |
|-------------|----------------|----------------|----------|
| 1 side (front) | 1.0× | Baseline | Budget campaigns |
| 2 sides (front + back) | 1.6–1.8× | 1.8× | Standard retail |
| 3 sides (F+B+side) | 2.0–2.3× | 2.2× | Trade shows |
| 4 sides (all panels) | 2.4–2.8× | 2.6× | Premium branding |
| All-over print | 3.0–4.0× | Maximum | Fashion/retail |

**Why not 2× for 2 sides?** Setup costs (screens, registration) are shared; only incremental printing labor and ink add cost. The multiplier reflects roughly 60% of single-side cost per additional surface.

### Strategic Multi-Side Usage
- **Front + back, same design**: Walking billboard effect; seen from both directions
- **Front logo + back URL**: Drives traffic; front attracts, back informs
- **Front English + back local language**: International events, multilingual markets
- **Front brand + side hashtag**: Social media engagement

## Common Design Mistakes

### Bleed Issues, Fine Lines, Registration

Even experienced designers make errors when designing for nonwoven fabric rather than paper or screen:

### Bleed Issues
- **Mistake**: Design ends exactly at intended cut line; no bleed allowance
- **Consequence**: White edge shows if cutting or registration shifts 1–2 mm
- **Solution**: Extend background and critical elements 3 mm beyond trim line

### Fine Lines and Details
- **Mistake**: Hairline borders (< 0.25 pt), small text (< 6 pt), intricate patterns
- **Consequence**: Lines fill in or disappear on textured fabric; text illegible
- **Solution**: Minimum line weight 0.5 pt; minimum text 8 pt; simplify patterns

### Registration Challenges
- **Mistake**: Multiple colors touching with no trap; tight registration requirements
- **Consequence**: Color gaps or overlaps visible on coarse fabric
- **Solution**: Build 0.3–0.5 mm trap between colors; avoid fine multicolor details

### Poor Contrast
- **Mistake**: Light logo on light bag, or subtle tonal variations
- **Consequence**: Brand invisible from normal viewing distance
- **Solution**: Ensure minimum 30% lightness difference (L* value) between logo and background

### Overcrowding
- **Mistake**: Logo, tagline, 3 URLs, QR code, 5 product images, disclaimer text
- **Consequence**: Visual noise; no single element captures attention
- **Solution**: Maximum 3 distinct elements per panel; use back panel for secondary info

### Ignoring Handle Intrusion
- **Mistake**: Critical logo elements positioned where handles attach
- **Consequence**: Handles cover logo; stitching distorts print
- **Solution**: Maintain 5–8 cm clear zone at top center for handle attachment

### Color Modes
- **Mistake**: Submitting RGB files; using neon/fluorescent colors not achievable with standard inks
- **Consequence**: Color shifts; disappointment with final print
- **Solution**: Convert to CMYK; specify Pantone for critical brand colors; request proof
