01
Process

What Is Needle-Punching?

Needle-punching (also called needle-felting) is a mechanical bonding process that transforms a loose web of fibers into a cohesive fabric using thousands of barbed needles.

1
Fiber Preparation

Short staple fibers are carded into a uniform web.

2
Needle Penetration

3,000–8,000 barbed needles per m² pierce the web.

3
Fiber Entanglement

Barbs catch fibers and drag them vertically through thickness.

4
Density Building

Multiple passes increase fabric density and strength.

Unlike thermal bonding or chemical bonding, needle-punching requires no heat, no adhesives, and no water. It is a purely mechanical process that preserves fiber characteristics.

The entangled fiber structure traps air pockets, giving needle-punched nonwoven natural thermal insulation—ideal for wine sleeves, temperature-control pouches, and protective bags.
