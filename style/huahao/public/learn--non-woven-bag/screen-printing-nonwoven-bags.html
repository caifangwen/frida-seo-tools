Screen printing remains the dominant decoration method for nonwoven bags—and for good reason. It delivers vibrant, opaque colors at the lowest per-unit cost for medium-to-large runs.

01
Process

How Screen Printing Works

01

Screen Prep

Mesh coated with light-sensitive emulsion, stretched over aluminum frame.

02

Exposure

UV light hardens emulsion through film positive. Unhardened areas wash away.

03

Printing

Squeegee pulls ink through open mesh onto fabric.

04

Curing

Conveyor dryer at 120–160°C cures ink for 30–90 seconds.

Design

Design Best Practices

  • Minimum positive line: 0.5 pt (0.18 mm)
  • Minimum reverse line: 1.0 pt (0.35 mm)
  • Avoid gradients; use solid colors when possible
  • Overlap adjacent colors by 0.3–0.5 mm (trapping)
  • Use high-contrast combinations for readability
  • Vector format preferred (AI, EPS, PDF)
