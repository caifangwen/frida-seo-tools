California's Proposition 65 requires businesses to provide clear warnings before exposing consumers to chemicals known to cause cancer, birth defects, or reproductive harm. For nonwoven bag exporters, this means understanding whether your products contain listed chemicals.

01
Requirements

Prop 65 Requirements

The Prop 65 chemical list contains 900+ substances. Two categories exist:

🦠

Carcinogens

Chemicals known to cause cancer

🧬

Reproductive Toxicants

Chemicals known to cause reproductive harm

Safe Harbor Levels: Below safe harbor = no warning required. Above safe harbor = warning label mandatory. No safe harbor established = any detectable exposure requires warning.

03
Labels

Warning Label Obligations

Long Form: "WARNING: This product can expose you to chemicals including [name], which is known to the State of California to cause cancer [and/or birth defects or other reproductive harm]. For more information go to www.P65Warnings.ca.gov."

Short Form: "WARNING: Cancer and Reproductive Harm—www.P65Warnings.ca.gov"

Safe harbor protection: Using OEHHA-approved warning text provides "safe harbor" against enforcement action. Custom warning language does not.

05
Risk

Risk Mitigation

  • Specify lead-free and cadmium-free pigments in purchase orders
  • Use phthalate-free inks (water-based or UV-curable)
  • Avoid PVC components unless certified phthalate-free
  • Choose virgin PP over untested recycled content for CA-bound orders
  • Request XRF screening at incoming inspection
