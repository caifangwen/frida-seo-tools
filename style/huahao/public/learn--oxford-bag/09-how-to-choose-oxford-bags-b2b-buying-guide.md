## Step 2: Select Material Specification

### Understanding D Numbers

**Denier (D)** measures yarn thickness. Higher D = thicker, stronger fabric.

| D Number | Strength | Weight | Best For |
|----------|----------|--------|---------|
| 210D | ★★☆☆☆ | Very Light | Promotional, foldable bags |
| 420D | ★★★☆☆ | Light | Casual bags, light use |
| **600D** | **★★★★☆** | **Medium** | **Most bags (recommended)** |
| 900D | ★★★★★ | Heavy | Premium, heavy-duty |
| 1680D | ★★★★★+ | Very Heavy | Luxury, tactical gear |

> 💡 **Deep dive**: [Oxford Fabric D Numbers Explained: 210D vs 420D vs 600D vs 1680D](02-oxford-fabric-d-numbers-explained.md)

### Polyester vs Nylon

| Factor | Polyester | Nylon |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Strength | Good | 20-30% better |
| Weight | Moderate | 10-15% lighter |
| Cost | Lower | 30-50% higher |
| UV Resistance | Excellent | Good |
| Color Options | Extensive | Moderate |
| Eco-Options | GRS recycled available | Limited |
| **Recommendation** | **Most applications** | **Premium/heavy-duty** |

> 💡 **Detailed comparison**: [Polyester vs Nylon Oxford Cloth: Which Is Better for Bags?](03-polyester-vs-nylon-oxford-cloth.md)

### Sustainable Option: GRS Recycled Oxford

For eco-conscious brands, **GRS certified recycled oxford fabric** offers:

- ✅ Same quality as virgin material
- ✅ 42% lower carbon footprint
- ✅ Diverts plastic bottles from landfills
- ✅ Growing consumer demand
- ✅ Only 10-15% price premium

## Step 3: Choose Coating Type

### PU vs PVC Coating

| Feature | PU Coating | PVC Coating |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Feel | Soft, flexible | Firm, structured |
| Waterproof | Good (1,500-3,000mm) | Excellent (5,000-8,000mm) |
| Eco-Friendly | Yes | Moderate |
| Cost | Moderate | 10-15% lower |
| Best For | Premium bags, eco-brands | Heavy-duty, budget |

### Coating Weight Selection

| Application | PU Coating Weight | PVC Coating Weight |
|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Light duty (totes) | 80-100 g/m² | 150 g/m² |
| Standard (backpacks) | 120-150 g/m² | 200-250 g/m² |
| Heavy duty (tool bags) | 180-200 g/m² | 300 g/m²+ |

> 💡 **Technical details**: [Understanding PU Coating vs PVC Coating on Oxford Bags](04-pu-coating-vs-pvc-coating-oxford-bags.md)

### Waterproofing Requirements

| Use Case | Minimum Water Column | Recommended Coating |
|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Indoor/light rain | 1,000mm | PU 120g |
| Standard outdoor | 2,000mm | PU 150g |
| Heavy rain | 5,000mm | PVC 250g |
| Marine/extreme | 10,000mm | PVC 300g+ or TPU |

## Step 6: Verify Supplier Credentials

### Essential Supplier Qualifications

| Qualification | Purpose | Verification Method |
|--------------|---------|-------------------|
| Business License | Legal operation | Request copy |
| ISO 9001 | Quality management | Certificate verification |
| BSCI/Sedex | Social compliance | Audit report |
| GRS Certificate | Recycled material supply | Online database check |
| REACH Compliance | EU chemical safety | Test reports |
| Factory Audit Report | Production capability | Third-party audit |

### Red Flags When Choosing Suppliers

- ⚠️ No verifiable business license
- ⚠️ Cannot provide product test reports
- ⚠️ Unusually low prices (quality risk)
- ⚠️ No physical factory address
- ⚠️ Poor communication or English capability
- ⚠️ Refuses sample requests
- ⚠️ No quality control process described

### Questions to Ask Potential Suppliers

1. How many years in oxford bag manufacturing?
2. What is your monthly production capacity?
3. Can you provide references from current clients?
4. What quality control processes do you have?
5. Can you provide free samples before production?
6. What is your standard production lead time?
7. Do you offer design/OEM services?
8. What certifications does your factory hold?

## Step 7: Request & Evaluate Samples

### Sample Types

| Sample Type | Purpose | Cost | Lead Time |
|------------|---------|------|-----------|
| Stock Sample | Evaluate quality | Free + shipping | 3-5 days |
| Custom Sample | Test your design | $50-$200 | 7-14 days |
| Pre-Production Sample | Approve before mass production | Free | 5-7 days |

### Sample Evaluation Checklist

- [ ] Material quality (feel, thickness, consistency)
- [ ] Coating uniformity (no bubbles, peeling)
- [ ] Stitch quality (8-10 stitches per inch)
- [ ] Zipper smoothness and quality
- [ ] Hardware quality (zippers, buckles, D-rings)
- [ ] Logo/print quality (if custom sample)
- [ ] Color accuracy (vs Pantone or approved sample)
- [ ] Dimensions accuracy (vs spec sheet)
- [ ] Overall workmanship and finishing

### Testing Samples

For critical orders, consider:

- ✅ Third-party lab testing (tensile, tear, waterproof)
- ✅ Real-world use testing (load bearing, daily use simulation)
- ✅ Wash test (if machine-washable is claimed)
- ✅ UV exposure test (for outdoor products)

### Import Documentation

| Document | Purpose | Provided By |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| Commercial Invoice | Customs valuation | Supplier |
| Packing List | Shipment details | Supplier |
| Bill of Lading | Shipping contract | Freight forwarder |
| Certificate of Origin | Import duty reduction | Supplier/Chamber of Commerce |
| Test Reports | Compliance proof | Supplier |

### Import Duty Considerations

- HS Code for oxford bags: Typically **4202.12** (travel bags) or **4202.92** (other bags)
- Duty rates vary by country (US: 0-20%, EU: 2.7-9.7%)
- Certificate of Origin may reduce duty under trade agreements

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: Choosing Lowest Price Only

**Problem**: Lowest price often means compromised quality.
**Solution**: Balance price with quality, certifications, and service.

### ❌ Mistake 2: Skipping Sample Evaluation

**Problem**: Production quality may differ from expectations.
**Solution**: Always test samples thoroughly before committing to production.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Not Specifying Requirements Clearly

**Problem**: Miscommunication leads to wrong products.
**Solution**: Provide detailed specification sheets with approved samples.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Ignoring Compliance Requirements

**Problem**: Products may be rejected at customs or face recalls.
**Solution**: Verify all required certifications before production.

### ❌ Mistake 5: Underestimating Lead Times

**Problem**: Missed selling seasons or promotional deadlines.
**Solution**: Plan 60-90 days ahead of required delivery date.

## FAQ: Buying Oxford Bags for Your Brand

### Q: What's the best oxford fabric for a school backpack?
**A:** 600D polyester with PU coating is the industry standard—offering the best balance of durability, weight, and cost.

### Q: How much should I budget for custom oxford bags?
**A:** Budget $3-$8 per bag for standard quality (600D), $8-$18 for premium (900D+). Retail pricing is typically 4-6x the FOB cost. [Pricing guide →](12-oxford-bag-price-range-wholesale-cost.md)

### Q: Can I order a small test batch first?
**A:** Yes, many suppliers offer low MOQ options (100-300 pieces) for test orders, though pricing will be 20-40% higher. [MOQ guide →](11-custom-oxford-bag-moq-guide.md)

### Q: How do I ensure consistent quality across orders?
**A:** Request pre-production samples for each order, specify AQL inspection standards, and consider third-party inspection for large orders. [Quality checklist →](18-quality-checklist-oxford-bag-importers.md)

### Q: What's the best logo method for oxford bags?
**A:** Heat transfer for detailed/colorful logos, embroidery for premium feel, screen printing for simple designs on a budget. [Logo methods →](47-oxford-bag-logo-printing-methods.md)
