## What Is GRS Certification?

### Global Recycled Standard (GRS)

The **Global Recycled Standard** is an international, voluntary standard developed by **Textile Exchange** that verifies recycled content in products and tracks it through the supply chain.

### What GRS Certifies

| Criteria | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Recycled Content** | Minimum 20% recycled material (50%+ for GRS label) |
| **Chain of Custody** | Full traceability from recycler to final product |
| **Environmental** | Restricted chemicals, wastewater treatment |
| **Social** | Fair labor practices, safe working conditions |
| **Quality** | Product meets specified quality standards |

### GRS Certification Levels

| Recycled Content | Certification Level |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| 20-49% | GRS Certified (no label claim) |
| 50-94% | GRS Certified ("Made with Recycled Materials") |
| 95-100% | GRS Certified ("Recycled" label claim) |

**Most recycled oxford fabrics** achieve 95-100% recycled content, qualifying for the highest certification level.

## How Recycled Oxford Fabric Is Made

### From Bottle to Fabric: The Process

#### Step 1: Collection & Sorting

Post-consumer PET bottles (water bottles, soda bottles) are collected from recycling programs and sorted by color and quality.

#### Step 2: Cleaning & Shredding

Bottles are thoroughly cleaned, labels and caps removed, then shredded into small flakes.

#### Step 3: Melting & Pelletizing

Clean flakes are melted and extruded into small pellets called **rPET (recycled PET) pellets**.

#### Step 4: Fiber Extrusion

Pellets are melted again and extruded through spinnerets to create polyester filaments of specified denier (210D-1680D).

#### Step 5: Weaving

Filaments are woven into oxford fabric using the standard basket-weave pattern.

#### Step 6: Dyeing & Finishing

Fabric is dyed using eco-friendly dyes and coated with PU or PVC coating (eco-certified options available).

#### Step 7: GRS Certification

The entire process is audited by independent certifiers (Control Union, ICEA, etc.) to verify recycled content and chain of custody.

## Certifications & Compliance

### GRS Certification: What to Verify

When sourcing GRS certified recycled oxford fabric, request these documents:

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **GRS Certificate** | Verifies recycled content and chain of custody |
| **Transaction Certificate (TC)** | Confirms each shipment is GRS certified |
| **Test Report** | Verifies physical properties |
| **Scope Certificate** | Shows certified products and processes |

### Additional Eco-Certifications

| Certification | Focus | Compatibility with GRS |
|--------------|-------|----------------------|
| **OEKO-TEX Standard 100** | Chemical safety | ✅ Compatible |
| **bluesign®** | Environmental production | ✅ Compatible |
| **EU Ecolabel** | EU environmental standard | ✅ Compatible |
| **RCS (Recycled Claim Standard)** | Recycled content (simpler than GRS) | Alternative to GRS |

## Sourcing GRS Recycled Oxford: Buyer's Guide

### What to Ask Your Supplier

1. **GRS Certificate**: Current and valid?
2. **Transaction Certificate**: Available for each order?
3. **Recycled Content %**: 95%+ or lower?
4. **Test Reports**: Physical properties verified?
5. **Coating Type**: Eco-certified PU preferred?
6. **MOQ**: What's the minimum for GRS fabric?
7. **Lead Time**: Same as virgin fabric?

### Red Flags

- ⚠️ Cannot provide GRS certificate
- ⚠️ No Transaction Certificates for shipments
- ⚠️ Vague about recycled content percentage
- ⚠ Significantly cheaper than market (may be fake)
- ⚠ No independent test reports

### Quality Verification Steps

1. **Request samples** before placing order
2. **Verify GRS certificate** online (certificate database)
3. **Request third-party test** if order value justifies
4. **Inspect production samples** before shipment
5. **Retain TC documents** for your records

## FAQ: GRS Recycled Oxford Fabric

### Q: Is recycled oxford fabric as good as virgin?
**A:** Yes! Physical properties are within 3% of virgin fabric—a negligible difference that doesn't affect real-world performance. Most users cannot tell the difference.

### Q: How much more does GRS recycled fabric cost?
**A:** Currently 10-15% more than virgin polyester. However, the price gap is narrowing each year as production scales up. [Current pricing →](12-oxford-bag-price-range-wholesale-cost.md)

### Q: Can recycled oxford fabric be any color?
**A:** Standard colors are widely available. Custom colors are possible but require higher MOQs. The color range is growing but still more limited than virgin polyester.

### Q: Is GRS certification required for recycled fabric?
**A:** Not legally required, but essential for credible marketing. Without GRS certification, you cannot make verified recycled content claims.

### Q: Can I get GRS fabric with PVC coating?
**A:** Technically yes, but PVC is not eco-friendly. For truly sustainable products, use water-based PU coating. [Coating guide →](04-pu-coating-vs-pvc-coating-oxford-bags.md)

### Q: How many bottles are in a bag?
**A:** Approximately:
- Shopping tote: 8-10 bottles
- Backpack: 15-20 bottles
- Travel duffel: 25-30 bottles
