Material Science

What Is PU Coating?

PU (Polyurethane) coating is a flexible, environmentally friendly treatment applied to the back of oxford fabric to enhance water resistance, durability, and hand feel. Unlike traditional coating methods, PU forms a continuous polymer film that maintains the fabric's natural breathability while providing reliable moisture protection.

The chemistry behind polyurethane coating involves a sophisticated curing process that creates long-chain polymer molecules with exceptional elasticity and resilience. This molecular structure is what gives PU-coated fabrics their characteristic soft drape and superior cold-weather performance.

PU Coating Application Process - Liquid Solution Being Applied to Oxford Fabric

How PU Coating Works

PU coating is manufactured through a precise industrial process that ensures consistent coverage and optimal performance:

1
Preparation

Base oxford fabric is cleaned and pre-treated to ensure optimal coating adhesion. Any contaminants or sizing agents are removed.

2
Liquid Application

PU coating solution is applied as a liquid using precision coating knives or roller systems, ensuring uniform thickness across the fabric width.

3
Penetration

The liquid solution penetrates the fabric weave, bonding with individual fibers and filling micro-gaps in the textile structure.

4
Thermal Curing

Coated fabric passes through high-temperature curing ovens (120-160°C), where the polymer cross-links to form a continuous, flexible film.

5
Quality Testing

Finished fabric undergoes water column testing, peel strength assessment, and flexibility evaluation before shipment.

Advantages of PU Coating

Manufacturers and brand owners choose PU coating for several compelling reasons:

  • Environmentally friendly formulation: Free of phthalates and heavy metals, making it compliant with the strictest global regulations
  • Soft hand feel: Maintains fabric flexibility and natural drape, essential for fashion-forward bag designs
  • Breathable structure: Reduces condensation inside bags, protecting contents during humidity changes
  • Light weight addition: Adds minimal weight to fabric, crucial for lightweight product categories
  • EU/US regulatory compliance: Meets REACH, CPSIA, and California Prop 65 requirements without additional warnings
  • Color stability: Does not yellow or discolor over time, even with UV exposure

Disadvantages of PU Coating

While PU coating offers excellent overall performance, there are some limitations to consider:

  • Higher cost than PVC coating—typically 10-20% more expensive per meter
  • Slightly lower waterproof rating compared to heavy-duty PVC formulations
  • May require re-coating or reinforcement after extended heavy wear in extreme conditions

Material Science

What Is PVC Coating?

PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) coating is a thick, waterproof treatment that creates a robust barrier on oxford fabric, offering maximum protection against water ingress, abrasion, and chemical exposure. PVC coating has been the industry standard for heavy-duty applications for decades.

The formulation of PVC coating involves a carefully balanced mixture of PVC resin, plasticizers for flexibility, stabilizers for UV resistance, and fillers for cost optimization. This complex chemistry delivers exceptional waterproofing but at the cost of some flexibility and environmental compliance.

PVC Coating Production Line - Heavy Duty Application on Oxford Fabric

How PVC Coating Works

PVC coating manufacturing follows a paste application and fusion process:

1
Paste Preparation

PVC resin is mixed with plasticizers, stabilizers, and additives to form a homogeneous paste with controlled viscosity.

2
Knife Coating

The paste is applied to fabric using precision knife coating equipment, building up the desired thickness in controlled layers.

3
Thermal Fusion

Fabric passes through heated zones (160-200°C) where the PVC paste fuses into a solid, continuous film bonded to the fabric.

4
Embossing & Finish

Surface texture may be embossed or smoothed depending on application requirements. Fabric is cooled and wound.

Advantages of PVC Coating

  • Maximum waterproofing: Best-in-class water resistance for prolonged exposure scenarios
  • Excellent durability: Superior abrasion resistance withstands harsh field conditions
  • Lower cost: 10-20% cheaper than PU coating, advantageous for budget-conscious production
  • Structured feel: Adds body and rigidity to bags, supporting self-standing designs
  • Chemical resistance: Resists oils, acids, solvents, and industrial contaminants
  • Easy to clean: Wipe-clean surface suitable for hygienic applications

Disadvantages of PVC Coating

  • Environmental concerns due to phthalate content—regulated in EU and some US states
  • Stiff hand feel reduces fabric flexibility and natural drape
  • Heavier than PU coating, adding to overall product weight
  • Yellowing risk with prolonged UV exposure, affecting aesthetic appearance
  • EU/US restrictions require careful compliance verification (REACH, EN71, Prop 65)

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is PU coating waterproof enough for outdoor bags?

Yes! PU-coated 600D+ fabric with proper seam sealing provides excellent water resistance for most outdoor applications. Our testing shows PU coating maintains 1,200mm+ waterproof rating even after 50 wash cycles. For extreme conditions involving prolonged submersion, consider PVC or TPU coatings.

Can PVC-coated bags pass EU REACH testing?

Yes, if the PVC formulation uses approved plasticizers (non-phthalate alternatives such as DOTP or DINCH). Always request REACH test reports from your supplier before production. Our facility maintains REACH-compliant PVC options with full documentation.

Which coating is better for logo printing?

PU coating provides a smoother surface for screen printing and heat transfer, resulting in sharper logo reproduction. PVC can also be printed but may require specialized inks formulated for non-porous surfaces. Learn about logo printing methods →

Does coating affect fabric weight significantly?

Yes. Coating adds 80-350g/m² depending on type and weight specification. PU adds less weight than PVC at comparable waterproof ratings—for example, PU 150g/m² versus PVC 200g/m² for similar water resistance. This weight difference matters for lightweight product categories.

Can I get coating samples for testing?

Yes, most manufacturers including our facility provide free coated fabric samples for evaluation. We recommend requesting samples in your target D number and coating weight before committing to production. Request samples →
