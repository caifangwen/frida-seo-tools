01
Client Background

About NaiXue Tea

NaiXue Tea is one of China's pioneering premium fresh-tea brands, known for combining high-quality tea beverages with European-style bakery products in spacious, design-forward retail environments. The brand has built a reputation for bold marketing collaborations that transcend typical beverage-industry partnerships. Previous collabs with artists, fashion brands, and entertainment IP have consistently generated social-media frenzies and sold-out products.

Our collaborations need to feel like cultural moments, not marketing stunts. When customers receive this bag, we want them to feel like they're holding a piece of the wizarding world.

The Harry Potter collaboration targeted NaiXue's core demographic of urban millennials and Gen-Z consumers who grew up with the films and books. The thermal bag served as both functional delivery packaging and collectible merchandise — a dual purpose that justified premium production investment and limited-edition positioning.

02
Design & Creative

Design Concept & Visual Identity

The creative concept translates the four Hogwarts houses into a cohesive patchwork design. Rather than choosing a single house color (which would alienate fans of other houses), the patchwork approach celebrates all four — creating an inclusive design that resonates with the entire Harry Potter fandom. The deep brown base color evokes aged parchment and wizarding robes.

House Patchwork Pattern

Gryffindor, Slytherin, Ravenclaw, and Hufflepuff motifs woven into a cohesive mosaic celebrating all four houses.

Hogwarts Crest Foil

Metallic gold foil stamping of the official Hogwarts crest adds collectible premiumness and light-catching drama.

Dual-Brand Lockup

Harry Potter and NaiXue logos presented as equal partners, signaling authentic collaboration rather than simple licensing.

Branded Handles

Dark webbing with Hogwarts text print extends the wizarding theme to the most frequently touched bag element.

04
Production

Manufacturing Process

Licensed IP packaging requires dual-approval workflows and precise brand-guideline compliance. The six-stage production process incorporated Warner Bros. quality gates alongside NaiXue's own technical standards.

1
Color Proof Approval

Printed color proofs submitted to Warner Bros. for approval. Pantone matching verified against brand guidelines under D65 lighting.

2
Fabric Production

100gsm non-woven PP in approved brown base. Corona treatment for print adhesion and foil compatibility.

3
Gravure Printing

CMYK process print for house patchwork pattern. Registration within ±0.5mm for intricate tile alignment.

4
Gold Foil Stamping

Metallic foil for Hogwarts crest and brand lockup at 145°C. Precision die registration aligned to printed background.

5
Thermal Lamination

3mm EPE foam laminated to aluminum-PE barrier. Clear gloss film surface protection preserving foil brilliance.

6
Assembly & Dual QC

Ultrasonic die-cutting with sealed edges. Dark handles attached with reinforced stitching. Brand licensor sample approval before full production.

06
Analysis

Why This Design Works

Three design decisions differentiate this IP collaboration bag from standard licensed merchandise. Each addresses the specific expectations of Harry Potter fans and NaiXue customers.

  • 01

    Inclusive House Patchwork

    By incorporating all four houses rather than favoring one, the design avoids alienating fans who identify with Slytherin, Ravenclaw, or Hufflepuff. This inclusivity was strategically calculated: NaiXue's data showed that Gryffindor fans were already highly engaged, and the patchwork approach expanded appeal to the full fanbase.

  • 02

    Gold Foil as Collectible Signal

    The metallic crest transforms the bag from disposable packaging into keepsake merchandise. Fans who collect Harry Potter memorabilia recognized the foil treatment as a premium marker, justifying the effort to preserve and display the bag rather than discarding it after use.

  • 03

    Dual-Brand as Authentic Partnership

    Presenting Harry Potter and NaiXue logos as equal partners rather than licensor-licensee hierarchy signals a genuine creative collaboration. Fans respond more positively to partnerships that feel artistically motivated than those that read as pure commercial exploitation.
