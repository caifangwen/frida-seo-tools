## Chapter 1: What Are Non Woven Bags?

### Definition

**Non woven bags** are bags made from non woven fabric, which is a sheet or web structure bonded together by entangling fiber or filaments mechanically, chemically, or thermally. Unlike traditional woven fabric made from interlaced yarns, non woven material is produced directly from fibers without the weaving process.

The most common material used is **polypropylene (PP)**, a thermoplastic polymer that creates a lightweight yet durable fabric when processed through spunbond or meltblown manufacturing methods.

### Key Characteristics

| Characteristic   | Description                                                      |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Material**     | Primarily polypropylene (PP) fibers                              |
| **Structure**    | Fiber web bonded mechanically/chemically/thermally               |
| **Weight Range** | 60-200 GSM (grams per square meter)                              |
| **Durability**   | Reusable 50-100+ times depending on GSM                          |
| **Printability** | Excellent for screen printing, heat transfer, laminated printing |
| **Eco Profile**  | Recyclable, reusable, lower carbon footprint than cotton         |

### Non Woven vs Woven Fabric

```
Traditional Woven Fabric:
Yarn → Weaving → Fabric → Cutting → Sewing → Product

Non Woven Fabric:
Polymer Pellets → Melting → Extrusion → Fiber Formation → Web Laying → Bonding → Fabric → Product
```

The key difference: non woven fabric skips the weaving and yarn production steps, resulting in:

- **Faster production** (direct fiber-to-fabric process)
- **Lower cost** (fewer manufacturing steps)
- **Consistent quality** (no weaving defects)
- **Customizable properties** (fiber orientation, density, thickness)

## Chapter 3: How Non Woven Bags Are Made

Understanding the manufacturing process helps you evaluate quality and make better sourcing decisions.

### The Spunbond Process (Most Common)

```
Step 1: Raw Material Preparation
Polypropylene pellets → Drying → Additive mixing

Step 2: Extrusion
Melting (200-220°C) → Filter → Metering pump

Step 3: Spinning
Spinneret → Filament formation → Quenching

Step 4: Web Formation
Filament laying → Random distribution → Web consolidation

Step 5: Bonding
Thermal calendering OR Chemical bonding OR Mechanical needling

Step 6: Finishing
Winding → Slitting → Quality control → Packaging
```

### Bag Manufacturing Process

After fabric production, the bag making process includes:

1. **Fabric Lamination** (optional) - Adding PP film for waterproofing
2. **Printing** - Screen printing, heat transfer, or laminated printing
3. **Cutting** - Ultrasonic or blade cutting to size
4. **Handle Attachment** - Ultrasonic welding or sewing
5. **Side Seaming** - Ultrasonic bonding or stitching
6. **Bottom Folding** - Creating gusset for volume
7. **Quality Inspection** - Checking seams, handles, print quality
8. **Packaging** - Baling or cartoning for shipment

### Quality Control Points

| Checkpoint               | What's Tested            | Standard                  |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------ | ------------------------- |
| **Fabric Weight**        | GSM consistency          | ±5% tolerance             |
| **Tensile Strength**     | Load-bearing capacity    | Industry standard per GSM |
| **Handle Strength**      | Pull test                | 10-15kg minimum           |
| **Seam Integrity**       | Seam bonding             | No separation under load  |
| **Print Quality**        | Color accuracy, adhesion | No peeling, fading        |
| **Dimensional Accuracy** | Size tolerance           | ±3mm                      |

## Chapter 7: Are Non Woven Bags Eco Friendly?

This is one of the most common questions we receive. The answer requires nuance:

### The Environmental Reality

✅ **Yes, non woven bags are more eco-friendly than single-use plastic bags** when:

- Used multiple times (50+ uses)
- Properly disposed of or recycled
- Replacing single-use alternatives

⚠️ **Considerations**:

- PP is still a petroleum-based plastic
- Recycling infrastructure varies by region
- Production has environmental impact

### Best Practices for Sustainability

1. **Maximize Reuse**: Encourage customers to use bags repeatedly
2. **Choose Appropriate GSM**: Don't over-specify thickness
3. **Opt for Recycled Content**: Some manufacturers offer recycled PP
4. **Plan End-of-Life**: Provide recycling information to customers
5. **Consider Biodegradable Options**: Emerging biodegradable PP alternatives

## Chapter 10: Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: How long do non woven bags last?

With normal use, quality non woven bags (100+ GSM) can last 2-5 years with regular use, supporting 50-100+ shopping trips. Lifespan depends on GSM, handle type, and usage patterns.

### Q2: Can non woven bags be washed?

Yes, non woven bags can be hand washed with mild soap and water. Air dry only—do not machine wash or tumble dry as heat can damage the fabric structure.

### Q3: What's the minimum order quantity (MOQ)?

Typical MOQs range from 500-1000 pieces for custom printed bags. Stock bags are available in smaller quantities (100+ pieces). MOQ varies by manufacturer and customization level.

[→ Food safety guide: Non Woven Bags for Food](./non-woven-bags-for-food.md)
