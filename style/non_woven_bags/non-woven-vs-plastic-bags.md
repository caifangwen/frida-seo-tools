## Chapter 1: Understanding the Bag Types

### What Are Plastic Bags?

**Plastic bags** typically refer to single-use bags made from:

| Type | Material | Common Uses |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **HDPE** (High-Density Polyethylene) | #2 Plastic | Grocery bags, retail bags |
| **LDPE** (Low-Density Polyethylene) | #4 Plastic | Produce bags, garment bags |
| **LLDPE** (Linear LDPE) | #4 Plastic | Stretch wrap, thinner bags |

**Characteristics**:
- Thickness: 10-30 microns (0.01-0.03mm)
- Weight: 5-10 grams per bag
- Single-use design
- Low cost ($0.01-0.05 per bag)

### What Are Non Woven Bags?

**Non woven bags** are made from bonded polypropylene (PP) fibers:

| Type | GSM Range | Common Uses |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Lightweight** | 60-80 GSM | Promotional bags, events |
| **Medium** | 90-100 GSM | Retail shopping, general use |
| **Heavy-duty** | 110-150+ GSM | Supermarkets, premium retail |

**Characteristics**:
- Thickness: 0.3-0.8mm (depending on GSM)
- Weight: 30-80 grams per bag
- Reusable design (50-100+ uses)
- Moderate cost ($0.25-0.80 per bag)

### Key Structural Differences

```
Plastic Bag (HDPE/LDPE):
Polymer → Extrusion → Blown Film → Printing → Sealing → Bag
(Continuous plastic film)

Non Woven Bag (PP):
Polymer → Melt Extrusion → Spunbond → Web Formation → Bonding → Fabric → Bag
(Bonded fiber structure)
```

**The fundamental difference**: Plastic bags are continuous film; non woven bags are bonded fibers creating a breathable, fabric-like material.

## Chapter 2: Environmental Impact Comparison

### Lifecycle Assessment Overview

We analyze four lifecycle stages:

1. **Raw Material Extraction**: Oil/gas drilling, refining
2. **Manufacturing**: Production energy, emissions, waste
3. **Use Phase**: Transportation, reuse potential
4. **End of Life**: Disposal, recycling, degradation

### Production Environmental Impact

| Impact Category | Plastic Bag (HDPE) | Non Woven Bag (PP, 100 GSM) |
|----------------|-------------------|----------------------------|
| **Energy Consumption** | 1.5-2.0 MJ per bag | 8-12 MJ per bag |
| **CO2 Emissions** | 0.05-0.08 kg per bag | 0.35-0.45 kg per bag |
| **Water Usage** | 0.3-0.5 L per bag | 3-5 L per bag |
| **Material Weight** | 5-10g | 40-60g |

**Initial finding**: Plastic bags have **lower production impact** per unit—but this changes dramatically when we factor in reusability.

### The Reuse Factor: Per-Use Environmental Impact

This is where the comparison transforms:

| Bag Type | Total Uses | CO2 per Use (kg) |
|----------|-----------|------------------|
| **Plastic Bag (single-use)** | 1 | 0.07 |
| **Non Woven Bag** | 10 | 0.048 |
| **Non Woven Bag** | 50 | 0.0096 |
| **Non Woven Bag** | 100 | 0.0048 |

**Break-even point**: A non woven bag must be used **10-15 times** to have lower per-use carbon footprint than single-use plastic bags.

### Full Lifecycle Carbon Footprint

```
Scenario: 1 year of shopping (52 trips)

Option A: Single-use plastic bags (2 bags per trip = 104 bags)
├── Production: 104 × 0.07 kg = 7.28 kg CO2e
├── Transport: Higher volume, more trips
├── End of Life: 104 bags to landfill/recycling
└── TOTAL: ~8.5 kg CO2e

Option B: Non woven bags (2 bags, used 52 times each)
├── Production: 2 × 0.40 kg = 0.80 kg CO2e
├── Transport: Compact, fewer trips
├── End of Life: 2 bags to recycling
└── TOTAL: ~1.5 kg CO2e

SAVINGS: 82% reduction with non woven bags
```

### Waste Generation Comparison

| Scenario | Plastic Bags | Non Woven Bags |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Annual waste (52 shopping trips)** | 104 bags (1-1.5 kg) | 1-2 bags (0.1 kg) |
| **Volume in landfill** | 15-20 liters | 1-2 liters |
| **Litter persistence** | 100-500 years | 50-100 years |
| **Marine impact** | High (floats, wildlife hazard) | Medium (sinks, entanglement risk) |

### Recycling Rates & Infrastructure

| Region | Plastic Bag Recycling Rate | Non Woven PP Recycling Rate |
|--------|---------------------------|----------------------------|
| **North America** | 10-15% | 25-35% |
| **Europe** | 20-30% | 45-55% |
| **Australia** | 15-20% | 40-50% |
| **Asia** | 20-40% | 30-45% |

**Note**: Non woven PP has higher recycling rates due to:
- Greater durability (survives collection/sorting)
- Higher value per unit weight
- Less contamination (reused before recycling)

## Chapter 7: When to Choose Each Option

### Choose Non Woven Bags When:

✅ **Long-term reuse is expected**
- Retail shopping bags
- Brand promotion items
- Customer loyalty programs

✅ **Plastic bag taxes apply**
- Avoid ongoing tax liability
- Pass savings to customers

✅ **Brand image matters**
- Sustainability is a brand value
- Premium positioning

✅ **Durability requirements**
- Heavy items (clothing, books, electronics)
- Multiple uses expected

✅ **Customization needs**
- Full-color logo printing
- Unique sizes and handle types

### Choose Plastic Bags When:

✅ **Single-use is necessary**
- Food safety requirements (raw meat, produce)
- Medical/hygiene applications
- Internal packaging (not customer-facing)

✅ **Cost is the only factor**
- No plastic taxes in region
- Short-term cost pressure
- High-volume, low-margin business

✅ **Waterproofing is critical**
- Wet/frozen products
- Outdoor use in rain

✅ **Regulatory exemptions apply**
- Food safety exemptions
- Medical/healthcare requirements

## Chapter 8: Hybrid Strategies

### Best of Both Worlds Approach

Many businesses adopt hybrid strategies:

#### Strategy 1: Tiered Bag Program

| Customer Segment | Bag Type | Rationale |
|-----------------|----------|-----------|
| Regular shoppers | Non woven (sold/given) | Reuse 50+ times |
| Occasional shoppers | Non woven (sold) | Encourage reuse |
| Forgot bag customers | Non woven (emergency sale) | Capture revenue |
| Food safety items | Compostable plastic | Regulatory compliance |

#### Strategy 2: Bag-as-a-Service

- Offer non woven bag rental/loan program
- Customers return bags on next visit
- Business handles cleaning and redistribution
- Reduces waste, builds customer loyalty

#### Strategy 3: Incentive Program

- Discount ($0.10-0.25) for customers who bring own bags
- Non woven bags available for purchase at cost
- Loyalty points for sustainable behavior
- Reduces bag consumption 40-60%

## Chapter 10: Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Are non woven bags better than plastic bags?

**Yes, for most retail applications.** Non woven bags have lower per-use environmental impact when reused 10+ times, offer superior durability, and provide better brand value. However, plastic bags may still be appropriate for food safety applications.

[→ Learn more: Are Non Woven Bags Eco Friendly](./are-non-woven-bags-eco-friendly.md)

### Q2: How many times must I reuse a non woven bag to beat plastic?

**10-15 uses** is the break-even point for carbon footprint. For cost savings (in taxed regions), just 2-3 uses makes non woven bags more economical.

### Q3: Can non woven bags replace all plastic bags?

**Not all applications.** Non woven bags excel for retail shopping, promotions, and general use. However, plastic bags remain necessary for:
- Raw meat/fish packaging (food safety)
- Produce bags (hygiene)
- Medical applications
- Some industrial uses

### Q4: Are non woven bags recyclable?

**Yes**, PP non woven bags are recyclable (#5 plastic) where facilities exist. Recycling rates are higher than plastic bags due to better durability and value.

[→ Learn more: Recyclable Non Woven Bags](./recyclable-non-woven-bags.md)

### Q5: Do non woven bags cost more than plastic bags?

**Per unit: Yes** ($0.30-0.80 vs. $0.01-0.05).  
**Per use: No** ($0.003-0.008 vs. $0.025+ with taxes).

When factoring in reuse and plastic bag taxes, non woven bags are often cheaper long-term.

### Q6: How do non woven bags compare to cotton bags?

Non woven bags have lower production impact than cotton bags and reach break-even faster (10-15 uses vs. 150-200 uses for cotton). For most applications, non woven offers better environmental and cost balance.

[→ Read full comparison: Non Woven vs Cotton Bags](./non-woven-vs-cotton-bags.md)

| Application | Recommended GSM |

| Clothing stores | 100-120 GSM |

### Q8: Can I print my logo on non woven bags?

**Yes**, non woven bags accept various printing methods:
- **Screen printing**: Best for simple logos, 1-4 colors

### Q9: Are non woven bags waterproof?

**Unlaminated non woven bags** are water-resistant (30+ minutes) but not fully waterproof. **Laminated non woven bags** with PP film coating are 100% waterproof.
