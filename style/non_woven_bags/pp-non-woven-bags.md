## Chapter 1: Understanding PP (Polypropylene)

### What is Polypropylene?

**Polypropylene (PP)** is a thermoplastic polymer made from propylene monomers through polymerization. It's one of the most versatile and widely used plastics globally.

**Chemical Structure**:
```
Propylene Monomer: CH₂=CH-CH₃
Polymerization → [-CH₂-CH(CH₃)-]n
```

**Key Characteristics**:
- **Density**: 0.895-0.92 g/cm³ (lightest commodity plastic)
- **Melting Point**: 160-170°C
- **Glass Transition**: -10 to -20°C
- **Crystallinity**: 60-70% (semi-crystalline)
- **Resin Code**: #5 (recyclable)

---

### Why PP for Non Woven Bags?

**Advantages Over Other Materials**:

| Property | PP | PE (Polyethylene) | PET (Polyester) | Cotton |
|----------|-----|-------------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Density** | 0.90 g/cm³ | 0.92-0.97 | 1.38 | 1.54 |
| **Melting Point** | 160-170°C | 105-135°C | 250-260°C | N/A (burns) |
| **Tensile Strength** | High | Medium | Very High | High |
| **Moisture Absorption** | <0.01% | <0.01% | 0.4% | 8-10% |
| **Chemical Resistance** | Excellent | Good | Good | Poor |
| **Cost** | Low | Low | Medium | High |
| **Recyclability** | Yes (#5) | Yes (#2, #4) | Yes (#1) | Compostable |

**PP wins for non woven bags because of**:
✅ Lowest density (lightest weight per volume)  
✅ Excellent moisture resistance  
✅ Good chemical resistance  
✅ High melting point (heat sealable)  
✅ Cost-effective production  
✅ Recyclable infrastructure  

---

### Types of PP Used in Non Woven Bags

#### 1. Homopolymer PP

**Characteristics**:
- Pure polypropylene resin
- Higher stiffness
- Better heat resistance
- Lower cost

**Best For**: Standard shopping bags, promotional bags

---

#### 2. Copolymer PP

**Characteristics**:
- PP + ethylene (3-8%)
- Improved impact strength
- Better flexibility
- Enhanced durability

**Best For**: Heavy-duty bags, bags requiring flexibility

---

#### 3. Recycled PP (rPP)

**Characteristics**:
- Post-industrial or post-consumer recycled content
- 20-50% recycled material typical
- Slightly reduced properties
- Lower carbon footprint

**Best For**: Eco-conscious brands, sustainability-focused applications

**Certification**: GRS (Global Recycled Standard) certified options available

## Chapter 3: GSM Grades Explained

### What is GSM?

**GSM (Grams per Square Meter)** measures the weight/thickness of non woven fabric. It's the primary specification for non woven bags.

**Relationship**:
- Higher GSM = thicker fabric = stronger bag = higher cost
- Lower GSM = thinner fabric = lighter bag = lower cost

### GSM Tolerance Standards

**Industry Standard Tolerance**: ±5%

**Example**: 100 GSM fabric should measure 95-105 GSM

**Premium Suppliers**: ±3% tolerance

**Testing Method**:
1. Cut 100 cm² sample
2. Weigh on precision scale (0.001g accuracy)
3. Calculate: Weight (g) × 100 = GSM
4. Average multiple samples

**Red Flags**:
❌ GSM variance >10% (inconsistent quality)  
❌ Systematic under-weight (cost-cutting)  
❌ No GSM specification on documentation

## Chapter 4: Manufacturing Process

### Complete Production Flow

```
Step 1: Raw Material Preparation
├── PP resin pellets (virgin or recycled)
├── Additives (UV stabilizers, colorants, fillers)
├── Drying (remove moisture)
└── Blending (homogeneous mixture)

Step 2: Extrusion
├── Melting (200-220°C)
├── Filtration (remove contaminants)
├── Metering pump (consistent flow)
└── Spinneret (fiber formation)

Step 3: Spinning
├── Filament extrusion through spinneret holes
├── Quenching (cooling with air)
├── Drawing (orient molecules, increase strength)
└── Filament diameter: 15-35 microns

Step 4: Web Formation
├── Filaments laid on moving conveyor
├── Random distribution (isotropic properties)
├── Web thickness controlled by line speed
└── Basis weight (GSM) determined here

Step 5: Bonding (Spunbond Process)
├── Thermal calendering (heat + pressure)
├── Bonding points: 15-25% of surface area
├── Pattern: Diamond, square, or custom
└── Fabric consolidation

Step 6: Winding
├── Roll-to-roll processing
├── Slitting to required width (160-320cm)
├── Quality inspection
└── Roll weight: 50-200 kg

Step 7: Bag Manufacturing
├── Fabric unwinding
├── Printing (screen, heat transfer, laminated)
├── Lamination (optional, for waterproofing)
├── Cutting (ultrasonic or blade)
├── Handle attachment (ultrasonic welding or sewing)
├── Side seaming (ultrasonic or stitching)
├── Bottom folding (gusset creation)
├── Quality inspection
└── Packaging and palletizing
```

---

| Stage | Parameter | Control Range | Impact |
|-------|-----------|---------------|--------|
| **Extrusion** | Temperature | 200-220°C | Fiber quality, MFI |
| **Spinning** | Air velocity | 3000-6000 m/min | Fiber diameter |

| **Bonding** | Calender temperature | 140-160°C | Bond strength |

## Chapter 5: Quality Indicators & Testing

### Essential Quality Tests

#### 1. GSM Verification

**Method**: ISO 9073-1
- Cut 5 samples (100 cm² each)
- Weigh on precision scale
- Calculate average GSM
- **Acceptance**: ±5% of specified value

---

#### 2. Tensile Strength

**Method**: ISO 527-3
- Test in MD (machine direction) and CD (cross direction)
- Sample size: 50mm × 200mm
- Test speed: 100 mm/min
- **Acceptance**: Meet minimum values for GSM grade

---

#### 3. Handle Pull Test

**Method**: Internal/Custom
- Fill bag with standard weight (based on GSM)
- Hang for 1 minute
- **Acceptance**: No tearing, deformation <10%

| GSM | Test Load |
|-----|-----------|
| 80 | 10 kg |
| 100 | 14 kg |
| 120 | 18 kg |
| 140 | 22 kg |
| 150+ | 25 kg |

---

#### 4. Seam Strength

**Method**: Internal/Custom
- Apply force to side seams
- **Acceptance**: No separation at 15-25 kg (depending on GSM)

---

#### 5. Print Adhesion

**Method**: Tape test (ASTM D3359)
- Apply adhesive tape to printed area
- Peel off rapidly
- **Acceptance**: <5% ink removal (Class 4-5)

---

#### 6. Color Fastness

**Method**: ISO 105-B02 (light fastness)
- Expose to xenon arc lamp
- Rate color change after 20-40 hours
- **Acceptance**: Grade 4+ (minimal change)

---

#### 7. Water Resistance

**Method**: AATCC 127 (hydrostatic head)
- Measure water pressure resistance
- **Acceptance**: >50 cm H₂O (unlaminated), >500 cm H₂O (laminated)

### Q4: How long do PP non woven bags last?

**Lifespan by GSM**:

### Q5: Are PP non woven bags waterproof?

**Unlaminated PP bags**: Water-resistant (not waterproof)
- Repels light moisture for 30+ minutes
- Not suitable for wet items or rain

**Laminated PP bags**: 100% waterproof
- PP film coating provides complete water barrier
- Suitable for frozen items, produce, outdoor use

### Q7: Can PP non woven bags be washed?

**Yes**, with proper care:
- **Hand wash**: Mild soap, lukewarm water, air dry
- **Machine wash**: Not recommended (can damage bonds)
- **Drying**: Air dry only, no tumble dry
- **Ironing**: Low heat if needed

**Print durability**:
- Screen printing: 50+ washes
- Heat transfer: 30-40 washes
- Laminated: Wipe clean only
