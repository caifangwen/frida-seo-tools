## Chapter 1: OEM vs. ODM Explained

### What's the Difference?

| Aspect | OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) | ODM (Original Design Manufacturer) |
|--------|--------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Design Ownership** | Buyer provides design | Supplier provides design |
| **Customization Level** | High (fully custom) | Medium (modified existing) |
| **Development Cost** | Higher (new tooling) | Lower (existing designs) |
| **MOQ** | 2,000-5,000+ pcs | 500-2,000 pcs |
| **Lead Time** | 25-40 days | 15-25 days |
| **Best For** | Unique products, brand differentiation | Faster launch, lower investment |

### OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer)

**How It Works**:
```
Buyer → Provides Design → Supplier → Manufactures → Buyer's Brand
```

**Pros**:
- Complete design control
- Unique product differentiation
- Full IP ownership
- Tailored to exact specifications

**Cons**:
- Higher development costs
- Longer lead times
- Higher MOQs
- More coordination required

**Best For**:
- Established brands with specific requirements
- Unique handle designs or structural features
- Companies with in-house design teams
- Large volume orders (10,000+ pcs)

### ODM (Original Design Manufacturer)

**How It Works**:
```
Supplier → Existing Designs → Buyer Selects/Modifies → Buyer's Brand
```

**Pros**:
- Lower development costs
- Faster time to market
- Lower MOQs
- Proven designs

**Cons**:
- Less differentiation (others may use same design)
- Limited customization options
- May share design with competitors
- Less IP protection

**Best For**:
- Startups and small businesses
- Quick product launches
- Budget-conscious projects
- Testing market demand

### Hybrid Approach

Many suppliers offer **OEM+ODM hybrid**:
- Start with ODM base design
- Customize handles, sizes, printing
- Lower cost than full OEM
- Better differentiation than pure ODM

**Example**:
```
ODM Base Bag + OEM Customization:
├── Standard bag structure (ODM)
├── Custom size (OEM modification)
├── Custom handle type (OEM modification)
└── Custom printing (OEM addition)
```

### Size Customization

#### How to Specify Size

Non woven bag dimensions are expressed as:
```
Width (W) × Gusset/Depth (D) × Height (H)

Example: 30cm(W) × 12cm(D) × 40cm(H)
```

**Measurement Guide**:
- **Width**: Bag opening measurement (flat)
- **Gusset/Depth**: Side-to-side expansion
- **Height**: Bottom to top (excluding handles)

#### Standard Size Chart

| Size | Dimensions (cm) | Dimensions (inches) | Common Use |
|------|-----------------|--------------------|------------|
| **XS** | 20×8×25 | 8×3×10 | Jewelry, cosmetics |
| **S** | 25×10×30 | 10×4×12 | Gifts, small retail |
| **M** | 30×12×35 | 12×5×14 | Clothing, books |
| **L** | 40×15×45 | 16×6×18 | Groceries, shopping |
| **XL** | 50×18×50 | 20×7×20 | Bulk items, multiple products |
| **XXL** | 60×20×60 | 24×8×24 | Large format retail |

#### Custom Size Considerations

**No Tooling Cost**: Stay within standard size ranges
**Tooling Required**: Unusual sizes may require new cutting dies ($100-300)
**Aspect Ratio**: Keep W:D:H proportional for structural integrity
**Fabric Width**: Consider standard fabric roll widths (160cm, 200cm) to minimize waste

## Chapter 3: Printing & Branding Options

### Printing Methods Deep Dive

#### 1. Screen Printing (Silk Screen)

**Process**: Ink pushed through mesh screen onto fabric

**Specifications**:
- **Colors**: 1-4 colors (spot colors)
- **MOQ**: 500+ pcs
- **Setup Cost**: $50-150 per color
- **Print Cost**: $0.05-0.15 per bag
- **Lead Time**: 7-10 days
- **Artwork**: Vector format (AI, EPS, PDF)

**Pros**:
- Cost-effective for simple designs
- Vibrant solid colors
- Durable print (50+ washes)
- Fast turnaround

**Cons**:
- Not suitable for gradients/photos
- Each color requires separate screen
- Setup cost for multiple colors
- Color matching limitations

**Best For**: Logos, text, simple graphics, solid color designs

#### 2. Heat Transfer (Thermal Transfer)

**Process**: Design printed on transfer film, heat-pressed onto bag

**Specifications**:
- **Colors**: Full color (CMYK)
- **MOQ**: 500+ pcs
- **Setup Cost**: $100-300
- **Print Cost**: $0.10-0.25 per bag
- **Lead Time**: 10-14 days
- **Artwork**: High-res raster (300+ DPI)

**Pros**:
- Full-color printing capability
- Photographic quality
- No color limitations
- Good for complex designs

**Cons**:
- Higher cost than screen printing
- Slightly less durable (30-40 washes)
- Transfer film waste
- Color variation possible

**Best For**: Detailed logos, photographs, gradient designs, artwork

#### 3. Laminated Printing (Lamination + Gravure)

**Process**: PP film printed, laminated to non woven fabric, cut and sewn

**Specifications**:
- **Colors**: Full color (up to 9 colors gravure)
- **MOQ**: 1,000+ pcs
- **Setup Cost**: $300-800 (cylinder cost)
- **Print Cost**: $0.15-0.35 per bag
- **Lead Time**: 14-20 days
- **Artwork**: Vector or high-res raster

**Pros**:
- Premium glossy finish
- Excellent color reproduction
- Waterproof surface
- Most durable print option

**Cons**:
- Highest setup cost
- Higher MOQ
- Longer lead time
- Not recyclable (mixed materials)

**Best For**: Premium brands, full-color photography, luxury packaging

#### 4. Digital Printing (Direct-to-Fabric)

**Process**: Inkjet printing directly onto non woven fabric

**Specifications**:
- **Colors**: Full color (CMYK+)
- **MOQ**: 100+ pcs
- **Setup Cost**: $50-100
- **Print Cost**: $0.20-0.40 per bag
- **Lead Time**: 5-7 days
- **Artwork**: Any digital format

**Pros**:
- Lowest MOQ for full color
- No setup fees for plates/screens
- Fastest turnaround
- Photographic quality

**Cons**:
- Highest per-unit cost
- Limited fabric compatibility
- Less durable (20-30 washes)
- Color consistency challenges

**Best For**: Small batches, prototypes, personalized bags, urgent orders

### Color Matching

#### Pantone Color System

**For Screen Printing**:
- Specify Pantone Solid Coated (e.g., "Pantone 185 C")
- Supplier matches ink to Pantone standard
- Tolerance: ΔE <3 (industry standard)

**For Full-Color Printing**:
- Provide CMYK values
- Request color proof before production
- Physical color swatch preferred

#### Color Matching Process

```
Step 1: Buyer provides Pantone code or physical swatch
Step 2: Supplier creates ink sample
Step 3: Buyer approves or requests adjustment
Step 4: Production uses approved formula
Step 5: QC checks color consistency during production
```

**Color Matching Tips**:
✅ Provide physical swatches when possible  
✅ Approve color proof under standard lighting (D65)  
✅ Specify acceptable tolerance (ΔE <2 for critical colors)  
✅ Consider fabric color impact on print (white vs. colored bags)  
❌ Don't rely on screen colors (monitor variation)  
❌ Don't skip color approval step

## Chapter 5: Artwork Preparation Guide

### File Format Requirements

| Format | Best For | Resolution | Color Mode |
|--------|----------|------------|------------|
| **AI (Adobe Illustrator)** | Vector logos, text | N/A (vector) | CMYK or Spot |
| **EPS** | Vector graphics | N/A (vector) | CMYK or Spot |
| **PDF** | Mixed content | 300+ DPI | CMYK |
| **PSD (Photoshop)** | Photos, complex graphics | 300+ DPI | CMYK |
| **TIFF** | High-quality images | 300+ DPI | CMYK |
| **JPG** | Photos (compressed) | 300+ DPI | CMYK |

**Recommended**: AI or EPS for logos, PDF for complete artwork

### Artwork Specifications

#### Screen Printing

```
✓ Vector format (AI, EPS, PDF)
✓ Convert text to outlines/paths
✓ Separate colors (one layer per color)
✓ Specify Pantone colors
✓ Minimum line width: 0.5pt
✓ Minimum text size: 6pt
✓ Include 3mm bleed
```

#### Heat Transfer / Full Color

```
✓ High-resolution raster (300+ DPI)
✓ CMYK color mode
✓ Actual size (1:1 scale)
✓ Include 3-5mm bleed
✓ Embed fonts or convert to outlines
✓ Flatten layers (or provide layered version)
```

### Common Artwork Mistakes

❌ **Low Resolution**: Images below 300 DPI appear pixelated  
❌ **RGB Color Mode**: Prints in CMYK, colors shift  
❌ **Missing Bleed**: White edges on full-bleed prints  
❌ **Thin Lines**: Lines below 0.5pt may not print  
❌ **Small Text**: Text below 6pt unreadable  
❌ **Unconverted Fonts**: Font substitution issues  
❌ **No Color Specification**: Unclear color expectations

## Chapter 7: Quality Control for Custom Orders

### Custom Order QC Checkpoints

#### Pre-Production

- [ ] Raw material inspection (GSM, color, quality)
- [ ] Printing plate/screen verification
- [ ] Color formula approval
- [ ] Cutting die inspection

- [ ] In-process quality checks (hourly)

- [ ] Seam integrity verification
- [ ] Dimensional accuracy checks

- [ ] AQL 2.5/4.0 inspection

## Chapter 8: Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: What's the minimum order quantity for custom non woven bags?

**MOQ varies by customization level**:
- Stock bags with custom print: 500 pcs
- Custom size (standard handles): 1,000 pcs
- Custom handles + size: 2,000-3,000 pcs
- Full OEM (new design): 5,000+ pcs

### Q2: How long does it take to produce custom non woven bags?

**Total Timeline**: 4-7 weeks
- Design & approval: 1-2 weeks
- Sampling: 1-2 weeks
- Production: 2-3 weeks
- Shipping: 1-4 weeks (depending on method)

**Rush orders**: Possible in 2-3 weeks with expedited fees (+30-50%)

### Q3: Can I order samples before committing to a full order?

**Yes**, samples are recommended:
- Stock samples: Often free or $10-30
- Custom samples: $50-200 (may be refunded with bulk order)
- Sample lead time: 5-14 days depending on customization

### Q4: What file format do you need for my logo?

**Best formats**: AI, EPS, or PDF (vector)
**Acceptable**: High-res PNG, JPG (300+ DPI)
**Color mode**: CMYK for full color, Pantone for spot colors

### Q5: Can you match my brand colors exactly?

**Screen printing**: Pantone color matching (ΔE <3 tolerance)  
**Full color printing**: CMYK color matching (proof provided)

Exact match depends on:
- Fabric color (white vs. colored bags)
- Printing method limitations
- Lighting conditions

### Q6: Do I own the design after customization?

**OEM (your design)**: You retain full IP ownership  
**ODM (supplier design)**: Supplier typically retains design rights  
**Hybrid**: Negotiate IP terms in contract

Always clarify IP ownership before production.

### Q7: What if I'm not satisfied with the samples?

**Revision Process**:
1. Provide detailed feedback on issues
2. Supplier creates revised sample
3. Repeat until approved (typically 1-2 revisions included)
4. If still unsatisfied, can request refund (minus sample cost)

**Tip**: Be specific about requirements upfront to minimize revisions.

### Q8: Can I change my design after production starts?

**Changes before production**: Usually no cost (if within timeline)  
**Changes during production**: May incur costs for:
- Material already purchased
- Printing plates/screens already made
- Production line changes
- Delay penalties

**Recommendation**: Finalize all details before production begins.

### Q9: How do I ensure print quality matches my expectations?

**Best Practices**:
1. Provide high-quality vector artwork
2. Specify Pantone colors explicitly
3. Approve physical color proof
4. Request pre-production sample with actual printing
5. Include print quality specifications in contract
6. Conduct during-production inspection

### Q10: What's the most cost-effective customization option?

**Budget-Friendly Approach**:
- Use standard bag sizes (no tooling)
- Choose D-cut or U-cut handles
- Limit printing to 1-2 colors (screen printing)
- Order 1,000+ pcs for volume discount
- Use stock bag colors (white, black, natural)

**Example**: 1,000 pcs, standard M size, D-cut, 1-color print: ~$0.35-0.45 per bag
