## Chapter 1: Logo Printing Methods Compared

### Complete Printing Method Comparison

| Method | Best For | MOQ | Setup Cost | Unit Cost | Durability | Lead Time |
|--------|----------|-----|------------|-----------|------------|-----------|
| **Screen Printing** | Simple logos, 1-4 colors | 500+ | $50-150/color | $0.05-0.15 | 50+ washes | 7-10 days |
| **Heat Transfer** | Detailed logos, photos | 500+ | $100-300 | $0.10-0.25 | 30-40 washes | 10-14 days |
| **Laminated Printing** | Premium full-color | 1,000+ | $300-800 | $0.15-0.35 | 50+ washes | 14-20 days |
| **Digital Printing** | Small runs, complex | 100+ | $50-100 | $0.20-0.40 | 20-30 washes | 5-7 days |
| **Foil Stamping** | Metallic effects | 1,000+ | $200-500 | $0.15-0.30 | 40+ washes | 14-20 days |
| **Embossing** | Textured logo | 2,000+ | $300-600 | $0.10-0.25 | Permanent | 14-20 days |

### Screen Printing (Most Popular)

**Process**: Ink is pushed through a fine mesh screen onto the bag surface. Each color requires a separate screen.

**Specifications**:
- **Colors**: 1-4 spot colors (Pantone)
- **Logo Size**: Minimum 2cm width for readability
- **Line Width**: Minimum 0.5pt (0.17mm)
- **Text Size**: Minimum 6pt
- **Print Area**: Up to 40×40cm (depending on bag size)

**Pros**:
✅ Cost-effective for simple logos  
✅ Vibrant, opaque colors  
✅ Excellent durability (50+ washes)  
✅ Fast turnaround  
✅ Works on colored bags  

**Cons**:
❌ Not suitable for gradients or photos  
❌ Each color adds setup cost  
❌ Color limitations (spot colors only)  
❌ Setup cost makes small runs expensive  

**Best For**:
- Company logos with 1-3 colors
- Text-based branding
- Simple icons and symbols
- Orders of 500-10,000+ pcs

**Pricing Example** (500 pcs, 2-color logo):
- Setup: $100 × 2 colors = $200
- Printing: $0.08 × 500 = $40
- Total printing cost: $240 ($0.48 per bag)

### Heat Transfer (Full Color)

**Process**: Design is printed on special transfer film, then heat-pressed onto the bag surface.

**Specifications**:
- **Colors**: Full color (CMYK + gradients)
- **Resolution**: 300+ DPI artwork required
- **Logo Size**: Minimum 3cm width for detail clarity
- **Print Area**: Up to 35×35cm

**Pros**:
✅ Full-color capability  
✅ Photographic quality  
✅ No color limitations  
✅ Good for complex designs  
✅ Moderate MOQ  

**Cons**:
❌ Higher cost than screen printing  
❌ Slightly less durable (30-40 washes)  
❌ Transfer film creates waste  
❌ Color variation possible  

**Best For**:
- Detailed logos with gradients
- Photographic elements
- Artwork with many colors
- Medium orders (500-5,000 pcs)

**Pricing Example** (500 pcs, full color):
- Setup: $150
- Printing: $0.18 × 500 = $90
- Total printing cost: $240 ($0.48 per bag)

### Laminated Printing (Premium)

**Process**: PP film is printed with your design, then laminated onto the non woven fabric before bag construction.

**Specifications**:
- **Colors**: Up to 9 colors (gravure printing)
- **Resolution**: Vector or 300+ DPI
- **Logo Size**: Any size (excellent detail)
- **Print Area**: Full surface available

**Pros**:
✅ Premium glossy finish  
✅ Excellent color reproduction  
✅ Waterproof surface  
✅ Most durable print option  
✅ Full-color photography capable  

**Cons**:
❌ Highest setup cost (cylinder fees)  
❌ Higher MOQ (1,000+ pcs)  
❌ Longer lead time  
❌ Not recyclable (mixed materials)  

**Best For**:
- Premium brand positioning
- Full-color photography
- Large orders (1,000-50,000+ pcs)
- Luxury retail applications

**Pricing Example** (1,000 pcs, full color):
- Setup (cylinder): $500
- Printing: $0.25 × 1,000 = $250
- Lamination: $0.12 × 1,000 = $120
- Total printing cost: $870 ($0.87 per bag)

### Digital Printing (Small Runs)

**Process**: Inkjet printing directly onto the non woven fabric surface.

**Specifications**:
- **Colors**: Full color (CMYK+)
- **Resolution**: 300+ DPI
- **Logo Size**: Any size
- **Print Area**: Limited by printer bed size

**Pros**:
✅ Lowest MOQ (100+ pcs)  
✅ No plate/screen setup  
✅ Fastest turnaround  
✅ Photographic quality  
✅ Cost-effective for small runs  

**Cons**:
❌ Highest per-unit cost at volume  
❌ Limited fabric compatibility  
❌ Less durable (20-30 washes)  
❌ Color consistency challenges  

**Best For**:
- Prototypes and samples
- Small promotional runs (100-500 pcs)
- Personalized/variable data printing
- Urgent orders

### Foil Stamping (Metallic Effects)

**Process**: Metallic foil is transferred to the bag surface using heat and pressure with a metal die.

**Specifications**:
- **Foil Colors**: Gold, silver, holographic, custom colors
- **Logo Size**: Minimum 1cm details
- **Foil Area**: Up to 30×30cm

**Pros**:
✅ Eye-catching metallic finish  
✅ Premium luxury appearance  
✅ Good durability  
✅ Unique brand differentiation  

**Cons**:
❌ Higher cost  
❌ Limited to metallic effects  
❌ Requires metal die (setup cost)  
❌ Not suitable for large solid areas  

**Best For**:
- Luxury brand logos
- Accent elements on logos
- Premium gift bags
- Special edition runs

### Embossing (Textured Logo)

**Process**: Logo is pressed into the fabric using matching male/female dies, creating a raised or recessed effect.

**Specifications**:
- **Depth**: 0.5-1.5mm embossing depth
- **Logo Size**: Minimum 2cm width
- **Detail Level**: Simple designs work best

**Pros**:
✅ Tactile, premium feel  
✅ No ink (eco-friendly)  
✅ Permanent (won't fade)  
✅ Subtle elegance  

**Cons**:
❌ Limited to simple designs  
❌ Higher setup cost (die creation)  
❌ Higher MOQ (2,000+ pcs)  
❌ Less visible than printed logos  

**Best For**:
- Minimalist brand logos
- Luxury applications
- Combined with other printing methods
- Premium corporate gifts

## Chapter 2: Logo Design Best Practices

### Logo Size Guidelines

| Bag Size | Recommended Logo Width | Maximum Print Area |
|----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Small (25×30cm)** | 8-12cm | 18×20cm |
| **Medium (30×35cm)** | 10-15cm | 22×25cm |
| **Large (40×45cm)** | 15-20cm | 30×35cm |
| **XL (50×50cm)** | 20-25cm | 40×40cm |

**Rule of Thumb**: Logo should be 1/3 to 1/2 of bag width for optimal visibility.

### Logo Placement Options

#### 1. Center Front (Most Common)

**Position**: Centered horizontally, 5-10cm from top edge

**Best For**:
- Primary branding
- Maximum visibility
- Most applications

**Pros**:
✅ Most visible when bag is carried  
✅ Standard, expected placement  
✅ Works for all logo sizes  

**Cons**:
❌ May be partially covered by contents  
❌ Handle may cross logo area  

---

#### 2. Center Back

**Position**: Centered on back panel

**Best For**:
- Secondary branding
- Additional messaging (website, slogan)
- Dual-sided branding

**Pros**:
✅ Additional brand exposure  
✅ Can include more information  
✅ Less visual clutter on front  

**Cons**:
❌ Less visible than front  
❌ Additional printing cost  

---

#### 3. Bottom

**Position**: Centered near bottom edge

**Best For**:
- Minimalist branding
- Premium/subtle look
- When bag is often stacked

**Pros**:
✅ Subtle, sophisticated  
✅ Doesn't compete with bag contents  
✅ Unique positioning  

**Cons**:
❌ Less visible  
❌ May be obscured when bag is full

#### 4. Side Gusset

**Position**: Vertical on side panel

**Best For**:
- Tall, narrow logos
- Unique brand positioning
- Additional visibility angle

**Pros**:
✅ Visible from side angle  
✅ Unique look  
✅ Doesn't interfere with front design  

**Cons**:
❌ Limited print area  
❌ Additional setup cost  
❌ Less common (may confuse some users)

#### 5. All-Over Print

**Position**: Entire bag surface covered

**Best For**:
- Bold brand statements
- Pattern-based branding
- High-impact promotions

**Pros**:
✅ Maximum brand visibility  
✅ Eye-catching design  
✅ Premium appearance  

**Cons**:
❌ Highest cost (+$0.30-0.60 per bag)

### Color Selection Guidelines

#### Contrast is Key

| Bag Color | Recommended Logo Colors | Avoid |
|-----------|------------------------|-------|
| **White/Natural** | Any dark color, vibrant colors | White, light pastels |
| **Black** | White, metallic, bright colors | Dark blue, dark green |
| **Navy** | White, silver, gold, yellow | Black, dark purple |
| **Red** | White, gold, silver | Black, dark colors |
| **Green** | White, gold, cream | Dark blue, black |
| **Blue** | White, silver, yellow | Black, navy |

**Contrast Ratio**: Aim for minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for readability.

#### Pantone Color Matching

**For Screen Printing**:
- Specify Pantone Solid Coated (e.g., "Pantone 185 C")
- Supplier matches ink to Pantone standard
- Tolerance: ΔE <3 (industry standard)

**Pantone Tips**:
✅ Use physical Pantone swatch book for accuracy  
✅ Specify Pantone C (Coated) for non woven  
✅ Provide Pantone code in artwork notes  
✅ Approve physical color proof before production  
❌ Don't rely on screen colors (monitor variation)  
❌ Don't use RGB colors (print uses CMYK/Pantone)

## Chapter 3: Artwork Preparation

### File Format Requirements

| Format | Best For | Resolution | Color Mode |
|--------|----------|------------|------------|
| **AI (Illustrator)** | Vector logos | N/A (vector) | CMYK or Spot |
| **EPS** | Vector graphics | N/A (vector) | CMYK or Spot |
| **PDF** | Complete artwork | 300+ DPI | CMYK |
| **PSD** | Complex graphics | 300+ DPI | CMYK |
| **TIFF** | High-quality images | 300+ DPI | CMYK |
| **JPG** | Photos (compressed) | 300+ DPI | CMYK |

**Recommended**: AI or EPS (vector formats scale without quality loss)

### Artwork Checklist for Logo Printing

#### Screen Printing

```
✓ Vector format (AI, EPS, PDF)
✓ Convert text to outlines/paths
✓ Separate colors (one layer per color)
✓ Specify Pantone colors
✓ Minimum line width: 0.5pt
✓ Minimum text size: 6pt
✓ Include 3mm bleed
✓ Artwork at actual size (1:1 scale)
```

#### Heat Transfer / Full Color

```
✓ High-resolution raster (300+ DPI)
✓ CMYK color mode
✓ Actual size (1:1 scale)
✓ Include 3-5mm bleed
✓ Embed fonts or convert to outlines
✓ Flatten layers (or provide layered version)
✓ Proof colors before submission
```

### Common Artwork Mistakes

❌ **Low Resolution**: Images below 300 DPI appear pixelated when printed  
❌ **RGB Color Mode**: Prints in CMYK, colors will shift  
❌ **Missing Bleed**: White edges on full-bleed prints  
❌ **Thin Lines**: Lines below 0.5pt may not print clearly  
❌ **Small Text**: Text below 6pt becomes unreadable  
❌ **Unconverted Fonts**: Font substitution issues  
❌ **No Color Specification**: Unclear color expectations  
❌ **Wrong File Format**: Word, PowerPoint files not suitable

## Chapter 5: Step-by-Step Ordering Process

### Complete Ordering Timeline

```
Week 1: Design & Quote
├── Day 1-2: Submit logo artwork, specify requirements
├── Day 3-4: Supplier provides quote and mockup
├── Day 5-7: Review, revise, approve quote

Week 2: Sample & Approval
├── Day 8-10: Supplier creates printed sample
├── Day 11-14: Sample shipped to you
├── Day 15-17: Evaluate sample, approve or request revision

Week 3-4: Production
├── Day 18-20: Deposit payment, production starts
├── Day 21-28: Manufacturing and printing
├── Day 29-30: Quality inspection

Week 5: Shipping
├── Day 31-33: Packaging, documentation
├── Day 34-40: Freight transport
└── Day 41+: Delivery to you
```

### Step 1: Submit Requirements

**Information to Provide**:

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Bag Specifications** | Size, GSM, handle type, bag color |
| **Logo File** | AI, EPS, or PDF (vector preferred) |
| **Logo Placement** | Front center, back, bottom, etc. |
| **Logo Size** | Width in cm or inches |
| **Printing Method** | Screen, heat transfer, laminated, etc. |
| **Pantone Colors** | If screen printing |
| **Quantity** | Estimated order size |
| **Timeline** | Required delivery date |
| **Destination** | Shipping address for freight quote |

### Step 2: Review Quote & Mockup

**Supplier Should Provide**:
- Detailed price breakdown (bag, printing, setup, shipping)
- Digital mockup showing logo on bag
- Production timeline
- Payment terms
- Sample availability and cost

**Review Checklist**:
- [ ] All specifications correctly understood
- [ ] Pricing matches expectations
- [ ] Timeline works for your needs
- [ ] Payment terms acceptable
- [ ] Mockup accurately represents your logo

### Step 3: Approve Sample

**Sample Options**:
- **Blank sample**: Check bag quality only ($20-50)
- **Printed sample**: Check logo printing ($50-150)
- **Pre-production sample**: Final approval ($100-300)

**Sample Evaluation**:
- [ ] Logo size matches specification
- [ ] Colors match Pantone/approved design
- [ ] Logo placement is correct
- [ ] Print quality is acceptable (no smudging, bleeding)
- [ ] Overall bag quality meets expectations

### Step 4: Production & Payment

**Before Production**:
- [ ] Sign sample approval form
- [ ] Pay deposit (typically 30-50%)
- [ ] Confirm production start date
- [ ] Confirm expected completion date

**During Production**:
- Request production photos
- Consider during-production inspection for large orders
- Stay available for any questions

### Step 5: Final Inspection & Shipping

**Pre-Shipment**:
- [ ] Receive pre-shipment inspection report
- [ ] Approve or request rework
- [ ] Pay balance payment
- [ ] Confirm shipping method and address

**After Shipment**:

## Chapter 6: Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: What's the minimum order for custom logo non woven bags?

**MOQ depends on printing method**:
- Digital printing: 100 pcs (lowest MOQ)
- Screen printing: 500 pcs (most common)
- Heat transfer: 500 pcs
- Laminated printing: 1,000 pcs
- Foil stamping/embossing: 1,000-2,000 pcs

### Q2: How much does it cost to add my logo?

**Cost varies by method and quantity**:

| Method | 500 pcs | 1,000 pcs | 5,000 pcs |
|--------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Screen (1-color) | $0.10/bag | $0.07/bag | $0.05/bag |
| Screen (2-color) | $0.18/bag | $0.12/bag | $0.08/bag |
| Heat Transfer | $0.20/bag | $0.15/bag | $0.10/bag |
| Laminated | $0.35/bag | $0.28/bag | $0.20/bag |
| Digital | $0.35/bag | $0.30/bag | $0.25/bag |

*Plus one-time setup fees ($50-800 depending on method)*

### Q3: Can you match my brand colors exactly?

**Screen printing**: Pantone color matching with ΔE <3 tolerance (industry standard)  
**Full color printing**: CMYK matching with physical proof approval

**For exact match**:
- Provide Pantone code
- Approve physical color swatch
- Review printed sample under standard lighting

### Q4: What file format do you need for my logo?

**Best**: AI, EPS, or PDF (vector formats)  
**Acceptable**: High-res PNG, JPG (300+ DPI, actual size)  
**Not suitable**: Word, PowerPoint, low-res web images

### Q5: How large should my logo be on the bag?

**General guideline**: Logo width should be 1/3 to 1/2 of bag width

| Bag Size | Recommended Logo Width |
|----------|----------------------|
| Small (25cm) | 8-12cm |
| Medium (30cm) | 10-15cm |
| Large (40cm) | 15-20cm |
| XL (50cm) | 20-25cm |

### Q6: Can I print on both sides of the bag?

**Yes**, double-sided printing is available:
- Adds 50-100% to printing cost
- Requires separate setup for each side
- Common for: front logo + back website/contact info

### Q7: How durable is the logo printing?

**Durability by method**:
- Screen printing: 50+ washes (most durable)
- Laminated printing: 50+ washes
- Foil stamping: 40+ washes
- Heat transfer: 30-40 washes
- Embossing: Permanent (no ink to fade)
- Digital printing: 20-30 washes

### Q8: Can I change my logo after placing the order?

**Before production**: Usually yes, may incur revision fee  
**During production**: May incur costs for materials already used  
**After production**: Not possible; new order required

**Recommendation**: Finalize logo before production starts.

### Q9: Do I own the printing plates/screens after production?

**Typically no**, screens/plates remain supplier property.  
**Exception**: Can negotiate ownership for large orders or custom dies (foil/embossing).

### Q10: What if the printed logo doesn't match my approval?

**Resolution process**:
1. Document the issue with photos
2. Notify supplier within 7 days of receipt
3. Provide approved sample for comparison
4. Negotiate: reprint, discount, or credit
5. For critical mismatches: full replacement

**Prevention**: Approve physical color proof and printed sample before production.
