### 6.2 Contract vs. Spot Buying

| Approach | Advantages | Disadvantages | Best For |
|----------|------------|---------------|----------|
| **Spot Buying** | Flexibility, market pricing | Price volatility, availability risk | Small orders, urgent needs |
| **Quarterly Contract** | Price stability, priority allocation | May miss price dips | Medium volume users |
| **Annual Contract** | Maximum stability, best terms | Less flexibility, commitment | Large volume users |
| **Formula Pricing** | Market-linked, fair | Complexity, basis risk | Sophisticated buyers |

### 6.3 Hedging Strategies

For large buyers, consider hedging raw material exposure:

| Strategy | Description | Complexity |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **LME Nickel Futures** | Lock in nickel prices | High |
| **Stainless Steel Swaps** | OTC price swaps | High |
| **Mill Formula Contracts** | Price linked to LME nickel | Medium |
| **Inventory Buffer** | Hold 2-3 months inventory | Low |

### 6.4 Supplier Negotiation Tips

## Chapter 7: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: How often do stainless steel prices change?

**A**: Prices can change:
- **Daily**: With nickel price movements
- **Weekly**: Mill price announcements
- **Monthly**: Contract price negotiations

For spot purchases, expect quotes valid for 24-48 hours.

### Q2: What is the best way to track stainless steel prices?

**A**: Options include:
- **LME nickel prices** (free, real-time indicator)
- **Mill price announcements** (monthly)
- **Price reporting agencies** (CRU, Argus, S&P—subscription)
- **Supplier quotes** (most accurate for your specifics)

### Q3: Why are 316 prices more volatile than 304?

**A**: 316 contains molybdenum (2-3%), which has:
- Smaller, less liquid market than nickel
- More concentrated supply (Chile, USA, Peru)
- Higher price volatility ($30-60/kg range historically)

### Q4: How do tariffs affect stainless steel prices?

**A**: Tariffs add directly to landed cost:
- **USA Section 232**: 25% on most countries
- **EU Safeguards**: 25% above quota
- **India**: 10-15% total duty

This makes domestic sourcing more competitive in protected markets.

### Q5: Can I lock in prices for future delivery?

**A**: Yes, options include:
- **Fixed-price contracts** (mill may add premium)
- **Formula pricing** (linked to LME nickel)
- **Hedging** (LME futures, swaps)

Discuss with your mill representative.

### Q6: What is the price difference between 2B and BA finish?

**A**: BA (Bright Annealed) typically costs **$0.30-0.50/kg more** than 2B due to:
- Additional processing steps
- Lower production yields
- Higher quality requirements

### Q7: How much can I save by buying in bulk?

**A**: Volume discounts typically range:
- **5-10 tons**: 5-10% discount
- **10-20 tons**: 10-15% discount
- **20+ tons**: 12-20% discount (contract pricing)
