## Chapter 2: Minimum Order Quantities (MOQ) Explained

### 2.1 MOQ by Supplier Type

| Supplier Type | Typical MOQ | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| **Service Center** | 1 coil (300-500 kg) | May charge premium |
| **Distributor** | 500 kg - 1 ton | Some stock available |
| **Trading Company** | 1-3 tons | Consolidates orders |
| **Small Mill** | 3-5 tons | Limited flexibility |
| **Large Mill** | 5-10 tons | Standard MOQ |
| **Mill Direct (Contract)** | 20-50 tons | Best pricing |

### 2.2 MOQ by Grade

| Grade | Typical MOQ | Reason |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **304/304L** | 3-5 tons | High production volume |
| **316/316L** | 5-10 tons | Lower production volume |
| **201** | 3-5 tons | High production volume |
| **430** | 3-5 tons | Common ferritic grade |
| **310/321** | 5-10 tons | Specialty grades |
| **2205 Duplex** | 10-20 tons | Limited production |
| **904L** | 10-20 tons | Super austenitic, special order |

### 2.3 MOQ Negotiation Strategies

If your order is below MOQ:

**Strategy 1: Combine Grades**
- Order multiple grades to meet total tonnage
- Example: 2 tons 304 + 2 tons 316 = 4 tons total

**Strategy 2: Group with Other Buyers**
- Join buying group or consortium
- Coordinate purchases with other companies

**Strategy 3: Pay Premium for Small Order**
- Accept small order surcharge ($0.10-0.30/kg)
- Still may be cheaper than retail

**Strategy 4: Commit to Future Volume**
- Promise annual volume commitment
- Get approved for lower initial MOQ

**Strategy 5: Use Stock Programs**
- Buy from mill's standard stock
- Limited sizes but lower MOQ

### 2.4 Container Load Optimization

For international orders, optimize container loading:

| Container Type | Max Weight | Typical Load | Best For |
|---------------|------------|--------------|----------|
| **20ft Container** | 27 tons | 22-25 tons | 10-12 coils |
| **40ft Container** | 27 tons | 22-25 tons | Same as 20ft (volume-limited) |
| **40ft HC Container** | 27 tons | 25-27 tons | Maximum weight |

**Loading Tips**:
- Standard coil weight: 2-3 tons each
- Plan coil dimensions to maximize space
- Consider weight distribution for safety

## Chapter 4: Contract Negotiation Strategies

### 4.1 Key Contract Terms to Negotiate

| Term | Buyer Preference | Supplier Preference | Compromise |
|------|-----------------|--------------------|------------|
| **Price** | Fixed for 12 months | Quarterly adjustment | Semi-annual review |
| **Volume** | Flexible ±20% | Fixed commitment | Fixed with rollover |
| **Payment** | Net 60 days | 30% advance, 70% before ship | LC at sight or Net 30 |
| **Lead Time** | 15 days | 30 days | 20-25 days |
| **Quality** | Zero defects | Industry standard | AQL 2.5 |
| **Claims** | 90 days to file | 30 days to file | 60 days |
| **Force Majeure** | Narrow definition | Broad definition | Standard ICC terms |

### 4.2 Volume Commitment Strategies

**Annual Volume Contract**:
```
Commitment: 100 tons/year (304 equivalent)
Pricing: Base + alloy surcharge, reviewed quarterly
Discount: 18% off list price
Payment: Net 30 days
Delivery: As needed, 20 days lead time
Minimum per Order: 5 tons
Shortfall Penalty: 5% of unfulfilled commitment
```

**Take-or-Pay Contract**:
```
Commitment: Take minimum 80 tons OR pay for 80 tons
Pricing: Preferential rates
Benefit: Supplier guarantees capacity
Risk: Buyer pays even if not needed
```

**Flexible Volume Contract**:
```
Commitment: 80-120 tons/year (±20% flexibility)
Pricing: Tiered based on actual volume
Benefit: Buyer flexibility
Risk: Lower discount than fixed contract
```

### 4.3 Price Protection Mechanisms

**Fixed Price Contract**:
- Price locked for contract period
- Best for stable markets
- Risk: Supplier may add premium for risk

**Formula Pricing**:
```
Price = Base Price + Alloy Surcharge

Alloy Surcharge = (Ni Price × 0.08) + (Cr Price × 0.18) + (Mo Price × 0.025)

Reviewed monthly based on LME prices
```

**Price Cap/Floor**:
- Maximum price buyer pays
- Minimum price supplier receives
- Shares risk between parties

### 4.5 Contract Red Flags

| Clause | Risk | Action |
|--------|------|--------|
| **100% advance payment** | Financial risk | Negotiate LC or progress payment |
| **No quality guarantees** | Quality risk | Require MTC and inspection |
| **Unlimited price adjustment** | Cost risk | Cap adjustments at X% |
| **No penalty for late delivery** | Delivery risk | Add liquidated damages |
| **One-sided force majeure** | Performance risk | Make mutual |
| **No termination clause** | Exit risk | Add termination for cause |

### 5.3 Inventory Management for Wholesale Buyers

**Economic Order Quantity (EOQ)**:

```
EOQ = √(2 × Annual Demand × Order Cost / Holding Cost)

Example:
Annual Demand = 100,000 kg
Order Cost = $500 per order
Holding Cost = $0.50/kg/year

EOQ = √(2 × 100,000 × 500 / 0.50) = 14,142 kg

Optimal: Order ~14 tons, 7 times per year
```

**Safety Stock Calculation**:

```
Safety Stock = (Max Daily Usage × Max Lead Time) - (Avg Daily Usage × Avg Lead Time)

Example:
Max Daily Usage = 500 kg
Avg Daily Usage = 300 kg
Max Lead Time = 60 days
Avg Lead Time = 40 days

Safety Stock = (500 × 60) - (300 × 40) = 18,000 kg
```

**Reorder Point**:

```
Reorder Point = (Avg Daily Usage × Avg Lead Time) + Safety Stock

Reorder Point = (300 × 40) + 18,000 = 30,000 kg

Order when inventory drops to 30 tons
```

## Chapter 6: Risk Management for Bulk Purchases

### 6.1 Key Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|--------|--------------------|
| **Quality Failure** | High | Third-party inspection, MTC verification |
| **Delivery Delay** | Medium-High | Safety stock, penalty clauses |
| **Price Volatility** | Medium | Fixed-price contracts, hedging |
| **Supplier Insolvency** | High | Financial checks, multiple suppliers |
| **Currency Fluctuation** | Medium | Currency hedging, local currency pricing |
| **Trade Policy Changes** | High | Diversify sourcing regions |
| **Logistics Disruption** | Medium | Multiple shipping options |

### 6.2 Quality Assurance for Wholesale Orders

**Pre-Production**:
- [ ] Raw material verification
- [ ] Production schedule confirmation
- [ ] Quality plan approval

**During Production**:
- [ ] In-process inspection
- [ ] Dimensional checks
- [ ] Surface quality verification

**Pre-Shipment**:
- [ ] Final inspection (third-party recommended)
- [ ] MTC verification
- [ ] Packaging inspection
- [ ] Loading supervision

**Post-Delivery**:
- [ ] Incoming inspection
- [ ] Test samples if needed
- [ ] File claims promptly if issues

### 6.3 Payment Risk Management

| Payment Method | Buyer Risk | Supplier Risk | Recommendation |
|---------------|------------|---------------|----------------|
| **100% Advance** | High | Low | Avoid |
| **30/70 T/T** | Medium | Low | Common for new suppliers |
| **LC at Sight** | Low | Low | Recommended for international |
| **LC 60/90 Days** | Low | Medium | Good for established suppliers |
| **Open Account** | Very Low | High | For long-term relationships |

### 6.4 Contract Exit Strategies

Include these clauses for flexibility:

**Termination for Cause**:
```
Either party may terminate if:
- Material breach not cured within 30 days
- Insolvency or bankruptcy
- Force majeure >90 days
```

**Termination for Convenience**:
```
Buyer may terminate with 60 days notice, subject to:
- Payment for goods in production
- Termination fee (if applicable)
```

**Gradual Phase-Out**:
```
Upon termination:
- Complete outstanding orders
- 90-day supply commitment
- Knowledge transfer if needed
```

## Chapter 7: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: What is the minimum order for wholesale pricing?

**A**: Typically:
- **Service centers**: 500 kg - 1 ton
- **Distributors**: 1-3 tons
- **Mill direct**: 5-10 tons
- **Contract pricing**: 20+ tons/year

### Q2: How much can I save buying wholesale?

**A**: Typical savings:
- **Small wholesale (1-5 tons)**: 5-10% vs. retail
- **Large wholesale (5-20 tons)**: 10-20% vs. retail
- **Mill direct contract (20+ tons)**: 15-30% vs. retail

### Q3: What payment terms are standard for wholesale?

**A**: 
- **New suppliers**: 30% deposit, 70% before shipment
- **Established suppliers**: LC at sight
- **Long-term partners**: Net 30-60 days

### Q4: Can I mix grades in a wholesale order?

**A**: Yes, most suppliers allow mixing:
- Same thickness range: Usually no surcharge
- Different thickness: May incur changeover cost
- Different grades: Priced separately

### Q5: What is the lead time for wholesale orders?

**A**: 
- **Stock items**: 3-7 days
- **Mill production (standard)**: 15-25 days
- **Custom specifications**: 25-40 days

### Q6: Should I use a sourcing agent for wholesale purchases?

**A**: Consider an agent if:
- You're new to importing
- Language barrier exists
- You need local market knowledge
- You want inspection coordination

**Cost**: Typically 3-5% of order value

### Q7: How do I handle quality disputes on wholesale orders?

**A**: 
1. Document issues with photos/videos
2. Notify supplier within contract period (typically 60 days)
3. Provide independent test reports
4. Negotiate resolution (credit, replacement, discount)
5. Escalate to arbitration if needed

### Q8: Is it better to have one or multiple wholesale suppliers?

**A**: Best practice is **2-3 approved suppliers**:
- **Primary**: 60-70% of volume (best pricing)
- **Secondary**: 20-30% of volume (backup)
- **Tertiary**: 10% of volume (emergency)

This balances pricing leverage with supply security.
