## Price Calculator

### Calculate Coil Price

Use this calculator to estimate the cost of your stainless steel coil order:

```
Formula:
Weight (kg) = Thickness (mm) × Width (mm) × Length (m) × Density / 1000
Price = Weight × Price per kg

Density of stainless steel ≈ 8.0 g/cm³
```

**Example Calculation**:

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Thickness | 2.0 mm |
| Width | 1219 mm |
| Length | 100 m |
| Grade | 304 2B |
| Price/kg | $3.20 |

```
Weight = 2.0 × 1219 × 100 × 8.0 / 1000 = 1,950 kg
Price = 1,950 × $3.20 = $6,240
```

## Understanding Price Per KG

### Why Price Per KG?

Price per kilogram is the standard pricing method for stainless steel because:

1. **Accurate comparison**: Eliminates confusion from different sizing methods
2. **Weight-based manufacturing**: Mills produce and sell by weight
3. **Raw material linkage**: Nickel, chromium priced per weight unit
4. **International standard**: Universal across global markets

### Alternative Pricing Methods

| Method | When Used | Conversion |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Price per KG** | Most common | Baseline |
| **Price per Ton** | Large volume orders | 1 ton = 1,000 kg |
| **Price per LB** | USA market | 1 kg = 2.205 lb |
| **Price per Foot** | Slit coils/strip | Requires weight/foot calculation |
| **Price per Sheet** | Cut-to-size | Requires weight/sheet calculation |

### Price Per KG vs Price Per LB

| Region | Standard Unit | Conversion |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| **USA** | USD/lb | USD/kg ÷ 2.205 |
| **Europe** | EUR/kg | Varies by exchange rate |
| **Asia** | USD/kg | Standard |
| **India** | INR/kg | Convert to USD for comparison |

**Quick Conversion**:
- USD/lb × 2.205 = USD/kg
- USD/kg ÷ 2.205 = USD/lb

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: What is the current price of 304 stainless steel per kg?

**A**: As of March 31, 2026, 304 2B stainless steel coil is priced at **$2.80-3.50/kg** ($1.27-1.59/lb), depending on thickness, width, and order volume.

### Q2: What is the current price of 316 stainless steel per kg?

**A**: As of March 31, 2026, 316 2B stainless steel coil is priced at **$3.80-4.80/kg** ($1.72-2.18/lb), with a premium of $1.00-1.30/kg over 304.

### Q3: How often do prices change?

**A**: Prices can change:
- **Daily**: With nickel price movements
- **Weekly**: Mill surcharge announcements
- **Monthly**: Contract price negotiations

Spot quotes are typically valid for 24-48 hours.

### Q4: Why is 316 more expensive than 304?

**A**: 316 contains 2-3% molybdenum, which:
- Costs ~$48/kg (vs. nickel at ~$18.50/kg)
- Adds ~$1.00-1.30/kg to the final price
- Provides superior corrosion resistance

### Q5: What is the cheapest stainless steel grade?

**A**: **201 stainless steel** is typically the cheapest austenitic grade at $2.20-2.80/kg. **430 ferritic** (no nickel) is also cost-effective at $2.40-3.00/kg for certain applications.

### Q6: Does order quantity affect price per kg?

**A**: Yes, volume discounts apply:
- **Full coil** (500-2000 kg): Best pricing
- **Partial coil**: +10-20% premium
- **Small quantities** (<100 kg): +30-50% premium

### Q7: Are prices different for different finishes?

**A**: Yes, finish affects price:
- **2B**: Standard pricing
- **BA**: +$0.30-0.50/kg
- **No. 4**: +$0.40-0.60/kg
- **No. 8 Mirror**: +$0.80-1.20/kg

### Q8: How do I calculate the weight of a coil?

**A**: Use this formula:

```
Weight (kg) = Thickness (mm) × Width (mm) × Length (m) × 8.0 / 1000

Example: 2mm × 1219mm × 100m coil
Weight = 2 × 1219 × 100 × 8.0 / 1000 = 1,950 kg
```
